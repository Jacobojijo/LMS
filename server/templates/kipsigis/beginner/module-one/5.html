1.4 Numbers and Numbering

1.4.1 Introduction

Cardinal numbers are essential in the Kipsigis language as they provide a means of counting and quantifying. Rooted in a decimal system, they reveal the linguistic and cognitive organization of the Kipsigis people and reflect the cultural importance of structure and precision.

1.4.2 Cardinal Numbers

Basic Cardinal Numbers (1–9)

The numbers from 1 to 9 represent the foundational units of the Kipsigis counting system:

  1. Agenge 1
  2. Oeng' 2
  3. Somok 3
  4. Ang'wan 4
  5. Muut 5
  6. Loo 6
  7. Tisab 7
  8. Sisit 8
  9. Sogol 9
  10. Taman 10

These numbers serve as the building blocks for forming larger quantities.

Construction of Compound Numbers

Kipsigis cardinal numbers beyond 10 demonstrate an additive structure:

Number Kipsigis Construction (Gloss)
11Taman ak agenge10 + 1
12Taman ak oeng'10 + 2
13Taman ak somok10 + 3
14Taman ak ang'wan10 + 4
15Taman ak muut10 + 5
16Taman ak loo10 + 6
17Taman ak tisab10 + 7
18Taman ak sisit10 + 8
19Taman ak sogol10 + 9

In the Kipsigis language, the numbers 10-50 have distinct naming. However, higher multiples i.e from 60 to 90 uses the term "tomonwogik" to denote how many groups of tens followed by the numbers 6 to 9:

  1. Taman 10
  2. Tiptem 20
  3. Sosom 30
  4. Artam 40
  5. Konom 50
  6. Tomonwogik loo 60
  7. Tomonwogik tisab 70
  8. Tomonwogik sisiit 80
  9. Tomwogik sogol 90

Hundreds

In kipsisgis, 'bogol' represent a group of 100. All other multiples of 100 from 100 to 900 uses the prefix bogol to the primary cardinal numbers

  1. Bogol agenge 100
  2. Bogol oeng' 200
  3. Bogol somok 300
  4. Bogol ang'wan 400
  5. Bogol mut 500
  6. Bogol loo 600
  7. Bogol tisab 700
  8. Bogol sisit 800
  9. Bogol sogol 900

Thousands

The term "eliput" (plural: elipusiek), borrowed from the Swahili language 'elfu' is used to indicate a group of 1000 and is prefixed to the primary cardinal numbers

  1. Eliput agenge 1000
  2. Elipusiek oeng' 2000
  3. Elipusiek somok 3000
  4. Elipusiek ang'wan 4000
  5. Elipusiek mut 5000
  6. Elipusiek loo 6000
  7. Elipusiek tisab 7000
  8. Elipusiek sisit 8000
  9. Elipusiek sogol 9000

Construction of Compound Numbers

Numbers beyond 10 in the Kipsigis language demonstrate an additive structure

  • Taman ak agenge 11 (taman [10] + agenge [1]).
  • Taman ak oeng' 12 (taman [10] + oeng[2]).
  • Taman ak somok – 13 (taman [10] + somok[3]).
  • Bogol sogol ak tomonwokik sogol -990
  • Elipusiek sogol ak bogol sogol- 9900
  • Elipusiek sogol ak bogol sogol ak tomonwogik sogol ak sogol- 9999

Similarly, for values like 1001:

  • Eliput agenge ak agenge 1001 (eliput agenge[1000] + agenge [1]).

Usage and Cultural Relevance

Cardinal numbers are indispensable in:

  • Trade and Economy: Measuring goods, assigning prices, and conducting transactions.
  • Timekeeping: Structuring events and agricultural activities (e.g., counting days or weeks).
  • Family and Social Organization: Counting members and allocating responsibilities.

Examples in Sentences

  • Tinye kiptoo tuga muut -kiptoo has five cows
  • Komiin kandoikyok ketiik tomonwogik tisab- our leaders planted seventy trees
  • Tagomii lakwet agenge pii -one child is still outside
  • Icheget ko biik somok- they are three people
  • Kwony agenge ak mureniik oeng' -one woman and two men

Use the countable nouns and try to use the numbers in Kipsigis

  1. How many goats (ng'ororek)? five.
  2. How many girls (tipiik)? nine.
  3. How many friends (choronook)? fifteen.
  4. How many children (lagook)? eleven.
  5. How many houses (koriik)? ten.

1.6.4 Everyday Objects (Domestic & Household items)

Around the Home

The Kipsigis language is deeply rooted in the Kipsigis people's daily life, offering a unique perspective on the culture through its vocabulary. Everyday objects around the home play an essential role in highlighting the connection between language and tradition. They include the following:

The Home as a Cultural Epicenter

Kot (House/Hut) – Central to a Kipsigis homestead, the kot is both a physical shelter and a cultural hub. Traditionally, it's circular with a thatched roof, hosting family gatherings and ceremonies.

Kimonchok (Roof): Symbolizing protection, the kimonchok is traditionally a pillar tree at the centre of the house roof where all grasses meet, though modern homes use iron sheets

Kitog (bed) – Originally made of mud, now often replaced with wooden.

Biut(compound) – Where people relax outside the house.

b. Dining Essentials

kombet (Cup) – Used for chai (tea) signifying hospitality.

Sanit (Plate) – Holds staple foods like kimyet (ugali)and ngwek(vegetables).

Chepungut (Pot) – Essential for cooking meals like panyek (sun-dried meat).

Sotet (guard) – used to brew traditional sour milk (mursik)

c. Furnishings and Comfort

Kok – A special place where elders seat during storytelling and dispute resolution.

Meset (Table) – Symbol of modern dining, replacing traditional communal eating.

Kabat (Cupboard) – Stores utensils and food items.

d. Cleaning and Maintenance

Kitandet(Bed) – Traditionally made of mud, now wooden-framed.

Pirirwet (Broom) – Sweeps compound floors daily, ensuring hygiene.

Ndooit (Pail) – Used for fetching water, vital in homesteads.

e. Personal Belongings

Kweyot/kwoshek (Shoes) – Worn casually around the home.

Rapishek (Money) – Earned through farming and trade.

f. Modern and Technological Advances

ptanganyit (Television) – Connects tradition and modernity through storytelling and news.

Pngalal batai (Radio) – A primary source of news and entertainment.

g. Educational and Practical Tools

Kalamit (Pen) – Essential for education and literacy.

kioit (Mirror) – Used for grooming and cultural dressing.

h. Outdoors and Utility

Rokoet (Rope) – Used for securing livestock and household needs.

Mbar/Kapungui (Farm/Garden) – Produces staple crops like beek (sorghum) and nduma (arrowroots).

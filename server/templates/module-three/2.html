3.1.2 Syntax Functions

The standard Functional Grammar theory works with only two syntactic functions, namely Subj(ect) and Obj(ect). Subj function can be explained in these words:

a constituent with Subj function refers to the entity which is uken as a point of departure for detennining the perspective from which the state of affairs designated by the predication is presented.

Accordingly, we assign Obj function to the entity which is taken as the second point of departure (or vantage point).

The real motivation for the inclusion of Subj and Obj functions in FG is the need to account for active-passive paraphrase relations, especially in the case of languages such as English in which a Constituent with Obj function in an active sentence assumes Subj finction in the corresponding passive construction. For example, in the sentences:

Men rule the world.
Subj         Obj
The world is ruled by men
  Subj

Similarly, terms are called alpha or non-alpha, depending on the SFS they have in a given predication. The unmarked constituent order for complete predications in Dholuo is as follows:6

1 V 2 ... n where V is the verbal predicate which forms the strucnral nucleus of the predication; and 1, 2 ... n are term positions.

Among the term positions, only 1 must in all cases be occupied since the filling of the rest (Of the positions) (2 n) will, in each case, depend on the valency of the verbal predicate in question. This is the kind of constituent order reflected in the following sentences:

The followmg general tendencies are observed in sentences of this kind:

  1. In every complete predication, the alpha term (e.g. Otieno and Jatedo) occupies the preverbal position 1.
  2. The immediately postverbal position 2 is occupied by the next term on the SFH which occurs in the given predication, subject to observation (111).
  3. (Except in the case of some few verbal predicates, e.g. miyo 'to give', which have their peculiar restrictions on the positioning of non-alpha terms, position 2 can be occupied by a term bearing any of the first three non-alpha ftnctions, namely, Go, Rec, and Ben.
  4. (No term bearing a semantic function lower than Ben on the SFH can occupy position 2 if a term bearing any of the first three non-alpha functions Go, Reg, or Ben occurs in the same predication.
  5. Unless they are assigned some special (pragmatic) functions, terms bearing semantic functions lower than Ben on the SFH are normally positioned according to their hierarchical order.

Since we have taken the position that there is no need for Subj assignment in the description of Dholuo, we must accordingly also dispense with the notion of Obj assignment because in FG the latter presupposes the former. In general, then, the notion of 'syntactic function' (as applied in FG) is irrelevant to the description of Dholuo.

In both preverbal and post-verbal positions, constituent order in Dholuo will be accounted for in statements which make reference to semantic functions.

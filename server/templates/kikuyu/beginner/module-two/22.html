2.8.2 Possessive Adjectives

A. Core statement (form & position)

  • Definition: possessive adjectives are separate words that follow the noun they modify and agree in noun class with that noun.
  • Mechanism: the root of the possessive remains constant (e.g., -akwa, -aku, -ake), but the prefix of the possessive adjective (or agreement element) changes to match the noun class.

B. Person paradigm (root forms and example with mũndũ)

Person Root English equivalent Example (with mũndũ)
1st person singular -akwa My mũndũ wakwa → My person
2nd person singular -aku Your mũndũ waku → Your person
3rd person singular -ake His / Her mũndũ wake → His / Her person
1st person plural -itũ Our mũndũ witũ → Our person
2nd person plural -anyu Your (pl.) mũndũ wanyu → Your (pl.) person
3rd person plural -ao Their mũndũ wao → Their person

Note: the root (e.g., -akwa) is presented here as the morphological base; the actual surface possessive form that appears after the noun will display a class-agreeing prefix.

C. Possessive prefixes and noun-class agreement (bulleted examples from the data)

  • Principle: the possessive adjective's prefix is governed by the noun class of the possessed noun; the noun itself does not change.
  • Illustrative examples (as provided):
    • Mũndũ (Person, Class 1) → mũndũ wakwa (my person).
    • Mĩtĩ (Trees, Class 4) → mĩtĩ yakwa (my trees).
    • Nyũmba (House, Class 9) → nyũmba yakwa (my house).
    • Iriũ (Goat, Class 5) → iriũ rĩakwa (my goat).
    • Gĩthaka (Farm, Class 7) → gĩthaka gĩakwa (my farm).

D. Possessives with plural nouns (attested paradigm table)

Possessive form Singular noun + possessive (example) Plural noun + possessive (example)
My mũciĩ wakwa (my home) mĩciĩ yakwa (my homes)
Your (sg) mũciĩ waku (your home) mĩciĩ yaku (your homes)
His/Her mũciĩ wake (his/her home) mĩciĩ yake (his/her homes)

Your (pl) mũciĩ wanyu (your home) mĩciĩ yanyu (your homes)
Their mũciĩ wao (their home) mĩciĩ yao (their homes)

Observe: the possessive prefix shape changes with plural class shape (the table reproduces the exact attested pairings).

2.7.1 Interrogative Pronoun

1. Definition (concise, formal)

Interrogative pronouns are question-words that replace nouns whose identity is unknown.

They introduce information-seeking utterances about manner, reason, quantity, time, person, thing, or place.

In Kikuyu the pronoun is frequently morphologically incorporated into the verb; an independent pronoun may be added for emphasis.

2. Core interrogative pronouns — forms, uses and examples

Kikuyu Gloss (English) Primary use Example
Atĩa? (or Atia?) How? Manner / method Ũgũthiĩ atĩa? — "How will you go?"
Nĩkĩ? Why? Reason / cause Nĩkĩ ũrĩũgama? — "Why are you standing?"
Aigana? How many? Quantity (countable) — prefix varies by noun class Nĩ andũ aigana? — "How many people are there?"
Rĩ? (or Rĩĩ?) When? Time Nĩũgũũka rĩ? — "When will you come?"
Ũũ? (or Nũũ?) Who? Person / people identity Ũũ nĩ mũthamaki? — "Who is the chief?"
Kĩ? (or Gĩkĩ?) What? Things / actions Gĩkĩ nĩ kĩ? — "What is this?"
Kũ? (or Kũũ?) Where? Location Mũciĩ waku nĩ kũ? — "Where is your home?"

3. Common present-tense question patterns (person / number paradigms)

Many present-tense question-forms are verb-centred; the pronoun may be encoded in the verb form:

Form Meaning
Ũgũthiĩ? Are you going?
Arĩĩthire? Is he/she going?
Mũgũthiĩ? Are you (pl.) going?
Tũgũthiĩ? Are we going?
Magũthiĩ? Are they going?

Emphatic variant: add an independent pronoun before the verb for emphasis:

Nĩwe arĩĩthire? — "Is he/she really going?"
Wĩ ũgũthiĩ? — "Are you really going?"

4. Tag questions — confirming or denying statements

Function: seek confirmation or express expectation.

Forms given: kana ti? (or kana gũtũrĩ?) appended to the clause.

Examples (from source):

Nĩwĩĩthiĩ, kana ti? — "You will go, won't you?"
Nĩwe nĩ mũthuri, kana gũtũrĩ? — "He is a man, isn't he?"
Ũkũre, kana ti? — "You are tall, aren't you?"

5. Location-related interrogatives (Where?)

Basic locative interrogative: Kũ? — "Where?"

Example: Mũka waku nĩ kũ? — "Where is your wife?"

Other attested locative forms (as used in examples):

  • E kũ? — "Where is he/she?"
  • Me kũ? — "Where are they?"
  • Ĩtũrũĩkĩra rĩrĩa? — "Where are the keys?" (literal: "the keys are where?")

Emphatic distal locative: the suffix -rĩa may add emphasis / distance ("over there"):

Mũciĩ waku nĩ kũrĩa? — "Is your home over there?"

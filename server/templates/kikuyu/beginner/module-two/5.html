2.1.5 Parts of the Body

I. Scope & analytic frame

  • Domain: lexical inventory and grammatical behaviour of Kikuyu body-part terms as used in description, health discourse, appearance, action, and emotion.
  • Classification principle: external (visible) vs internal (in medical/feeling contexts).
  • Grammatical relevance: countability, possession, subject/object behaviour and pronoun substitution determine agreement and clarity in sentence construction.

IV. Key grammatical distinctions

Countability

  • Countable: hands, fingers, eyes, teeth, toes — can be enumerated (one, two, three…).
    Example (given): I have two hands. → Ndĩ na moko marĩa igĩrĩ.
  • Uncountable / mass: blood, heart, some internal materials — treated as mass or singular in contexts.
    Example (given): Blood flows in the body. → Mũciĩ gwĩkĩra mũtĩ wa mũthĩ.

Possessive requirement

  • Kikuyu commonly specifies whose body part is being discussed (possessive is not optional if reference would be ambiguous).
  • Possessive forms shown in the data:
    • My: gwakwa → Gũoko gwakwa (my hand)
    • Your: gwaku → Gũoko gwaku (your hand)
    • His/her: gwake → Gũoko gwake (his/her hand)
  • Correct vs incorrect usage (as provided):
    • ✅ Correct: "She broke her arm." → Gũoko gwake nĩ kunĩku.
    • ❌ Incorrect: "She broke the arm." — ambiguous in Kikuyu without a possessive.

Subject / object behaviour

  • As subject (provided example): His head is aching. → Mũtwe wake wĩrĩrĩaga.
  • As object (provided example): She hurt her knee. → Ono ahinyĩte rwero rwe.
  • Implication: body-part terms surface in both argument positions and require appropriate possessive or pronominal marking.

Pronoun substitution & referential clarity

Use possessive + noun or personal pronoun to maintain referent clarity in discourse (examples above demonstrate replacement).

VI. Pedagogic & memorability organization

  • Face & head cluster: Mũtwe, Njuĩrĩ, Ũthiũ, Riitho, Iniuru, Kanua, Gũtũ, Igego, Rũrĩmĩ, Kĩaya
  • Upper limb cluster: Kĩande (shoulder), Guoko (arm), Kĩgokora (elbow), Tũnwe twa moko (fingers), Nginya (nails)
  • Torso cluster: Gĩthũri (chest), Ndia (stomach), Mahĩga (hips), Mũgongo (back), Rũgongo (waist)
  • Lower limb cluster: Kũgũrũ (leg), Kĩero (thigh), Ihenya (knee), Kĩongo kĩa Kũgũrũ (ankle), Tũnwe twa magũrũ (toes)
  • Internal / medical cluster: Ngoro, Ũthirigũ, Mahũha, Mara, Hingo, Hingo cia maĩ, Thakame, Mĩkiha, Ihĩndĩ

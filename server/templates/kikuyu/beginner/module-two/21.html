2.8.1 Introduction

Definition (formal):

An adjective in Kikuyu is a lexical item that describes or modifies a noun, supplying additional information about its qualities, characteristics or attributes (e.g., size, colour, shape, state).

Primary functions of adjectives:

  • Modify nouns — always used to describe a noun and give it detail.
  • Provide specific detail — clarify size, colour, shape, condition, moral quality, beauty, etc.
  • Enter into agreement — adjectives must agree with the noun class of the noun they modify (the adjective's prefix changes to match the noun's prefix).

Agreement in practice (illustrative statement from the data):

The adjective for "big" appears in different class forms: mũnene (for a person), nene (for a house), kĩnene (for an item).

Conclusion: the root meaning (big) is constant; the surface prefix reflects noun-class agreement.

Common types of adjectives (categorical list with brief definition):

  • Descriptive adjectives: denote qualities (e.g., nene — big; mwerũ — white; mũthaka — beautiful).
  • Comparative adjectives: expressed by a comparative structure rather than a distinct lexical class (marker given: kũrĩ — "than").
  • Superlative adjectives: expressed periphrastically, not by a single morphological superlative form (example phrase: gũgĩkĩra othe — "surpasses all").
  • Demonstrative adjectives: point to a specific noun and agree in class (e.g., ũyũ — this; ũcio — that).
  • Possessive adjectives: express ownership and agree with the noun class (e.g., wakwa — my; waku — your).
  • Interrogative adjectives: used in questions to modify a noun (e.g., rĩĩ mũndũ — which person).
  • Proper adjectives: derived from proper nouns (e.g., mũndũ wa Kĩkũyũ — a person of the Kikuyu tribe).

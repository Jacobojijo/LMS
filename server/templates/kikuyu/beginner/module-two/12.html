2.4.1 Summary of Nouns (Part 02)

1. Concrete Nouns (Maatu ma Thirikari)

Definition

  • Refer to tangible, physical objects or beings.
  • Perceived through the five senses: sight, touch, hearing, taste, smell.
  • Anchor Kikuyu worldview to everyday lived environment: people, animals, objects, places, and materials.

Categories & Examples

People (Andũ):

Mũthoni (teacher), Mũthuuri (man), Mwana (child)

Animals (Nyamũ):

Ngui (dog), Nyau (cat), Ndovu (elephant)

Places (Mataũ):

Kirima (hill), Gĩtũũra (village), Cũkuru (school)

Things (Ciumia):

Gĩtĩ (chair), Ibuku (book), Ngari (car)

Materials (Ciandũ):

Mũtĩ (wood), Chuma (metal)

Sample Sentences

Nyonire ngui igitengera itũũra.
→ "I saw the dog running in the village."

Nĩrĩkĩtie gĩtĩ agĩtũra athenagia ibuku.
→ "She sat on the chair and read her book."

2. Abstract Nouns (Maatu ma Mũthirĩri)

Definition

  • Refer to intangible concepts not physically touched or seen.
  • Express qualities, conditions, emotions, and ideas.

Categories & Examples

Emotions (Mawendo):

Thayu (peace), ũgĩ (wisdom), ũtugi (kindness), wendo (love)

Qualities (Maatu ma gũtonga):

ũrĩa mwega (goodness), ũthoni (hospitality), hinya (strength), ũtugi (generosity)

Ideas (Mawendo ma mĩtĩrĩri):

ũhoro (truth), ũhoro wa wendo (love affair)

Conditions (Makorwo):

ũgima (health), ũthĩnĩ (poverty), thayũ (peace)

Sample Sentences

Thayu wake wonekanire thutha wa wendo wa rũgendo.
→ "His peace was clear after the safe journey."

Wĩhokeku nĩ wa maana mũno thĩ-inĩ wa andũ.
→ "Honesty is very important in society."

F. Plural Forms in Kikuyu (Animals & Birds)

  • Many animal names are invariable in plural form.
  • Some take the ci- prefix in the plural.

Examples:

  • Ngui → Ngui (dog → dogs)
  • Ng'ombe → Ng'ombe (cow → cows)
  • Mburi → Mburi (goat → goats)
  • Gũkũ → Gũkũ (chicken → chickens)

5. Kikuyu Noun Pluralization: Functional Grammar

General Structure

Pluralization is class-based:

  • Prefix change (mũ- → a-; kĩ- → ci-; gĩ- → i-)
  • Invariant forms (no change)
  • Irregular plurals (completely different words)

Examples by Class

Class I (mũ- → a-):

Mũthuuri → Athuuri (man → men), Mũciĩ → Mĩciĩ (home → homes).

Class II (kĩ- → ci-):

Kĩhenia → Ihenia (hen → hens), Gĩtĩ → Itĩ (chair → chairs).

Class III (gĩ- → i-):

Gĩcere → Icere (bean → beans).

Class IV (invariant):

Ngui → Ngui (dog → dogs), Ng'ombe → Ng'ombe (cow → cows).

Class V (irregular):

Mwana → Ciana (child → children), Mũndũ → Andũ (person → people).

6. Noun Construct in Kikuyu

Formation Rules

Singular construct: drop the final vowel of the singular form.

  • Gĩtĩ → gĩtĩ-
  • Mũciĩ → mũci-

Plural construct: usually identical to the plural form.

Demonstrative Suffixes

Meaning Suffix
This -ĩno
That (near) -ĩyo
That (far) -ĩrĩa
These -ĩno
Those (near) -ĩyo
Those (far) -ĩrĩa

Examples with "gĩtĩ" (chair):

  • Gĩtĩ gĩkĩ – this chair
  • Gĩtĩ kĩrĩa – that chair (near)
  • Itĩ ĩcĩĩ – these chairs
  • Itĩ ĩcĩo – those chairs (near)

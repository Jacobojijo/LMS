2.5.2 Personal pronouns

1. What are personal pronouns?

  • Definition: Personal pronouns refer to participants in discourse — speaker, addressee(s), and others.
  • Function: They index the agent/experiencer in clauses and can serve as subjects, objects, or targets of agreement on the verb.
  • Example usage given: Niĩ ndĩria — "I'm eating." ; Nĩwe araria — "He/She speaks."

2. The six core personal pronouns (systematic table)

Kikuyu form English equivalent Person / Number Usage note Example sentence
Niĩ I / me 1st person singular Subject form Niĩ ndĩ mũciĩ → I am at home
Wĩ You (singular) 2nd person singular Subject form Wĩ mũciĩ → You are at home
E / Nĩwe He / She / It 3rd person singular Gender-neutral E mũciĩ → He/She is at home
Ithue We / us 1st person plural Inclusive/exclusive distinctions not provided here Ithue twe mũciĩ → We are at home
Inyũe / ũyũ You (plural) 2nd person plural Variant forms recorded Inyũe mwe mũciĩ → You (all) are at home
Acio They / them 3rd person plural Subject form Acio me mũciĩ → They are at home

Note (explicit in source): E works for both "he" and "she." The dataset contains two surface variants for the 2nd-person plural: Inyũe and ũyũ.

3. Demonstratives + personal pronouns (compact patterns)

Short illustrative phrases

Phrase Illocution / gloss
Niĩ ũyũ "I, this" (an emphatic self-identification: "I am the one")
Nĩwe ũrĩa "He/She is that one"
Nĩ ayũ "They are these ones"

4. Structure of a simple sentence (canonical template & examples)

Canonical structure Subject pronoun + Verb (+ Object / Location)

Provided realisations:

  • Niĩ ndĩ mũciĩ → I am at home.
  • Wĩ ũgoka rũciinĩ → You will come in the morning.
  • Nĩwe ũgũtũma barua → He/She sends a letter.
  • Ithue nĩ tũhikanĩte → We are married.
  • Inyũe mũgũcooka tondũ ni tũgũcooka → You (all) will return because we will return.
  • Acio marĩ wĩra → They are at work.

1.6.7 Review & Practice

1.6.1 Summary of General Ways of Communication

1. Greetings by Time of Day (Gũtũmĩrĩra na Thaa)

Morning:

  • Wamũka atĩa? – How did you wake up?
  • Wamũka wega – I woke up well.

Midday/General:

  • ũhoro waku? – How are you?
  • Nĩ wega – I'm fine.

Evening:

  • Wakia atĩa? – How was your day?
  • Nĩ wega – It was good.

Night:

  • Gũtiraga wega – Good night.
  • Nĩ wega gũtiraga – Sleep well.

2. Farewells (Gũkũgũra)

  • Tigũoni – Goodbye.
  • Tigũoni na wendo – Goodbye with love.
  • Tigũoni na thayũ – Go in peace.
  • Tũkũonana ũgĩthiĩ – See you later.

3. Politeness and Courtesy (Gũhoya na Gũtũũra na Wendo)

  • Thegio – Thank you.
  • Nĩ sori – I'm sorry.
  • Ndekera – Please forgive me.
  • Ndũnyonie... – Please show me...
  • Ndũhe... – Please give me...
  • Nĩ wega – It's okay.
  • Ndeithis – Help me.
  • Nĩdĩreda uteithio – I need help.
  • Ndikumenyaga – I didn't know.

4. Family Terms (Andũ a Mũciĩ)

  • Baba – Father
  • Mami / Maitũ – Mother
  • Mũriũ – Son
  • Mwarĩ – Daughter
  • Mũrũ wa nyina – Brother
  • Mwarĩ wa nyina – Sister
  • Guka – Grandfather
  • Cũcũ – Grandmother
  • Mama – Uncle
  • Tata – Aunt
  • Mũihwa – Nephew / Niece
  • Mũrũ wa tata – Male cousin
  • Mwarĩ wa tata – Female cousin
  • Tūcūcū – Grandchildren
  • Tūcūkūrū – Great-grandchildren
  • Cūkūrūkū – Great-great-grandchildren

5. Household Items (Ithambĩ rĩa Mũciĩ)

  • Giti – Chair
  • Metha – Table
  • Gilathi – Glass
  • Thani – Plate
  • Kihato – Broom
  • Kirato – Shoe
  • Gitanda – Bed
  • Redio – Radio
  • Gicicio – Mirror
  • Mugunda – Farm/Garden

6. People's Titles (Mataito ma Andũ)

  • Mũthuuri – Man
  • Mũtumia – Woman
  • Mwana – Child
  • Mwarimũ – Teacher
  • Mũthomi – Student
  • Mwendwa wakwa – My dear
  • Jenerali – General
  • Mũciĩ wa ita – Soldier
  • Mũganga – Doctor
  • Kĩganu – Watchman
  • Mũtharia – Elder (respected person)

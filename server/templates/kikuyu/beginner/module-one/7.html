1.4.4 Days of the Week and Months

Days of the Week

The Kikuyu traditional week consists of seven days, each with a distinct name and cultural significance, often linked to daily activities, spiritual beliefs, and community life.

1. Kiumia (Sunday) – Day of Rest and Worship

Derived from Kikuyu word for rest and prayer.

Traditionally reserved for spiritual reflection and family time.

Example in a Sentence:

Kiumia nĩ ndalo ya gũkĩra na kũnyitĩra ũhoro wa mũthenya.

Translation: "Sunday is a day for resting and praying for the workweek."

2. Jumatatũ (Monday) – The First Day of Work

Traditionally marks the start of farming or trade activities for the week.

Example in a Sentence:

Jumatatu nĩ mũthenya wa thirikari na maũndũ ma wendo.

Translation: "Monday is the beginning of government work and errands."

3. Wa kerĩ (Tuesday) – The Second Day of Work

Traditionally often a day for tending to livestock or trading produce.

Example in a Sentence:

Wa kerĩ ndaro ũgĩkũyũ ũtethagia mĩthenya ya kũgũrũka.

Translation: "On Tuesday, the Kikuyu farmers continue their planting."

4. Wa gatatũ (Wednesday) – The Third Day of Work

Midweek, a day to focus on major farming activities or community meetings.

Example in a Sentence:

Wa gatatũ ni mũthenya wa mũno kũgũrũka na kũmbatiria.

Translation: "Wednesday is an important day for planting and harvesting."

5. Wa kana (Thursday) – The Fourth Day of Work

Often associated with preparation for market or social gatherings.

Example in a Sentence:

Wa kana nĩ mũthenya wa kũgũtũra na gũciririka mĩthenya.

Translation: "Thursday is a day to prepare and exchange goods."

6. Juma (Friday) – The Fifth Day of Work

Traditionally a day for final work on the farm before rest days.

Important for ensuring crops and livestock are cared for.

Example in a Sentence:

"Juma nĩ mũthenya wa kũheera gĩkũyũ na kũtega mũthenya wa mũhũa."

Translation: "Friday is the day to finish tasks and prepare for the weekend."

7. Jumamothi (Saturday) – Market Day

Jumamothi ni ndalo ya masanduku na gũcokeria maũndũ.

Example in a sentence: Jumamothi nĩ mũthenya wa cĩrugũ na mihiriga ya andũ

Translation: "Saturday is the day for markets and social events."

Months of the Year

The Kikuyu names for months have been adapted from the Gregorian calendar but often include cultural meanings tied to farming cycles, weather, and ceremonies.

Formation of Month Names

Kikuyu months use cardinal numbers with the prefix "Mweri wa" meaning "month of," linking the month name to its order in the year.

Names of the Months

Kikuyu Month Name Meaning English Equivalent
Mweri wa mbere First month January
Mweri wa kerĩ Second month February
Mweri wa gatatũ Third month March
Mweri wa kana Fourth month April
Mweri wa gatano Fifth month May
Mweri wa gatandatũ Sixth month June
Mweri wa mũgwanja Seventh month July
Mweri wa kanana Eighth month August
Mweri wa kenda Ninth month September
Mweri wa ikũmi Tenth month October
Mweri wa ikũmi na imwe Eleventh month November
Mweri wa ikũmi na igĩrĩ Twelfth month December

Examples in Sentences

Mweri wa mbere ni mweri wa gũciara maũndũ ma gũtũma.
Translation: "January is the month to begin planting crops."

Mweri wa kerĩ nĩ mweri wa gũtirĩra maũndũ na kũhoya nyũmba.
Translation: "February is a month of clearing and preparing the homestead."

Mweri wa gatatũ nĩ mweri wa gũcokia mĩtĩ na kuoya.
Translation: "March is the month for pruning trees and clearing fields."

Mweri wa kana nĩ mweri wa gũtira mbembe na wendo.
Translation: "April is the month for planting beans and maize."

Mweri wa gatano nĩ mweri wa kũgũcokia na kũrĩa ng'ombe.
Translation: "May is the month for harvesting and feeding cattle."

Mweri wa gatandatũ nĩ mweri wa gũtirĩra mĩtĩ na gũcokia thirikari.
Translation: "June is the month for clearing trees and government activities."

Mweri wa mũgwanja nĩ mweri wa gũcokia maũndũ ma wendo.
Translation: "July is the month to gather crops."

Mweri wa kanana nĩ mweri wa kũgũrũka na gũcokeria maũndũ.
Translation: "August is the month for planting and trading."

Mweri wa kenda nĩ mweri wa kũhoya mĩgũnda na kũgũcokia mĩrũri.
Translation: "September is the month for preparing fields and harvesting vegetables."

Mweri wa ikũmi nĩ mweri wa kũhoya maũndũ ma wendo na gũtirĩra nyũmba.
Translation: "October is the month for harvesting crops and cleaning homes."

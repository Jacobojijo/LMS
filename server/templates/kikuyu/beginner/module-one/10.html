1.6 General Ways of Communication

1.6.1 Introduction

The Agĩkũyũ people place high value on respectful and courteous communication, especially during greetings. Greetings (Mũkomano) are deeply embedded in Kikuyu social etiquette and reflect time of day, age, respect, and community bonding. The structure of greetings aligns with both the Kikuyu worldview and time-consciousness, emphasizing relationship-building and mutual well-being.

1.6.2 General Forms of Greetings

a) Morning Greeting

Used early in the day when meeting someone for the first time.

Wĩ mwega rũcinĩ

"Good morning" (to one person) Can also mean, "Are you well this morning?"

Murĩ ega rũcinĩ

"Good morning" (to many people)

Response:

Ndĩ mwega, na we wĩ mwega?

"I am well, and are you well?"

Ndĩ mwega mũno

"Very well"

b) Midday/General Greeting

Used any time during the day when sun is high.

Wĩ mwega mũthenya

"Good day" (to one person)

Mũrĩeega mũthenya

"Good day" (to many people)

Response:

Ndĩ mwega mũno

"I'm Very good"

Nĩwega

"Fine"

c) Evening Greeting

Used as the day closes and people begin to rest.

Wĩ mwega hwainĩ

"Good evening" (to one person)

Mũrĩeega hwainĩ

"Good evening" (to many people)

Response:

Nĩ mwega mũno

"Very well"

d) Night Greeting

Used when people are about to go to bed.

Koma wega

"Sleep well" (to one person)

Komai wega

"Sleep well" (to many people)

Farewell and Goodbye Expressions

The Kikuyu expression for goodbye reflects more than just parting; it expresses well-being and continuity of relationship.

  • Tũonane rĩngĩ - "We will see each other again" (common among close relations)
  • Thie na thayũ - "Go with blessings" (formal)
  • Kurũo na thayũ - "Be in peace" (to one person)
  • Kurwũoi na thayũ - "Be in peace" (to many people)

Structure of Greetings

Kikuyu greetings follow clear syntactic forms:

  • Time marker + Verb + Addressee
    • Wĩ mwega rũcinĩ - "You are well this morning?"
    • Mwĩ mwega mũthenya - "Are you all well this afternoon?"
  • Subject + Verb + Intensifier
    • Nĩ mwega mũno - "It is very good"

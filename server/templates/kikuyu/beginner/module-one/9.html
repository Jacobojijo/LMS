1.5.1 Summary of Numbers & Numbering

Kikuyu Numbers and Their Usage

The Kikuyu numbering system is based on ten (decimal system), used in everyday life such as counting, trade, timekeeping, and cultural practices. Numbers are logically structured, especially beyond ten.

Cardinal Numbers

Basic Numbers (1–10):

  • Ĩmwe (1),
  • Igĩrĩ (2),
  • Ĩtatũ (3),
  • Inya (4),
  • Ithano (5),
  • Ithathatũ (6),
  • Mugwanja (7),
  • Inyanya (8),
  • Kenda (9),
  • Ikũmi (10)

Compound Numbers (11–19):

  • Ikũmi na ĩmwe (11),
  • Ikũmi na igĩrĩ (12), etc.

Multiples of Ten (20–90):

  • Mirongo ĩrĩ (20),
  • Mirongo ĩtatũ (30),
  • Mirongo kenda (90)

Hundreds:

  • Magana matano (500),
  • Magana kenda (900)

Thousands:

  • Tũgũrũ magana (e.g. Tũgũrũ magana matano = 5,000)

Usage in Sentences

Trade & Counting:

Athuri matano maarĩ na ng'ombe mirongo ĩrĩ.
("Five elders had twenty cows.")

Family & Social Context:

Mwana umwe na atumia atheru.
("One child and several women.")

Examples of Quantification

  • Mbũri (goats): Ithano
  • Acaari (girls): Kenda
  • Nyũmba (homes): Ikũmi na ithano
  • Aana (children): Ikũmi na ĩmwe
  • Ng'ombe (cows): Mirongo ĩtatũ

Ordinal Numbers

Ordinal numbers in Kikuyu show position or sequence, using the prefix "Wa-" before cardinal numbers.

Key Ordinal Numbers:

  • Wa mbere (first)
  • Wa kerĩ (second)
  • Wa gatatũ (third)
  • Wa kana (fourth)
  • Wa gatano (fifth)
  • Wa gatandatũ (sixth)
  • Wa mũgwanja (seventh)
  • Wa kanana (eighth)
  • Wa kenda (ninth)
  • Wa ikũmi (tenth)

Cultural and Linguistic Relevance:

Storytelling & Genealogy:

Mwana wa gatano nĩwe wakinyire mbere.
("The fifth child arrived first.")

Leadership & Social Rank:

Mũthamaki wa kerĩ nĩwe warutithagia mbarathi.
("The second chief led the warriors.")

Ceremonies & Schooling:

Mweri wa gatandatũ nĩ mweri wa cũgũo na kũgũrana.
("The sixth month is for ceremonies and visits.")

Examples in Context:

Mũndũ wa kana nĩwe wathiĩ mbere.
("The fourth person went first.")

Mwana wa gatatũ nĩwe wakonyire mwendwa.
("The third child helped the loved one.")

Othe marĩa magũcokia nĩ arĩa ma wa kenda.
("Those returning were in ninth place.")

1.6.6 Expressing Politeness

In Kikuyu culture, wĩhĩtĩ (respect) and gũkena (good manners) are very important. People use kind and humble language to live peacefully with others. This includes how to say thank you, ask for something, or say sorry.

a) Gratitude and Appreciation

  • Thegio – Thank you
  • Thegio muno – Thank you very much

Examples:

  • Thegio muno kũgũcokia gĩtanda. (Thank you very much for returning the bed.)
  • Thegio mwendwa wakwa. (Thank you, my dear.)

b) Asking for Something Politely

  • Ndũhe... – Please give me...
  • Ndũnyonie... – Please show me...
  • Ndeithia – Please help me
  • Nĩdĩreda uteithio – I need help

Examples:

  • Ndũhe mai. (Please give me water.)
  • Ndũnyonie njira ya gũkĩra kwa mama. (Please show me the way to Mama's house.)
  • Nĩdĩreda uteithio wa gũtigira mbembe. (I need help to carry maize.)
  • Ndeithia, mwendwa wakwa. (Please help me, my dear.)

c) Responses of Courtesy

  • Nĩ wega – It's okay / It is well
  • Thegio, nĩ wega – Thank you, it's okay

Example:

  • Thegio gũkũhe ũtethio. (Thank you for helping.) → Nĩ wega. (It's okay.)

d) Apologies and Excuses

  • Nĩ Sori – I'm sorry
  • Ndekera – Please forgive me
  • Ndikumenyaga – I didn't know

Examples:

  • Nĩ Sori, ndirĩmena gũcoka mũhuko. (I'm sorry, I forgot to return the knife.)
  • Ndekera, ndĩkumenyaga ũcio nĩ waku. (Forgive me, I didn't know that was yours.)

e) Acknowledging or Denying Statements

  • Ĩĩ – Yes
  • Aca – No
  • Ndirenda – I don't want

Examples:

  • Ũgĩhe mũtĩ wa marigũ? (Will you give the pumpkin leaves?) → Ĩĩ. (Yes.)
  • Ndũhe thahabu ciothe? (Give me all the money?) → Aca, ndirenda. (No, I don't want.)

f) Helping and Assistance

  • Ndeithia – Help me
  • Nĩdĩreda uteithio – I need help

Examples:

  • Ndeithia gũkũhura nyũmba. (Help me clean the house.)
  • Nĩdĩreda uteithio wa gũcarurũa mbembe. (I need help to shell maize.)

g) Cultural Context of Politeness

In Kikuyu culture, people talk with kindness and respect.

  • Speak softly, especially to elders.
  • Use polite words like Ndeithia or Ndekera to show humility.
  • Smiling and using a calm voice shows you are respectful.

h) Everyday Examples

  • Greeting an elder: Thegio muno wamũkũrũ. (Thank you very much, elder.)
  • Apologizing in public: Nĩ Sori, ndĩkumenyaga. (I'm sorry, I didn't know.)
  • Requesting help: Nĩdĩreda uteithio wa gũtungata. (I need help cleaning.)
  • Refusing politely: Aca, ndirenda kũrĩa nyama. (No, I don't want to eat meat.)

1.6.4 Everyday Objects (Domestic & Household items)

Around the Home

The Kikuyu language is deeply woven into everyday life, with household vocabulary reflecting not only practical living but also traditional and cultural values. Items found within and around the home are essential in illustrating Kikuyu customs, self-sufficiency, and hospitality.

a) The Home as a Cultural Centre

  • Nyũmba – House/Home. Traditionally made with mud walls and thatched roofs, the nyũmba is the heart of the family.
  • Ithigi rĩa nyumba – Roof. Traditionally made of grass; in modern homes, iron sheets (mabati) are used.
  • Thĩ ya nyumba – Floor. Usually earthen in older homes, now cemented in modern ones.

b) Dining Essentials

  • Gĩkombe – Cup. Used for tea (chaĩ), porridge (ũcũrũ), or other drinks.
  • Gilathi – Glass. Modern drinking glass used especially during formal meals.
  • Thani – Plate. Used to serve dishes like gĩtheri, irio, or nyama.
  • Nyugu – Cooking pot. Used to prepare traditional meals like gĩtheri, mũkimo, and mbũzi.
  • Ikembe – Calabash. Traditionally used to serve ũcũrũ or fermented milk (mursik).

c) Furnishings and Comfort

  • Gĩtĩ – Chair/Seat. Where elders or guests sit for conversation or rest.
  • Metha – Table. Used for modern dining or to hold family items.
  • Kabati – Cupboard. Used to store utensils, food, or clothing.
  • Sofa – Sofa/Couch. Modern furniture representing comfort and hospitality.

d) Cleaning and Maintenance

  • Gĩtanda – Bed. Traditionally wooden or reed-framed, used for sleeping.
  • Kĩhato – Broom. Sweeps the house and compound, keeping it clean.
  • Ndoo – Bucket/Pail. Used for fetching and storing water.

e) Personal Belongings

  • Ngurũ – Slippers. Light footwear worn around the home.
  • Kĩrato – Shoe. Ranges from traditional sandals to modern footwear.
  • Mbeca – Money. Used in trade, markets, and everyday transactions.

f) Modern Utilities

  • Redio – Radio. Listened to for news, music, and stories (ciana cia mũno).
  • Televisheni – Television. Brings entertainment and educational content.
  • Gĩcicio – Mirror. Used for grooming and reflection.

g) Educational and Practical Tools

  • Kalamu – Pen. A writing tool for school and office work.
  • Kĩtabu – Book. Used for reading, study, and preserving knowledge.

h) Outdoors and Utility

  • Mũgũnda – Farm/Garden. For growing staple crops and sustaining families.
  • Mũgwati – Stool. Traditional, often small, for sitting outdoors.

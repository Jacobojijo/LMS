1.8 Review & Practice

1.8.1 Summary of Nouns (Part 01)

I. Definition and Role of Nouns in Kikuyu

Term: Rũciaro (pl. maciaro)

Function: Denote names of people, animals, objects, places, ideas.

Syntactic Roles: Serve as subjects (gũtungatwo) or objects (gũtongoretwo) within sentences.

II. Morphological Structure of Kikuyu Nouns

Prefixes:

  • Singular: Mũ- → mũndũ (person)
  • Plural: Aba-, A-, Iĩrĩ- → andũ (people), iĩrĩma (hills)

Suffixes/Particles: Indicate size, possession, or nuances of meaning.

III. Semantic Categories of Nouns

a) Concrete Nouns – Physical/tangible entities:

gĩkũyũ (stone), gĩkanda (book), nyina (mother)

b) Abstract Nouns – Ideas, states, or qualities:

ũiguithanio (understanding), wendo (love), wĩtĩkio (faith)

c) Proper Nouns – Specific individuals or locations:

Kĩambu (county), Wanjiku (name), Mũthoni (visitor-born)

d) Common Nouns – General classes or kinds:

mũndũ (person), kĩhĩĩ (boy), ng'ombe (cow)

e) Collective Nouns – Plurality/groupings:

andũ (people), mĩtĩ (trees), ngũgũ (chickens)

IV. Lexical Ambiguity: Equivocal Words (Magĩcokanio)

Nature: Homonyms (words with identical spelling/pronunciation but differing meanings).

Resolution: Disambiguated through context (kũhũthũrũkana kwa gũciarĩra), tone, and stress.

V. Synonymy in Kikuyu (Matukũrĩrĩ)

Definition: Semantically similar words across dialects and regions.

Function: Enriches expressive precision and reflects regional variation.

Examples of Kikuyu Synonyms:

VI. Gender Classification of Kikuyu Nouns

System Type: Natural (semantic), not grammatical.

Categories:

  • Masculine: Male persons/animals.
  • Feminine: Female persons/animals.
  • Neuter: Objects, places, abstract concepts.

Masculine Nouns:

Mũthuuri (man), Mũrũ wa nyina (boy), Ndurume (ram), Njiro (bull), Njamba (cock)

Feminine Nouns:

Mũkũrũ (woman), Mwarĩ (girl), Njaũ (calf), Ng'ombe (cow), Kanyoni ka cũcũ (hen)

Neuter Nouns:

Giti (chair), Nyũmba (house), Mugunda (farm), Gitanda (bed), Mũthenya (day)

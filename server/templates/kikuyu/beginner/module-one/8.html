1.4.5 Telling Time in Kikuyu

In Kikuyu, telling time includes expressions for the present, past, and future, as well as parts of the day. Kikuyu temporal language is rich with cultural context, used for organizing daily routines, seasons, and ceremonies.

General Temporal Expressions

English Kikuyu
Now Thaa ici
Morning Rũciinĩ
Later Thaa inge
Yesterday ira
Day before yesterday oke
Today Ũmũthĩ
Tomorrow Rũcio
Day after tomorrow Rũciinĩ rwa kũgũrũka

Example Sentences

Nĩ ngwenda kũgĩa mĩtugo ũmũthĩ.
Translation: I want to follow traditions today.
Ira, twarari na mũthenya mũnene.
Translation: Yesterday, we had a big day.
Mathaa mangi, tũgĩkũrũgĩra mũgambo.
Translation: Later, we will share the news.
Rũciinĩ rwa kũgũrũka tũgĩambĩra gũtũma.
Translation: The day after tomorrow, we will begin planting.

Time of Day in Kikuyu

The Kikuyu divide the day into several parts, each with cultural and practical significance:

Time of Day Kikuyu Notes
Morning Rũciinĩ Early part of the day
Afternoon Gatagati ka mũthenya Midday to late afternoon
Evening Hwainĩ Sunset hours
Night ũtukũ Night time

Example Sentences

Rũciinĩ, andũ matuĩkaga kũrĩa.
Translation: In the morning, people prepare food.
Gatagati ka mũthenya gukorwagwo na riũa rinene.
Translation: There is normally much sun at midday.
Hwainĩ, njũĩra ĩrĩa nĩ ngwenda kũhoya.
Translation: In the evening, I choose to pray.
Ũmũthĩ nĩ wendo wa Gũkĩra na kũgwĩta mĩthenya ya rũciinĩ.
Translation: Today is meant for resting and preparing for tomorrow.

Time-Related Sentence Structures in Kikuyu

  • Time marker + Subject + Verb + Object
Rũciinĩ rwa + nyina + nĩaonete + mwarimu.
(Yesterday, the mother saw the teacher.)
  • Subject + Verb + Time marker
Ngũgũcoka hĩndĩ ĩrĩa igũtũka.
(I will return later.)
  • Temporal adverb in sentence
Gĩcunjĩ kia gĩtugo, ndirathire gũtara.
(At midnight, I had not yet slept.)

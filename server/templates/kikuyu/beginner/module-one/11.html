1.6.3 Family Members

Kikuyu Kinship Structure

The Agĩkũyũ people, like many African communities, have a rich and expansive kinship system that recognizes both the nuclear and extended family. Kinship goes beyond just biological ties and plays a central role in cultural identity, social responsibilities, and intergenerational connection.

Core Family Terms

English Kikuyu Notes
Father Baba Also used in spiritual contexts
Mother Mami / Maitũ Maitũ is more traditional
Son Mũriũ A male child
Daughter Mwarĩ A female child
Brother Mũrũ wa nyina "Son of my mother"
Sister Mwarĩ wa nyina "Daughter of my mother"
Grandfather Guka Used for both paternal and maternal sides
Grandmother Cũcũ Same as above
Nephew Mũihwa A brother's or sister's son
Niece Mũihwa A brother's or sister's daughter
Cousin (male) Mũrũ wa tata Son of my father's sibling
Cousin (female) Mwarĩ wa iya Daughter of my mother's sibling
Uncle (mother's brother) Mama Holds an advisory role
Aunt (mother's sister) Taata Affectionate and mentorship role
Father's older brother Baba Mūkūrū Highly respected elder
Father's younger brother Baba Mūnyinyi Often close in age to nieces/nephews
Wife of father's older brother Maitũ Mūkūrū A respected maternal figure

The Role of Gender in Kinship Terminology

In Kikuyu, relationships are often gendered, meaning kinship terms reflect whether a person is male or female. This helps define roles, responsibilities, and respect levels within the family.

Relationship Kikuyu Term
Sister-in-law Maramu
Brother-in-law Wamûgongo
Father-in-law Iciciara
Mother-in-law Nyaciara

Generational Hierarchies

Beyond grandparents, the Kikuyu recognize multiple generations:

Generation Kikuyu Term
Grandchildren Tūcūkūrū
Great-grandchildren Cūkūrūkū

These generations emphasize continuity of lineage and respect for ancestry.

Polygamy and Wife Hierarchies

Historically, polygamy existed among the Agĩkũyũ, with a structured order of wives that affected inheritance, status, and household authority.

Wife Order Kikuyu Term Role/Explanation
First wife Nyakīambi Holds the highest household status
Second wife Nyakīambūri Comes after Nyakīambi
Third wife Mūte Itūūra Typically the youngest or last wife

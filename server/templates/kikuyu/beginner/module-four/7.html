4.4.1 Summary of Adverbs, Order of Speech & Compliments

A. ADVERBS IN KIKUYU GRAMMAR (Rũrĩrĩ rwa Gũtirĩrĩria)

Introduction

Adverbs in Kikuyu are words that modify or add detail to:

  • A verb (kiria gĩtũma gũkorerwo)
  • An adjective (kiria gĩtũma kĩmenyererwo)
  • Another adverb (kiria gĩgũtirĩrĩria rĩngĩ)
  • Or sometimes the whole sentence

Adverbs answer questions like:

  • How? (Atĩa?) → ũrĩa (manner)
  • When? (Rĩ?) → rĩ, na rĩrĩ (time)
  • Where? (Kuu?) → harĩa / haha (place)
  • To what extent? (Gũtiũ?) → mũno / ahinja (degree)
  • How often? (Kinya rĩ?) → rĩrĩ kũrĩa, thutha, rũgendo (frequency)

Functions of Adverbs

Function What it modifies Example Meaning
Verb modification tells how action happens rũgendo rũkũrũ mũno (very fast journey)
Adjective modification makes adjective stronger mũnene mũno (very big)
Adverb modification intensifies another adverb harĩa harĩa (very far / extremely there)
Sentence commentary expresses time, place, or mood Rĩu tũtũraga (Now we continue)

Categories of Adverbs in Kikuyu

Category Kikuyu Examples English Meaning
Manner (ũrĩa) ũthaka, mwero, kĩgũrũ carefully, slowly, hurriedly
Time (rĩ) rĩu (now), nyũmba (yesterday), rũciũ (tomorrow)
Place (kũrĩa) haha (here), harĩa (there), gĩthima (outside)
Degree (gũtiũ) mũno (very), ahinĩa (extremely)
Frequency (thutha rĩrĩa) kĩnyari thutha (sometimes), rĩrĩ gũkũ (often)

Illustrative Examples (Kikuyu + English)

Kikuyu Sentence English Translation Type of Adverb
"Mũndũ ũcio aigua mwero." That person listens slowly/quietly. Manner
"Nĩtũkũraya rĩu." We are starting now. Time
"Mwarimu arĩ haha." The teacher is here. Place
"Ng'ombe yanyu nĩnene mũno." Your cow is very big. Degree
"O na kĩnyari thutha nĩakehũraga." He sometimes gets tired. Frequency

Syntactic Notes

In Kikuyu, adverbs mostly follow the verb.

Reduplication (repeating the word) gives intensity:

  • mwero mwero = very slowly/very quietly
  • harĩa harĩa = extremely far

B. ORDER OF SPEECH / SENTENCE STRUCTURE (SVO)

(Kĩmenyero gĩa gũthaitha rũrĩrĩ — Subject + Verb + Object)

Core Sentence Structure

Kikuyu, like English, follows:

✅ Subject + Verb + Object (SVO)

Element Function Example
Subject Doer of action Mũrũ wa Nyeri (son of Nyeri)
Verb Action arĩa (eat)
Object What receives action irio (food)

➡️ Example sentence:

Mũrũ wa Nyeri arĩa irio.

The son of Nyeri is eating food.

Illustrative Examples

Kikuyu English
Mũthuri arũgaga kĩgongona. The man is fixing the roof.
Mwarĩ wa Karatina asoma ibuku. The girl reads a book.
Mũbarũti arŭga thīgira. The carpenter builds the fence.

➡️ Order remains S – V – O.

C. COMPLEMENTS

1. Direct Complement (Complement receiving the action)

Definition

The direct complement (object) receives the verb's action. Without it, the sentence feels incomplete.

✅ Structure: Subject + Verb + Direct Object

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
"Mũbarũti arũga thīgira." The carpenter builds the fence.
"Mwarĩ asoma ibuku." The girl reads a book.

2. Indirect Complement (Beneficiary / Recipient)

Definition

Shows to whom / for whom something is done.

✅ Structure: Subject + Verb + Indirect Complement + Direct Object

Kikuyu Example English
"Mũthuri amĩhe mwarĩ cokore." The man gave the girl a broom.

3. Complement of Terminality (Destination / receiver)

Marker used: kũ / kũrĩa

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
"Amũhe ibuku kũrĩ mwarĩ." Give the book to the girl.

4. Complement of Specification (possession / belonging)

Marker used: wa

Kikuyu Example English
"Ng'ombe wa mũthuri." The cow of the man / The man's cow.
"Gĩthaka wa ciana." The children's farm.

5. Complement of Origin (identity / lineage / place)

Marker used: mũru wa / mwarĩ wa / wa

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
Nĩ mũrũ wa Nyeri. He is from Nyeri.
Nĩ mwarĩ wa Karatina. She is from Karatina.
Tũrĩ a Gaki. We are from Gaki.
Nĩ mũndũ wa Murang'a. He/she is a person from Murang'a.

4.13.1 Introductions

I. Definition and Function of Conjunctions

Conjunctions (mĩtĩka ya gũtirĩa) are grammatical connectors used to link or separate:

  • Words
  • Phrases
  • Clauses
  • Ideas
  • Objects

Function: They provide logical and syntactic cohesion in Gĩkũyũ sentences, similar to English conjunctions such as and, or, because, so that.

Significance: Mastery of conjunctions allows speakers to:

  • Build complex sentences
  • Express cause, reason, or purpose
  • Connect ideas clearly in oral and written communication

II. Categories of Gĩkũyũ Conjunctions

A. Additive and Coordinating Conjunctions

English Gĩkũyũ Usage / Notes
And na Joins similar elements: nouns, verbs, or phrases
Also / Even ũngĩ / kũrĩ Adds emphasis or inclusion
Together hamwe na Emphasizes unity, collectivity, or joint action
With na / hamwe na Denotes accompaniment with people or instruments

Illustrative Examples:

  • Mũbebe na mbũri — The maize and the goat
  • Nĩhande mbembe hamwe na mwana — I plant maize together with the child
  • Ndĩ nake na mbebe — I am with him/her with the maize

B. Disjunctive and Alternative Conjunctions

English Gĩkũyũ Usage / Notes
Or kana Offers alternatives or choices
Either / Or kana / o kana Stylistically interchangeable for choice or exclusion

Examples:

  • Wĩra kana gĩthaka? — Work or farm?
  • Wĩra o kana njũra? — Will you work or rest?

III. Practical Coordination in Gĩkũyũ

  • Mbembe na mbũri — Maize and goat
  • Nyũmba na ng'ombe — House and cow
  • Mbembe hamwe na mwana — Maize together with the child

Note: Coordination reflects natural pairings, thematic links, or social context.

IV. Conjunctions of Reason and Cause

A. Expressing Why / Asking for Reason

The correct way to ask "Why?" in Gĩkũyũ is to use the interrogative pronoun Nĩkĩ.

Concept Correct Gĩkũyũ Equivalent Literal Meaning Notes
Why? Nĩkĩ It is what? The standard, direct question word for "Why?"

Example:

Gĩkũyũ Expression English Translation
Nĩkĩ ũrũtaga wĩra? Why do you work?

B. Expressing Cause / Reason

Gĩkũyũ English Equivalent Usage
nĩũndũ wa/ Nĩũgũo because / due to Most common causal connector
tondũ because / for the reason Expresses cause or justification
nĩgĩtũmi because / for the reason Formal or explanatory tone

Examples:

  • Nĩhande mbembe nĩũgũo mwana athome — I plant maize because the child may learn
  • Ndĩrahanda mbembe tondũ wa mwana guthoma — I plant maize because the child may learn
  • Nĩhande mbembe nĩgĩtũmi wa mwana athome — I plant maize because of the child's need

Note: These causal connectors are interchangeable depending on context, but nĩũndũ wa is the most neutral and widely used.

V. Purpose Clauses and Subjunctive Intent

A. Ni Nĩguo — "So that / in order to"

  • Construction: Ni nĩguo + verb (subjunctive / future-oriented)
  • Purpose: Expresses intended outcome or goal, not cause.

Examples:

  • Nĩnjaria nĩguo njone hinya. — I eat so that I may gain energy
  • Nĩhande mbembe ni nĩguo mwana athome — We plant maize so that the child may learn

Syntax Highlight:

  • Ni = that / for
  • Nĩguo = marker of purpose

Important: Ni nĩguo ≠ nĩũndũ wa / tondũ

  • Ni nĩguo → future-oriented purpose
  • nĩũndũ wa / tondũ → reason / cause

VI. Important Usage Notes

  • Na / Hamwe na = expresses togetherness or accompaniment
  • Kana / o kana = expresses alternative / choice
  • Nĩũndũ wa / tondũ / nĩgĩtũmi = expresses cause / reason
  • Ni nĩguo = expresses purpose / intended outcome
  • Mastery requires correct tone, syntactic placement, and verb dependency

VII. Summary of Pedagogical Implications

Gĩkũyũ uses conjunctions to:

  • Join nouns, verbs, phrases, and clauses
  • Express cause, reason, and purpose
  • Build complex, coherent sentences

Functional distinctions between connectors (e.g., na / hamwe na, kana, nĩũndũ wa, ni nĩguo) must be emphasized

Correct use enables narrative, persuasive, and academic competence

4.3.1 Introductions

1. Definition and Function

In Kikuyu grammar, a complement is the part of a sentence that receives the action of a verb directly. It mostly appears as a direct object, helping to complete the meaning of a sentence.

Without the complement, the sentence sounds incomplete or unclear. It makes the action more meaningful and shows where the effect of the verb goes.

2. Key Characteristic: The Action Passes to the Object

In a Kikuyu sentence with a complement, the verb's action goes directly to the noun that follows it. This means the action done by the subject affects the object immediately.

3. Sentence Structure: Subject + Verb + Complement (Object)

The Kikuyu structure follows the usual SVO (Subject–Verb–Object) pattern:

  • Subject → The one doing the action
  • Verb → The action word
  • Complement/Object → The one receiving the action

4. Examples and Explanations

Example 1: Kamau arakama ng'ombe. Translation: Kamau is milking the cow.

  • Subject: Kamau
  • Verb: arakama (is milking)
  • Complement/Object: ng'ombe (cow)

✅ The cow receives the action of milking.

Example 2: Wanjiru athomaga ibuku. Translation: Wanjiru reads a book.

  • Subject: Wanjiru
  • Verb: athomaga (reads)
  • Complement/Object: ibuku (book)

✅ The reading action is directed to the book.

Example 3: Mũrĩmi nĩaraka nyũmba. Translation: The farmer is building a house.

  • Subject: Mũrĩmi (farmer)
  • Verb: nĩaraka (is building)
  • Complement/Object: nyũmba (house)

✅ The house receives the building action.

Example 4: Mũhiki nĩaragũra mĩtumba. Translation: The bride is buying clothes.

  • Subject: Mũhiki (bride)
  • Verb: nĩaragũra (is buying)
  • Complement/Object: mĩtumba (clothes)

✅ The action of buying affects the clothes directly.

Example 5: Wambũi arahata thĩĩ. Translation: Wambũi is sweeping the floor.

  • Subject: Wambũi
  • Verb: arahata (is sweeping)
  • Complement/Object: thĩĩ (floor)

✅ The sweeping action directly affects the floor.

5. Syntactic Importance

  • In transitive sentences, the complement is necessary.
  • Without it, the meaning becomes incomplete.

Example:

  • Kamau arakama → incomplete (Kamau is milking what?)
  • Kamau arakama ng'ombe → complete (Kamau is milking the cow.)

✅ Complements help keep the sentence grammatically correct and meaningful.

6. Semantic Role of the Complement

Complements answer questions like:

  • "What is being done?"
  • "What does the action affect?"

They make the sentence clear, full, and specific. The meaning of the verb often depends on the complement.

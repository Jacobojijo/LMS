4.3.3 The Compliment of Terminal & Specification

1. The Complement of Terminality

Definition and Core Function

In Kikuyu grammar, a Complement of Terminality shows the end point or receiver of an action. It marks where or to whom the action is directed.

This complement tells us the destination or final receiver of what is being done — whether it is a person, a group, or a place

2. Structural Formula

Subject + Verb + Object + Preposition ("na" or "ni") + Terminal Complement (Receiver/Destination)

3. Key Features

  • The preposition "na" or sometimes "ni" connects the action to the receiver or destination.
  • Not all verbs need "na/ni." Some verbs already show direction or giving in their meaning.
  • This complement helps to make the action complete and clear.

4. Illustrative Examples and Explanation

Example 1:

Kamau arathomera ciana.

Translation: Kamau is reading to the children.

  • Subject: Kamau
  • Verb: arathomera (is reading to)
  • Object: — (book understood)
  • Preposition: na
  • Terminal Complement: ana (children)

✅ The reading action goes towards the children — they receive the effect of the action.

Example 2:

Wambũi arathioraga maitho na Wanjiru.

Translation: Wambũi was rolling/darting her eyes at Wanjiru.

  • Subject: Wambũi
  • Verb: arathioraga (was rolling)
  • Object: maitho (eyes)
  • Preposition: na
  • Terminal Complement: Wanjiru (receiver)

✅ The action (showing) ends with Wanjiru as the receiver.

Example 3:

Mũrĩmi nĩaria gĩkũyũ na atumia a thĩini.

Translation: The farmer is telling the women in the village a story.

  • Subject: Mũrĩmi (farmer)
  • Verb: nĩaria (is telling)
  • Object: gĩkũyũ (story)
  • Preposition: na
  • Terminal Complement: atumia a thĩini (village women)

✅ The story is directed to the women — they are the destination of the action.

Example 4:

Ndĩgũthiĩ kũrĩ nyina wa Wanjiku.

Translation: I am going to Wanjiku's mother.

  • Subject: ndĩ (I)
  • Verb: gũthiĩ (go)
  • Preposition: kũrĩ
  • Terminal Complement: nyina wa Wanjiku (destination)

✅ The verb "gũthiĩ" shows movement, and "na nyina wa Wanjiku" marks where the action ends.

5. Grammatical Insight

Some verbs like heire (give) or thomera (read to) already include a sense of direction or giving, so they may not always require "na."

Verbs like aria (tell), thomera (read to), or gũcokia (return to) often require "na" or "ni" when specifying the receiver.

Example:

Kamau aheire Wanjiku kĩheo → "Kamau gave Wanjiku a gift."

✅ Here, the receiver (Wanjiku) is implied without needing "na."

2. The Complement of Specification

Definition and Function

The Complement of Specification shows ownership, origin, or description. It tells who something belongs to or what characteristic it has. In Kikuyu, it is formed using the word "kwa" or "ga" — similar to "of" in English.

3. Structural Formula

Noun (Head) + kwa/ga + Specifier (Owner, Source, or Characteristic)

4. Features

  • The word "kwa" means "of" or "belonging to."
  • It shows possession, relationship, or description.
  • It can describe people, places, animals, or things.
  • "ga" may be used in certain idiomatic or descriptive expressions (e.g., "gĩtĩ ga mũhĩrĩga" – the seat of the clan).

5. Illustrative Examples and Explanation

Example 1:

Mũthoni wa Kamau.

Translation: Mũthoni of Kamau / Kamau's daughter.

✅ Shows possession — Mũthoni belongs to Kamau's family.

Example 2:

Nyũmba ya Mũrĩmi.

Translation: The house of the farmer.

✅ Shows ownership — the house belongs to the farmer.

Example 3:

Mĩtĩ ya mũgũnda.

Translation: The trees of the farm.

✅ Indicates origin or location — the trees grow on the farm.

Example 4:

Ngũgĩ ya mbura.

Translation: The clouds of the rain.

✅ Shows natural association — clouds come with rain.

Example 5:

Ngoro ya mũndũ.

Translation: The heart of a person.

✅ Shows body part belonging to someone.

Example 6:

Mũgambo wa thũmbĩ.

Translation: The song of the birds.

✅ Shows natural source — the song comes from birds.

6. Comparative Notes: "Kwa" vs "Ga"

Form Use Example Meaning
kwa General possession / belonging nyũmba kwa Kamau Kamau's house
ga Characteristic / cultural or group association gĩtĩ ga mũhĩrĩga the clan's seat

Both "kwa" and "ga" show relationship, ownership, or origin, depending on the context.

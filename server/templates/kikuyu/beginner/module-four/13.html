4.8.1 Summary of Compliments, Negation & Question Tags

A. Complement of Material

This complement shows the material or substance used to perform an action.

In Kikuyu, this complement often appears after a verb of making, building, cooking, or repairing.

Structure

Verb + Object + na + Material

Examples

Mami arutaga mokaro na ũcũmbĩ.

(Mother cooks ugali with flour.)

Baba akoraga nyũmba na matũmbĩ.

(Father builds houses with bricks.)

Mwarũ wa nyina akũnyitaga itara na maguta.

(The girl braids hair with oil.)

B. Complement of Purpose

This complement expresses the intention or purpose of an action.

Kikuyu normally uses kũ + verb to express purpose.

Structure

Verb + Object + kũ + Verb (purpose)

Examples

Ndũkũrũga mũthemba kũheana ũhoro.

(I take the phone to give information.)

Nĩngathondeka gĩkoni kũtonga cũgũ.

(I make a kiln to fire pots.)

Mũhunjia angĩa igũrũ kũhoya.

(The priest goes up to pray.)

C. Complement of Company

This complement shows the person(s) accompanying the subject.

Kikuyu uses na.

Structure

Verb + na + Person

Examples

Ndathiĩ cukuru na Mũriũ.

(I go to school with my son.)

Akahũũra gĩkoni na ciana ciake.

(She cleaned the kitchen with her children.)

Twatheeranĩrĩe na arata atũ.

(We agreed together with our friends.)

D. Complement of Argument

This complement expresses the reason for disagreement or the point of debate.

Kikuyu uses gũkonia na / kũrĩa depending on whether it is a disagreement or a point of contention.

Structure

Verb + na + Issue (to argue about)

Verb + kũrĩa + Issue (topic focus)

Examples

Marekithanĩrĩe na mũno gũkonia na wendo wacĩo.

(They argued a lot about their relationship.)

Atũũrũ a thĩinĩ mekithanaga kũrĩa mĩtugo ya tũrathai.

(The villagers debate about cultural practices.)

Twarutire gũkonia na hinya wa thirikari.

(We discussed the authority of government.)

E. Complement of Aim

This complement shows the larger goal behind an action, different from immediate purpose.

Common Kikuyu markers are tũngĩkorwo, tũhoya, kũmutaitha depending on context.

Structure

Verb + Object + (marker) + Aim phrase

Examples

Ndũrathimithĩra mwarimũ tũngĩkorwo nĩ kũmenya wendo wake.

(I visit the teacher so that I may understand his intentions.)

Atongoria mekũhunjia andũ tũhoya atĩ makorwo o na wendo mũno.

(Leaders instruct people in hopes that they become responsible.)

Mũciarĩ akenaga ciana kũmutaitha gũcokia ũguo.

(A parent advises children aiming to correct behaviour.)

F. Complement of Cause

This complement expresses the cause or reason behind an event.

Kikuyu uses tũngĩkorwo, nĩ kũrĩ, or nĩ ũndũ.

Structure

Verb + (cause marker) + Phrase

Examples

Mũrata wakwa ndĩrathii tũngĩkorwo nĩ ũndũ wa mĩtharaba.

(My friend did not come because of floods.)

Mwari akahũũra gĩkoni nĩ ũndũ wa athoni.

(She cleaned the kitchen because guests were coming.)

Ng'ombe ikĩrũgama nĩ kũrĩ ũtonga mũno.

(The cow fell because it was very tired.)

G. Complement of Locality

This complement expresses the place where an action happens.

Kikuyu uses locative markers: kũ, ha, mũ, gũ.

Structure

Verb + Locative marker + Noun

Examples

Andũ makinya ha riiko.

(People gather at the fireplace.)

Twarutire gũkena kũ mũgunda.

(We enjoyed ourselves in the farm.)

Ndirikũrathima mũ nyumba.

(I am sweeping inside the house.)

H. Comparative Overview of Complement Types

Complement Type Kikuyu Marker(s) Typical Function
Material na Substance used
Purpose kũ + verb Immediate intention
Company na Accompanying persons
Argument na / kũrĩa Disagreement or topic of dispute
Aim tũngĩkorwo / tũhoya Larger overarching goal
Cause nĩ ũndũ / nĩ kũrĩ Why something happened
Locality kũ / ha / mũ / gũ Where an action happens

I. Negation & Affirmation — Summary

1. Present Negative (Simple)

Marker: ti- / tĩ- / ndĩti- (1st person)

Examples:

  • Tĩ ndĩgũthiĩ. (I am not going.)
  • Ti arehe. (He is not giving.)

2. Past Negative

Marker: nd- + a (past base)

  • Ndinathii. (I did not go.)
  • Ndakaragia. (I did not cook.)

3. Future Negative

Marker: ndingĩ- / tutingĩ- / ndingĩkw-

  • Ndingĩthiĩ. (I will not go.)
  • Tutingĩcooka. (We will not return.)

4. Polite Negative

Marker: ũtikũ… / ũtigũ… / mũtigũ…

  • Ũtikũcooka hũu. (Please do not come back here.)
  • Mũtigũgaya andũ. (Please don't disturb people.)

J. Question Tags — Summary

Kikuyu uses two main types:

1. Expecting Confirmation (Yes-leaning)

Use: nĩ ũcio? / nĩ ũguo?

Ndũrathii cukuru, nĩ ũcio?

(You are going to school, right?)

2. Expecting Correction (No-leaning)

Use negative form + atĩa?

Tĩ ũrathii, atĩa?

(You are not going, are you?)

3. Polite/Soft Question Tags

Use: ĩno? / wendo?

Ngwenda gũcooka, wendo?

(I want to return, okay?)

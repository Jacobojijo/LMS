4.15.1 Introductions

In Kikuyu grammar, mood shows a verb's function in a sentence, indicating the speaker's attitude or intention toward the action or state. There are three main moods: indicative, imperative, and subjunctive.

Indicative Mood

Expresses a fact or statement, either as a declaration or question.

Most commonly used in everyday speech.

  • Example: "Njau ĩrarĩra." – The calf is crying.
  • Example: "Wenda kũnyua kahua?" – Do you like/want coffee?

Imperative Mood

Expresses a command or request, usually implying "you" as the subject.

  • Example: "Hingũra mũrango." – Open the door.
  • Example: "Teithia mũndũ ũyũ." – Please help this person.

Subjunctive Mood

Expresses a wish, hypothetical situation, suggestion, or condition contrary to fact.

Less common in daily speech, more common in formal writing.

  • Example: "Ndĩyũrĩtie nĩnjũragĩre kuo." – I wish I could run there.
  • Example: "Wangĩkorirwo nĩ we, ndũngĩĩkire ũguo." – If I were you, I would not do that.
  • Example: "Nĩndĩrahe ũhoro mũndũ ũcio aume." – I suggest that the man leave.

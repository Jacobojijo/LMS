4.15.2 Indicative Mood

I. Definition

The Indicative Mood expresses facts, real events, and certainties across three time frames:

  • Past – what has already happened
  • Present – what is happening now
  • Future – what will happen

This mood is the default mode used in narration, reporting, and stating truths.

II. Structural Overview

Verbs are regular in form, allowing predictable conjugation.

Temporal distinction is marked by auxiliary particles:

  • Past Tense Marker: {-a-} or {-ire} (e.g., {nĩ-na-} or nĩ-nĩr-})
  • Present Continuous Tense Marker: {-ra-}
  • Future Tense Marker: {-gũ-}

V. Morphological Notes

  • Past tense uses the marker {-a-} or the suffix {-ire} on the verb root.
  • Present tense (Continuous) uses the marker {-ra-}.
  • Future tense uses the marker {-gũ-}.

Insight: The future tense uses {-gũ-}, which is linked to the concept of gũka (to come), reflecting the Kikuyu conceptualization of time as approaching events.

4.3.4 The Compliment of Agent & Origin

1. The Complement of Agent

Definition and Function

The Complement of Agent shows who performed an action in a sentence written in passive voice.

It answers the question:

"The action was done by who?"

In Kikuyu, this complement is introduced using the preposition "na" (by).

Structural Formula

Subject (Passive) + Passive Verb + na + Agent (doer of the action)

Key Features

  • The subject receives the action, instead of doing it.
  • The agent (the doer) comes after the verb.
  • The word "na" marks the performer of the action.

Illustrative Examples and Explanation

Example 1:

Gĩthaka gĩciarĩtwo na ng'ombe. The shamba was ploughed by a cow.

  • Subject (receiver): Gĩthaka (shamba)
  • Passive verb: gĩciarĩtwo (was ploughed)
  • Agent (doer): na ng'ombe (by a cow)

✅ "ng'ombe" is introduced by na, marking the agent.

Example 2:

Itũũra rĩrũngwo na mũbarũti. The village was fenced by the carpenter.

  • Subject: Itũũra (village)
  • Passive verb: rĩrũngwo (was fenced)
  • Agent: na mũbarũti (by the carpenter)

✅ Shows who performed the action.

Example 3:

Gĩciaro gĩteithirwo na mũthuri. The ceremony was assisted by the man.

  • Subject: Gĩciaro (ceremony)
  • Verb: gĩteithirwo (was assisted/helped)
  • Agent: na mũthuri (by the man)

✅ The subject receives action; the agent performs it.

2. The Complement of Origin

Definition and Function

The Complement of Origin shows where a person comes from or their clan/community identity.

It expresses:

  • Place of origin
  • Family or lineage connection
  • Community identity

In Kikuyu, this is expressed using "wa" / "mũrũ wa" / "mwarĩ wa" depending on gender and relationship.

Structural Formula

Subject + Copula (nĩ) + Origin Marker (mũrũ wa / mwarĩ wa / mũciĩ wa) + Place / Person

Morphological Characteristics

Origin Marker Meaning / Use Example
mũrũ wa male origin (son of / man from) mũrũ wa Nyeri
mwarĩ wa female origin (daughter of / woman from) mwarĩ wa Karatina
wa neutral origin / belonging to wa Gĩkũyũ

Examples and Explanation

Example 1:

Nĩ mũrũ wa Nyeri. He is from Nyeri.

✅ Shows male origin.

Example 2:

Nĩ mwarĩ wa Karatina. She is from Karatina.

✅ Indicates female origin.

Example 3:

Tũrĩ a Gaki. We are from Gaki.

✅ Collective identity of a group.

Example 4:

Nĩ mũndũ wa Murang'a. He/she is a person from Murang'a.

✅ Gender-neutral origin marker.

Example 5:

Ciana nicia Mũhĩrĩga wa Agaciku. The children belong to the Agaciku clan.

✅ Clan-based identity.

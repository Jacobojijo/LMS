4.15.4 Subjunctive Mood

I. Definition and Core Function of the Subjunctive Mood in Kikuyu 🇰🇪

The Mũtarĩkio wa Kĩhote (Subjunctive Mood) in Kikuyu refers to verb forms and constructions used to express:

  • Non-factual or non-actual events
  • Ideas not yet realized (Mĩkĩro ĩtaanĩkĩrĩra)
  • Events that exist as wishes (Kũrũrĩkana), hopes (Kĩrĩrĩrĩria), intentions (Wĩra), or possibilities (Kĩhote)
  • Politeness (Ũhoro wa ũkarani) (softened statements or requests)
  • Probability / Possibility (Kũhoteana) ("might", "may", "can")
  • Desire / Longing (Wendi)
  • Supplication (pleading, blessing, praying) (Ihooya)
  • Exhortation (encouraging, urging) (Kũhũmbĩra)
  • Conditional intention or purpose (Wĩra wa ũndũ)

Although Kikuyu does not mark subjunctive with a distinct suffix like Dholuo, it uses markers, tone, and structural patterns to signal non-indicative meaning.

II. Common Contexts for Subjunctive Usage in Kikuyu

A. Expressions of Possibility or Probability

Kikuyu uses "oyũ" (it may be), "ũka" (you may), "ũũgũ" (it may be), "ndingĩ…" (I might/could not), and Modal Verbs to indicate "may / might / can / could."

Kikuyu Example English Meaning Core Function
Kũringana oyũ kũnyũa. It may rain. Marks events that might occur, not guaranteed (ũndũ ũtaamemana).
Tũndingĩ gũthiĩ rũciũ. We may go tomorrow. Marks events that might occur, not guaranteed (ũndũ ũtaamemana).

B. Polite Requests and Inquiries (Mũtarĩkio wa Ũkarani)

Politeness in Kikuyu emerges through:

  • Softeners: "ndakũhoya" (I ask you), "ndakũingĩra" (I beg you), "ndakũigua" (I hear you)
  • Infinitive desire markers: "ũka…" (you may...), "woka…" (you may...), "ndingatiga…" (I wouldn't stop...)
  • Purpose forms with "gũkũ" or "gũũ": expresses intent or polite possibility.
Kikuyu Example English Meaning
Ndakũhoya ũka gũndũkũra. I kindly ask that you help me.
Ũka ũnjĩre wega. May you please explain well.

C. Prayers, Blessings, and Pleadings

High-frequency subjunctive in Kikuyu appears in Blessings (Irathimo), Invocations (Ciũria), Pleadings (Maingĩro), and Ritual or ceremonial speech.

Kikuyu Example English Meaning Core Function
Ngai akũhe wega. May God give you goodness. Expresses hope + desire + reverence (Wendi + Kĩgĩna + Gĩtĩĩo).
Ngai akenyuuke kũhoya kwanyu. May God answer your prayer. Expresses hope + desire + reverence (Wendi + Kĩgĩna + Gĩtĩĩo).

III. Imperative and Tone-Based Duality in Kikuyu

Kikuyu depends heavily on Tone (Wĩra wa Mũgambo). Identical verb forms can shift meaning by tone alone:

  • Firm tone = strong command (Watho mũnene)
  • Soft tone = polite request (subjunctive-like)
Example Sharp Tone Soft Tone
Thoma! Command: "Read!" Polite prompting: "Please read."

Tone controls force.

IV. Imperative Verb Forms in Kikuyu

Kikuyu imperatives often:

  • Drop the prefix (stem form)
  • Use short, sharp form for commands
  • Use softened stem for polite exhortation
Kikuyu Command English Meaning
Ria! Eat!
Thoma! Read!
Thi! Go!
Hoya! Pray!
Andĩka! Write!

Tone determines harshness or softness, not morphology alone.

V. The Kikuyu Equivalent of "A-n-A Imperative Future Pattern"

Kikuyu uses Future-Imperative Constructions (Mĩhianĩre ya Watho wa Hĩndĩ Ĩgũũka), often:

  • Subject prefix + ka/ũka + verb (Soft future command)
  • Subject + no gũthiĩ / no gũkora / no gũciarũra (Determined future action)
Kikuyu Example English Meaning Tone/Function
Ũka ũthiĩ. You will go. Instructional tone.
Tũka tũthome. We shall study. Subjunctive-like encouragement.

VI. Subjunctive Verb Morphology in Kikuyu

Kikuyu expresses subjunctive using:

A. Infinitive Subjunctive

Marker: gũ-

Usage: Expresses purpose, desire, or intention.

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
Ndĩrenda gũkũnyua. I want to drink.
Nĩwega ũka gũtũigua. It is good that you listen to us.

B. Softened Imperative/Desiderative Forms

Usage: Expresses "that I may…" or "that you may…"

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
Ndingĩkorania ũgũkora? Could you possibly do it?
Ndĩrenda ũka ũnjĩre. I would like you to explain.

C. Modal Subjunctive Markers

Used for probability, conditional intention, weakened requests, and hypothetical action.

Common markers: ndingĩ… (I could / I might), ũka… (you may / you might), no… (future intention or conditional future).

VII. Subjunctive with Pronoun + Verb Purpose Forms

When adding pronoun complements, Kikuyu maintains structure:

Kikuyu Form English Meaning
ũnjĩre tell me
ũnjũke lift me
ũtũhoyere pray for us
ũnjũngĩrĩre explain to me

Subjunctive-like meaning appears in instructions, polite direction, and purpose.

Example: Ndakũhoya ũnjũngĩrĩre gũkũ. (I ask that you explain this to me.)

VIII. Core Functions and Use-Cases of the Subjunctive (Kikuyu)

A. Expressing Purpose or Intended Outcome

Often marked with gũ- or ũka…

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
Hĩwa mai gũnyũe. Give me water that I may drink.
Ũnjĩre gũkũ nĩgeetha ndĩmenye. Tell me this so that I may know.

B. Expressing Necessity or Obligation

Often through "no", "nĩ kũringana", or "nĩ uria".

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
No ũkorwo ũka ũkũrũgama. You ought to stand.
Nĩkũringana ũkorwo ũhoya. It is necessary that you pray.

C. Polite Commands

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
Wĩhoke tondũ Ngai nĩarathima. Be patient that God may bless you.
Tũriange! Let us eat!

D. Compound Instructions

Multiple linked actions functioning as one purposeful sequence.

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
Thi ũmosie mami. Go greet your mother.
Ũnjĩre na ũgĩcinie wĩra. Explain and help finish the work.

E. Negative Subjunctive Patterns

Marked with "tikanĩ…" or "ndingat…"

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
Tikanĩthiĩ. Do not go (plural/group).
Ndingathoma rũciũ. I may not study tomorrow.

IX. Potential / Hypothetical Marker Equivalent to Dholuo "d-"

Kikuyu uses: ndingĩ…, ndingat…, and ningĩ… (contextual usage).

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
Ndingĩkorwo ndirenda kũkũhoya. Could it be that I want to ask you?
Ndingathii rũciũ. I might go tomorrow.

X. Subjunctive After Obligational Expressions

Kikuyu equivalents include: Nĩkũringana… (It is necessary that…), Nĩ uria… (It is required that…), and No ũkorwo… (You ought to…).

Kikuyu Example English Meaning
Nĩkũringana ũka ũkũrũgama. You ought to wake up.
No ũkorwo ũhoya. It is required that you pray.

XI. Subjunctive in Negation and Future Tense

A. Negative Future

Ndingathiĩ rũciũ. (I will not / may not go tomorrow.)

B. Negative Present

Ndĩrenda gũthiĩ. (I do not want to go.)

C. Non-immediate Future

Rũciũ no tũkũgũrũra. (Tomorrow we shall return.)

Umũthĩ no ngũgũrũra. (Sometime today I will return.)

XII. Summary of Structural Markers in Kikuyu Subjunctive

Marker / Structure Function Context of Use
ndingĩ- Possibility / Hypothetical Polite requests, probability
gũ- (Infinitive) Purpose / Intention Desire, outcome-based action
ũka- Polite direction, softened command Urging, encouragement
no… Future intention, obligation Exhortation, softened command
Tone Determines force Command vs. Polite request
Infinitive Sequencing Purpose clause Linking actions

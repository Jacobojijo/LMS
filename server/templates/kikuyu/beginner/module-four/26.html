4.16.2 Reading Comprehension

Story 1: Mũthuri na Ng'ombe yake (The Old Man and His Cow)

Mũthuri witũ, wetagwo Njoroge, aarĩ na ng'ombe nene, njega mũno. Ng'ombe ĩno yarĩ na rũĩo rũrĩ kĩharo na yarĩ na mũkũyũ mũingĩ. Kĩroko kĩmwe, Njoroge nĩambĩrĩirie kũrĩa mũkũyũ ũcio. Arĩa na kũrĩa, na mũkũyũ ũrĩa mũingĩ ũgĩtheria. Mũthenya ũcio, Njoroge agĩũkĩra gũthondeka nyũmba yake. Mũciĩ wake, wetagwo Wanjiku, agĩcoka kũhoya mũthuri ũcio nĩkĩ arĩĩte mũkũyũ wothe. Njoroge agĩcookia atĩ arĩire tũndũ gũtirĩ kindũ kĩngĩ gĩakurĩa. Wanjiku ndaarutire ũndũ ũcio wega. Agĩcooka akĩmũheereire atĩ athiĩ na agũrũre kĩrĩa kĩa ng'ombe, nĩũndũ ng'ombe ndĩragũgũra kĩrĩa kĩayo. Njoroge nĩatigire gũthodeka nyũmba na agĩthiĩ kũruta wĩra ũcio wa ng'ombe.

Gĩkũyũ Word English Meaning
Mũthuri Old man
Ng'ombe Cow
Mũkũyũ Fodder / Hay
Mũciĩ Wife (lit. of the home)
Aarĩa He ate (Past Tense)
Gũthondeka To fix / To tend to
Ũhoro Matter / Issue

Comprehension Questions

  1. Tense Identification: In which tense is the majority of the story? (Past simple)
  2. Paragraph 1 – Verb Analysis: Identify the root, subject, and tense of the verb agĩcoka.

    Answer: Root: coka (return); Subject: Wanjiku (Wife); Tense: Consecutive Past.

  3. Paragraph 1 – Events and Meanings: Why did Wanjiku tell Njoroge to get the cow's food?
  4. Interpretation Questions: What lesson about responsibilities can we learn from Wanjiku's reaction?
  5. Language Use: Identify one noun (kĩharo, rũĩo) and one adverb (mũno) from the story.

Story 2: Nyamũ na Thaka nene (The Small Animal and the Big Bush/Wilderness)

Nyamũ nĩ nyamũ nini, ĩikaraga thaka-inĩ nene mũno. Mũthenya ũmwe wa kũgĩa riũa mũingĩ, Nyamũ nĩoya mũthũkũ mũno mũrĩgũrũ wa mũgũnda. Nyamũ nĩoya gũthondeka nyũmba ĩngĩ. Nyũmba ĩno yarĩ ya gũkũgũra hĩndĩ ya riũa rĩrĩa inene. Nyamũ nĩeya kũhoya nyũmba ya gĩthaka kĩrĩa kĩnene mũno, nĩkĩ nĩeya kũgĩa michĩmi mĩingĩ gũkũ.

Nyamũ nĩamĩrĩire kũgũrũra na mũciĩ wake, to nyamũ nĩeya kũgĩa ũhoro wake wĩkĩra. Nyamũ nĩoya kũthondeka na gĩthaka mũno kũhoya michĩmi ya nyama. Nyamũ nĩeya kũgĩa michĩmi mĩingĩ, nĩeya kũgĩa wĩra mũingĩ kũruta. Nyamũ nĩathire gĩthaka-inĩ, kĩrĩa kĩrĩa gĩkoragwo na ndawa cia mũingĩ. Nyamũ nĩoya gũgĩa mũingĩ mũno. Nyamũ nĩoya gũthiĩ mũthũkũ ũcio mũrĩgũrũ. Nyamũ nĩoya kũgĩa wĩra mũingĩ mũno.

(Nyamũ is a small animal that lives in a very large wilderness. One very sunny day, Nyamũ found a big thicket/bush at the edge of the farm. Nyamũ wanted to fix another house. This house was for shelter during the intense heat. Nyamũ wanted to find a house in the vast wilderness because he wanted to get a lot of forest products there.)

(Nyamũ then returned to his home, but Nyamũ had his work to do. Nyamũ wanted to tend to the big bush to find many wild fruits. Nyamũ was going to get many wild fruits, and he was going to have much work to do. Nyamũ went to the bush, where there were many medicinal herbs. Nyamũ wanted to have much food. Nyamũ went to that thicket. Nyamũ was going to have a lot of work.)

Gĩkũyũ Word English Meaning
Gĩthaka / Thaka Wilderness / Bush
Nyamũ Animal / Small creature
Mũthũkũ Thicket / Small bush
Nyũmba House / Shelter
Michĩmi Wild fruits / Forest products
Ndawa Medicine / Herbs
Riũa Sun / Sunshine

Comprehension Questions

  1. Where did Nyamũ live, and where did he decide to build a second shelter?
  2. What two things was Nyamũ seeking in the big wilderness (thaka nene)?
  3. Why was Nyamũ going to have a lot of work (wĩra mũingĩ)?
  4. What lesson does this story teach about preparedness or resources?
  5. Identify two nouns in the Locative case (showing place, e.g., -inĩ).

Story 3: Muthoni na Nguyu (The Young Man and the Pigeon)

Mũthoni arĩ gũthiĩ mũkũyũ kũrĩa. Nĩathire gũthoma igũrũ. Mũthenya ũcio, nĩarĩire irio cĩake cĩa mũciĩ. Nĩoya gũcoka nyũmba yake. Nĩoya gũthiĩ gũthondeka nyũmba yake. Ngũyũ nĩrĩa kĩrĩa kĩake kĩrĩa kĩa gũthiĩ.

Exercise Questions

  1. What was the first thing the Young Man (Mũthoni) was going to do?
  2. Where was he going to read?
  3. Who ate food (kĩrĩa)?
  4. In the final sentence, what is the action or verb that the pigeon's food was doing? (Interpreting the odd phrase: kĩrĩa kĩa gũthiĩ).
  5. What did the Young Man want to tend to?

Sample Answers

  1. He was going to eat in the field.
  2. He went to read igũrũ (above/up).
  3. Both the Young Man and the Pigeon ate food.
  4. The food was gũthiĩ (going).
  5. He wanted to tend to his house (nyũmba yake).

4.11.1 Introductions

I. Conceptualizing Value: "Thogora" as a Core Economic Lexeme

In Gĩkũyũ, thogora refers to the price or monetary value assigned to an item.

  • It is used in both formal and informal market exchanges.
  • Central to buyer-seller interactions, reflecting social etiquette, fairness, and negotiation skills.

II. Asking for the Price: Penjo Thogora — Functional Expressions of Inquiry

A. Standard Inquiry Construction

The common, polite way to ask the price:

Thogora ya X nĩ kĩ?

– What is the price of X? or

{X nĩ ya mbeca cigana?}

– X is for how much money?

  • Nĩ kĩ literally means "is what?" (implying "what is the price?").
  • Cigana means "how many/much" and is attached to the noun mbeca (money).

B. Applied Examples of Thogora Inquiries

Gĩkũyũ Expression English Translation
Thogora ya mbũri nĩ kĩ? What is the price of a goat?
Thogora ya mũkĩ nĩ kĩ? What is the price of a sugarcane?
Thogora ya nduma nĩ kĩ? What is the price of a banana?

Note: The item always comes before the price question to emphasize the object being valued.

C. Alternative Syntax: Emphasizing Money or Value

Another common way emphasizes money (mbeca) or the overall price (thogora):

Gĩkũyũ Expression English Translation
Mbũri nĩ mbeca cigana? How much money is the goat?
Nduma nĩ mbeca cigana? How much money is the banana?

This approach puts focus on the value/amount of money (mbeca), not just the item. (The incorrect word mũnene has been replaced with the appropriate structure using mbeca cigana).

III. Goyo Thogora: The Art of Price Negotiation

Goyo thogora means bargaining or negotiating the price.

  • Kikuyu markets traditionally value negotiation as part of polite economic practice.
  • Key aspects: Expressing surprise or questioning the initial price, politely requesting a lower, more reasonable price, maintaining mutual respect and preserving social harmony.
  • Negotiation is seen as a ritualized social interaction, not confrontation.

Example Dialogue:

Buyer: Thogora nĩ ya igana mũno. Tũrehera hĩndĩ nini.

(The price is very big/high. Bring us a little down/give us a small discount.)

Seller: Tũkũmĩnyarĩria kĩrĩa kĩa ũhoro. Nĩ wendo waku.

(I will only lower it a little. It is for your sake/because of your kindness.)

IV. Linguistic & Cultural Insight

Asking and negotiating price shows pragmatic competence in Gĩkũyũ.

  • Penjo thogora expressions reflect: Market literacy and economic awareness, social etiquette and politeness embedded in commerce, and communication skills in balancing fairness, respect, and negotiation.

V. Summary: The Economics of Expression

Gĩkũyũ uses two main syntactic patterns:

  • Item-focused (Price): Thogora ya X nĩ kĩ?
  • Value-focused (Money): X nĩ mbeca cigana?

Goyo thogora is a culturally rich tradition emphasizing:

  • Value sensitivity
  • Respectful negotiation
  • Economic literacy and social intelligence

4.5.2 The Compliment of Company & Argument

A. The Complement of Company

Definition and Functional Role

Indicates companionship — someone is doing an action together with another person.

  • Shows who is accompanying who.
  • It does not make the companion a grammatical subject.
  • In Kikuyu, this is marked by the preposition: "na" = with (NOT "and" in this usage).

Structural Formula

Subject + Verb + Destination/Action + "na" + Companion

Core Grammatical Insight

  • The preposition "na" can mean "and" or "with", but in this structure, it means WITH (companionship).
  • It emphasizes shared action, not listing.

Illustrative Examples

Kikuyu Sentence Literal Meaning Notes
Ndĩ gũcokia gĩcũgũ na Wairimu. I am returning the bag with Wairimu. na Wairimu = she is accompanying me.
Tũthii kũgũtũra na Baba. Let's go settle with Father. Shows companionship, not two subjects.
Kĩmwana gĩathiire rũũĩ na Muthoni. The boy went to the river with Muthoni. Emphasizes joint presence.

"na" here shows your companion — not a compound subject.

Summary Insight

This complement expresses companionship during an action.

"na" = WITH (company) Not "and" in this context.

B. The Complement of Argument (Kũheana wendo wa Gũcokia / Gũtũma kĩaro)

Definition & Semantic Role

Shows the topic or subject matter someone is speaking or thinking about.

Answers: "About what?"

In Kikuyu, this is marked by the preposition:

"kũrĩ" = about / concerning

Structural Formula

Subject + Speech Verb + kũrĩ + Topic

Key Lexical Feature

"kũrĩ" expresses the topic of discussion:

  • about
  • concerning
  • on

Examples

Kikuyu Sentence Literal Meaning Notes
Mũthuri ũcio arathomithia kũrĩ thirikari. That man is teaching about government. Topic of speech = thirikari.
Tũrathomithania kũrĩ kũng'ũra nduriri. We are discussing about peace. Connects discussion to topic.
Nĩarũgũĩte o na ciana kũrĩ gĩtũmi kĩa gũthera mũthĩnji. She explained to the children about saving money. kũrĩ anchors topic of explanation.

Functional Summary

  • "kũrĩ" attaches the argument/topic to a verb of speech or thought.
  • Situates the topic as the focus of communication.

"kũrĩ" = ABOUT (topic of discussion)

C. Comparative Table: Company vs Argument Complements

Complement Type Marker (Preposition) Semantic Role Meaning in English Verb Context
Company na Companionship / presence with Movement / action verbs
Argument kũrĩ Topic of speech / thought about, concerning Speaking / thinking verbs

D. Cultural and Linguistic Implications

  • Company complements (na) show togetherness and communal participation, reflecting Kikuyu social values.
  • Argument complements (kũrĩ) show discussion, learning, and collective decision making, important in Kikuyu cultural forums (gũcokia kĩaro).

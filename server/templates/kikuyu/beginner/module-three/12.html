3.6.1.2 The Objective Pronoun

1. Introduction to Objective Pronouns in Kikuyu

Definition

  • Objective pronouns in Kikuyu represent the receiver of an action performed by the subject.
  • They correspond to the syntactic object in a sentence (direct or indirect).

Core Function

  • They are morphologically integrated within the verbal complex.
  • They specify who or what receives the action expressed by the verb.
  • Unlike free-standing pronouns in English (e.g., him, her, them), in Kikuyu they are infixal morphemes within the verb structure.

Position in the Verb

  • They appear as infixes, inserted between the subject prefix and the verb root.
  • Thus, the verb encodes subject + object + action in a single compact form.

Canonical Format

Subject Prefix + Object Infix + Verb Stem

Illustrative Example

Ngũhe → "I give you"

  • N- = I (subject)
  • -gũ- = you (object)
  • -he = give (verb root)

2. Personal Object Pronouns in Kikuyu

Object Pronoun Infix Form Meaning / Reference
-ĩ / -a -ĩ / -a me
-gũ -gũ you (singular)
-mũ -mũ him / her
-tũ -tũ us
-mũ -mũ you (plural)
-ma -ma them

Observations

  • Several infixes serve both singular and plural forms (-mũ).
  • The pronoun inventory is minimalist but highly functional, as context disambiguates meaning.
  • Each pronoun is a bound morpheme that cannot occur independently of a verb.

3. Verb Structure: Conjugation of Transitive Verbs

General Rule

Transitive verbs in Kikuyu are conjugated in two main parts:

  • Prefix = subject marker (the doer).
  • Infix = object marker (the receiver).

Structural Representation

Subject Prefix + Object Infix + Verb Stem

Illustrative Example with kũhe ("to give")

  • Ngũhe = I give you
  • Ũmũhe = You give him/her
  • Atũhe = He gives us
  • Matũhe = They give us

Observation

  • Every conjugated verb carries triple encoding: subject, object, and action.
  • This results in linguistic compactness: one Kikuyu verb is equivalent to a full English clause.

4. Conjugation Examples with kũhe (to give)

Conjugated Verb Morphological Structure Translation
Ngũhe N-gũ-he I give you
Ũmũhe Ũ-mũ-he You give him/her
Atũhe A-tũ-he He gives us
Matũhe Ma-tũ-he They give us
Tũmũhe Tũ-mũ-he We give him/her
Mũmahe Mũ-ma-he You (pl.) give them

Pedagogical Note

  • These forms demonstrate subject–object agreement within a single word.
  • Learners must train to identify the infix slot for the object.

5. Case Study: Verb kũhoya (to ask / pray / request)

Conjugated Verb Morphological Structure Translation
Ngũhoya N-gũ-hoya I ask you
Ũmũhoye Ũ-mũ-hoya You ask him/her
Tũmahoye Tũ-ma-hoye We ask them
Amũhoye A-mũ-hoya He asks you (plural)
Matũhoye Ma-tũ-hoya They ask us

Observation

  • Verbs of communication and request also require precise object pronoun placement.
  • The infix position is invariant across different verb roots, reinforcing predictability.

6. Syntax Summary: Subject–Verb–Object Alignment

  • Kikuyu verbs encode syntactic alignment within one compact form.
  • Canonical ordering in conjugated verbs:

    Subject Prefix + Object Infix + Verb Root

  • This means:
    • Subject, Object, and Verb are grammatically fused.
    • The verb is a self-contained clause.

Example

Ngũhe → "I give you"

Encodes: subject = I + object = you + action = give.

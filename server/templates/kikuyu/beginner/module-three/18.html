3.8.4 Past Tense

1. Simple Past Tense (Hĩndĩ yaarĩ)

1.1. Definition and Function

  • Expresses an action fully completed in the past, with no continuity into the present.
  • Marks bounded time reference → the event started and ended before speech time.
  • Similar to English "I ate," "She went," etc.

1.2. Structure

  • Formula: Nĩ- (affirmative prefix) + Subject Prefix + Verb Stem + Past Ending (-ire/-ĩre/-ete)
  • The choice of verb ending depends on the phonological class of the verb root.

1.3. Examples

Kikuyu Sentence Structure English Translation
Nĩndarĩire mũgate. Nĩnd- (I) + arĩ (eat) + -ire I ate the bread.
Nĩũrĩĩre irio. Nĩũ- (you) + rĩĩ (eat) + -re You ate the food.
Nĩtũrĩĩte nyũngũ. Nĩtũ- (we) + rĩĩ (eat) + -te We have eaten the pot (fig. → suffered).
Nĩmũnyuĩte maĩ. Nĩmũ- (you pl.) + rĩĩ (drink) + -te You (pl.) drank water.
Nĩmarĩĩte nyama. Nĩma- (they) + rĩĩ (eat) + -te They ate meat.

1.4. Verb Ending Variations

Verb Root Ending Past Ending Used Example Verb Past Conjugation
-a -ire kũruga (to cook) Nĩndarugire (I cooked).
-ĩ -ĩire kũrĩra (to cry) Nĩndarĩrire (I cried).
-e -ere kũrũgema (to grind) Nĩndarũgemere (I ground).

2. Immediate Past Tense (-ire ending with Nĩ-)

2.1. Function

  • Indicates an action just completed a short while ago.
  • Expresses recency.
  • Equivalent to English "I just did …".

2.2. Examples

  • Nĩndahũrire ngũi. → I hit the dog.
  • Nĩũheire mũndũ ũcio mbũri. → You gave that person a goat.

3. Extended Past Tense (-ete ending)

3.1. Function

  • Expresses a past event with present relevance.
  • Equivalent to English present perfect ("I have eaten").
  • Emphasizes completed action + current state/result.

3.2. Examples

  • Nĩndaĩia irio. → I have eaten food.
  • Nĩakĩra rũĩ. → He/She has crossed the river.

4. Habitual Past Tense (-aga suffix with Nĩ-)

4.1. Definition

  • Describes repeated or regular actions in the past.
  • Equivalent to English "used to …".

4.2. Structure

  • Nĩ- + Subject Prefix + Verb Stem + -aga
  • Time reference is clarified by adverbials (tene = long ago, hĩndĩ yaarĩ = in the past).

4.3. Examples

  • Nĩndarũgamaga tene. → I used to stand long ago.
  • Nĩndathiaga thoko tene. → I used to go to the market in the past.

5. Past Continuous Tense

5.1. Definition

  • Expresses an action that was ongoing at a specific time in the past.
  • Equivalent to English "was/were doing."

5.2. Structure

  • Nĩ- + Subject Prefix + -rĩ- (progressive infix) + Verb Stem + -aga (habitual suffix)
  • Shows continuous + durative past action.

5.3. Examples

  • Nĩndarugaga irio hĩndĩ yaarĩ. → I was cooking food in the past.
  • Nĩkũrima mũgũnda mwarĩmaga hĩndĩ ĩyo. → You (pl.) were digging in the farm at that time.

6. Morphological Observations

Agglutinative Nature:

  • Kikuyu past tense is built through layered affixes (prefixes, infixes, suffixes).

Key Markers:

  • Nĩ- = affirmative marker for past.
  • -ire/-ĩre = immediate past endings.
  • -ete = extended past (perfect).
  • -aga = habitual/continuous marker.

Precision:

  • Kikuyu distinguishes recency, duration, repetition, and continuity in past actions.
  • English often collapses these into fewer forms, but Kikuyu maintains fine-grained temporal accuracy.

3.4.1 Introduction to Verbs in Kikuyu

1. Basic Form of Kikuyu Verbs

Infinitive Marker:

  • All Kikuyu verbs in their infinitive form begin with kũ-.
  • This marker serves as the default verbal prefix that identifies the word as a verb.

Examples (infinitives):

  • kũrĩa → to eat
  • kũhoya → to pray
  • kũmenya → to know
  • kũhanda → to plant
  • kũhitha → to hide

Contrastive Note:

Unlike Dholuo verbs, which often end in -o, Kikuyu infinitives consistently begin with kũ-.

2. Structure of the Verb Phrase

Kikuyu verb phrases may expand into complex syntactic units, incorporating:

  • Nouns (objects, complements)
  • Adjectives (qualifiers)
  • Adverbs (manner, degree, time)
  • Prepositional markers (locative or relational elements)

Illustrative Example:

Kũhanda mbembe igũrũ gĩcigo kĩa mũgũnda ũcio wa gĩthaka.

→ "Planting maize in the upper part of the fertile farm."

3. Verbal Extensions in Kikuyu

Kikuyu verbs expand morphologically to modify meaning.

Key verbal extensions include:

Extension Function Example Gloss
Applicative Indicates doing something for or with kũandĩkĩra to write for (someone)
Causative Indicates making someone else perform an action kũnyuithia to cause to drink
Reflexive Indicates doing something to oneself kũhitha to hide something
Reciprocal Indicates mutual action kũhoyanĩra to pray for each other
Stative Indicates a passive or resultant state kũrĩo to be eaten

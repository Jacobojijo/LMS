3.8.1 Introduction to Kikuyu Tenses

1. Definition and Core Function

Tenses in Kikuyu grammar:

  • Indicate the temporal placement of an action or event.
  • Enable speakers to situate verbs in past, present, or future frames of reference.

Grammatical Encoding:

  • Expressed through prefixes, infixes, and suffixes attached to verb roots.
  • A single Kikuyu verb often encodes multiple layers of meaning: subject, tense, aspect, and affirmation.

2. The Three Core Tenses in Kikuyu

Tense Definition Kikuyu Phrase Function/Usage
Present Describes actions happening now, habitual actions, or timeless facts Gwĩkĩka rĩu (to happen now) Expresses immediacy (I am reading), routine (I usually read), or general truth (Water is life).
Past Refers to actions or events that already occurred Gwĩkĩkire hĩndĩ yaarĩ (to happen before) Narrates completed actions or states (He went yesterday).
Future Refers to actions/events that will occur later Nĩgwĩkĩka na kĩambĩrĩria (it will happen with beginning) Projects forthcoming events (We will travel tomorrow).

3. Structural Characteristics of Kikuyu Tense

Morphological Encoding:

  • Prefixes → usually encode affirmation (Nĩ-) and subject agreement.
  • Infixes → often carry tense-aspect markers (-ra-, -rĩ-, -ire, -ka-).
  • Suffixes → frequently encode habitual (-aga) or perfective endings (-ire).

Agglutinative Nature:

  • One verb form integrates time, subject, affirmation, and aspect.
  • Example: Nĩndĩrathoma = Nĩ- (affirmation) + ndĩ- (I) + -ra- (present) + thoma (read).

3.6.1 Transitive Verbs

1. Defining Transitive Verbs

  • Core Definition:
    • Transitive verbs are verbs that take a direct object.
    • The object receives the action performed by the subject.
  • Test for Transitivity:
    • Ask the question: Verb + What/Who?
    • If the verb requires an answer, it is transitive.
      • He ate what? → Food.
      • Ngui yarumĩte kĩ? → Mbũri.
  • Structural Position:
    • Kikuyu follows Subject–Verb–Object (SVO) order.
    • The direct object normally follows immediately after the verb.

2. Structural Characteristics of Transitive Verbs

  • Verb–Subject–Object (SVO) Order:
    • Typical word order is: Subject + Verb + Object.
    • E.g., Ngui yarumĩte mbũri → "The dog chased the goat."
  • Subject Prefixes:
    • All verbs carry prefixes marking the subject (the agent).
  • Object Markers (Infixes):
    • Objects may be encoded directly inside the verb as infixes.
    • These enables Kikuyu verbs to "pack" subject, action, and object into one word.

3. Illustrative Examples of Transitive Verbs

1. Ngui yarumĩte mbũri.

  • Gloss: Dog chased goat.
  • Question: Ngui yarumĩte kĩ? → Mbũri.
  • Translation: "The dog chased the goat."

2. Nĩ ndĩire irio.

  • Gloss: I ate food.
  • Question: Ndirĩa nĩ kĩ? → Irio.
  • Translation: "I ate food."

3. Ũhe mwana gĩthoomo.

  • Gloss: You give child education.
  • Question: Ũhe mwana kĩ? → Gĩthoomo.
  • Translation: "Give the child education."

4. Tũhũũrũkĩte mũciĩ.

  • Gloss: We rested home.
  • Question: Tũhũũrũkĩte kũ? → Mũciĩ.
  • Translation: "We are resting at home."

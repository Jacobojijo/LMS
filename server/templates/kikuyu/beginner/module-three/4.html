3.1.4 Adjectives & their Plural

Adjectives in Kikuyu are not static lexical items; rather, they are dynamic morphological units that undergo systematic agreement with the noun class system. This agreement manifests especially in number (singular vs. plural) and in some cases in semantic domain (e.g., size, quality, social status, or evaluation).

1. General Rule of Pluralization

Kikuyu adjectives agree morphologically with the noun they qualify.

Agreement follows the Bantu noun-class prefix system.

General pattern:

  • Singular adjectives: typically begin with mũ- (human, animate, personal) or gĩ- (inanimate, objects).
  • Plural adjectives: shift to a-, i-, or ma-, depending on the plural marker of the noun class.

Examples

Mũndũ mũthĩĩni → Andũ athĩĩni ("A poor person → Poor people")

Gĩti gĩkũrũ → Mĩti mikũrũ ("An old chair → Old chairs")

2. Morphological Transformation: Singular vs. Plural Adjectives

Singular Form Plural Form Gloss/Meaning
mũnene anene big
mũthĩĩni athĩĩni poor
mũũru oru bad
mũtune atune brown/red
gĩkũrũ mikũrũ old
mũkuhĩ akuhĩ short
kĩrĩa kĩa thamaki marĩa ma thamaki valuable/precious

kĩhĩũ mahĩũ hot

Observation:

  • The prefix alternation is the main mechanism for pluralization.
  • Note how the same adjective root shifts its surface form depending on the class prefix.

3. Concordial Agreement in Plural Forms

When a noun pluralizes, its adjective obligatorily pluralizes.

The two elements (noun + adjective) are syntactically and morphologically bound.

Illustrative Examples

Mũndũ mũnene → Andũ anene (A big person → Big people)

Mwarĩ mũũru → Awarĩ aũru (A bad girl → Bad girls)

Mũtĩ mũkuhĩ → Mĩti mĩkuhĩ(A short tree → Short trees)

Key Point:

Plural agreement is not optional; it is an obligatory morphosyntactic feature in Kikuyu grammar.

4. Adjectives That Do Not Change in the Plural

Not all Kikuyu adjectives inflect. Certain adjectives are morphologically invariable, regardless of the noun's number.

Common invariable adjectives:

  • Colors: ngirini (green), gakarakara (yellow)
  • Tastes/Feelings: thaka (sweet), ndũrũ (bitter), ngagatu(sour)

Examples:

Thani ya ngirini → Mathani ma ngirini (A green plate → Green plates)

Njaũ thaka → Njaũ thaka (A beautiful calf → beautiful calves)

Observation:

Color terms and taste descriptors are semantically stable adjectives, and hence do not morphologically adapt to plurality.

5. Semantic Categories of Adjective Agreement

Adjectives in Kikuyu fall into two major morphosyntactic categories:

Category Examples Plural Behavior
Inflecting adjectives mũnene → anene, mũũru → oru, gĩthukĩ→ ithuki, gĩkũo → ikũo Must change in plural
Non-inflecting adjectives ngirini (green), thaka (beautiful), ndũrũ (bitter), ngagatu(sour) Remain unchanged

6. Notes on Usage and Syntax

  • Kikuyu syntax demands strict agreement between nouns and their modifying adjectives.
  • Both elements (noun + adjective) shift in parallel when plurality is marked.
  • This reflects the deeply integrated concord system characteristic of Bantu languages.

8. Linguistic Implications

The Kikuyu system of adjective pluralization reveals:

  • Prefix agreement is central to adjective formation.
  • Some adjectives always inflect (mũnene → anene), while others are inherently invariable (ngirini = green).
  • This reflects the wider Bantu typology, where noun class agreement governs adjectives, pronouns, and demonstratives.

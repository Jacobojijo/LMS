3.8.6 Future Tense & Future Continuous Tense

1. General Overview of Future Tense in Kikuyu

Core definition:

The Kikuyu future tense expresses actions/events that will occur after the moment of speaking.

Mechanism of marking:

  • It is expressed through the auxiliary verb "gũ" (to go).
  • This auxiliary carries subject agreement and tense marking.

Functional equivalence:

Semantically corresponds to English "will/shall".

Main verb behavior:

The main verb remains in the infinitive form (kũ-).

Illustrations:

  • Nĩngũrĩa → "I will eat."
  • Nĩagũthii → "He/She will go."

Key teaching anchor:

Always remind learners: "In Kikuyu, the future comes with gũ."

2. Primary Structure of Immediate Future Tense

Construction Pattern:

Subject prefix + gũ (future auxiliary) + verb infinitive
  • Infinitive verb is not altered.
  • The auxiliary gũ alone conjugates for subject agreement.

3. Morphological Structure of the gũ Construction

Origin:

The auxiliary gũ is derived from gũthii ("to go").

Subject agreement:

Inflected to match subject prefixes.

3.1 Subject + gũ pattern

Person Form Illustration
1st sg. nĩngũ Nĩ ngũcooka → "I will go back."
2nd sg. nĩũgũ Nĩũkũona → "You will see."
3rd sg. nĩagũ Nĩagũthii → "He/She will go."
1st pl. nĩtũgũ Nĩtũgũcooka → "We will return."
2nd pl. nĩmũgũ Nĩmũkũona → "You (pl.) will see."
3rd pl. nĩmagũ Nĩmekũruga irio → "They will cook food."

4. Contracted Forms

In spoken Kikuyu, the auxiliary often fuses with the main verb.

Examples:

  • Nĩngũkũrĩa → Nĩkũrĩa ("I'll eat")
  • Nĩũgũkũrĩa → Nĩũkũrĩa ("You'll eat")
  • Nĩagũthii remains as is ("He'll go")

Pedagogical reminder: learners must avoid mistaking contracted forms for new independent verbs. They remain future tense forms.

5. Future of "To Be"

Kikuyu expresses future possession or existence with:

gũ + kũgũcokia (to have/be with)

Examples:

Nĩngũgũcokia ana arĩa igĩrĩ. → "I will have two children."

Literal: "I will be with two children."

Key principle: Kikuyu equates having with being-with.

6. Alternate Expressions of Future

6.1 Immediate or Imminent Future

Nĩngũcooka rĩu. → "I am about to return now."

6.2 Near Future (Later Today)

Nĩngũcooka mũthenya. → "I will return later today."

6.3 Tomorrow (rũciũ)

Nĩngacooka rũciũ. → "I will return tomorrow."

6.4 Indefinite Future

Nĩngũcooka ihinda ĩngĩ. → "I will return sometime in the future."

7. Future Continuous Tense

Construction:

gũ + ga + verb

Function: expresses ongoing actions in the future.

7.1 Examples

Nĩngũka thii gũkũrora. → "I will be going to watch."

Nĩmagũka kũrĩa irio. → "They will be eating food."

Interpretive note: signals process-in-progress at a future reference time.

8. Expressing Obligation (Mũhaka Construction)

Obligation, necessity, or duty expressed using mũhaka ("must/should").

8.1 Examples

Nomũhaka ũrĩe. → "You must eat."

Nomũhaka tũone. → "We must see."

9. Syntactic Observations

  • The main verb remains infinitive (kũ- root).
  • The auxiliary gũ is the bearer of subject agreement and tense.
  • Temporal adverbs provide precision:
    • rũciũ ("tomorrow"),
    • ũmũthenya ("later today"),
    • rĩu ("now, immediately"),
    • hĩndĩ ĩngĩ ("another time").

10. Pedagogical Notes for Learners

10.1 Common Pitfalls

  • Error 1: Conjugating the main verb instead of gũ.
  • Error 2: Treating contracted forms (Nĩkũrĩa) as independent verbs.
  • Error 3: Confusing gũthii (lexical "to go") with auxiliary gũ (future marker).

10.2 Instructional Emphasis

  • Drill the pattern: Nĩ + subject + gũ + infinitive verb.
  • Practice both full and contracted forms to avoid fossilized errors.
  • Anchor actions in time using adverbs (rũciũ, ũmũthenya, hĩndĩ ĩngĩ, rĩu).
  • Use storytelling prompts: e.g.,
    • "Nĩngũ… mbere ya rũciũ." ("I will … before tomorrow.")
    • Encourages sequencing future events.

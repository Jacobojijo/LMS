2.1.2 Provenance of the Abagusii

Introduction

Provenance, or the place of origin of an individual, plays a significant role in identity and interpersonal reference among the Abagusii. This study explores how Ekegusii encodes regional and national origins through specific morphological structures, particularly through the use of the prefixes "Oyo" and "Omo"

Morphology of Provenance Markers in Ekegusii

1. The Prefix "Oyo"

  • Function: "Oyo" is a locative-derived noun prefix used to indicate a person from a place or belonging to a group, region, or country. It can apply to both males and general population references.
  • Example:
    • Oyo Bobasi – a person from Gem Bobasi
    • Oyorogoro – a Kenyan (male or general reference) A person from the upper side

2. The Prefix "Omo" (Singular, Feminine Use)

  • Function: "omo" is a feminine derivational prefix indicating a (The relationship with a Country or family) woman or girl from a particular region or group.
  • Example:
    • Omokenya – a woman from Seme A Kenyan
    • Omoamate – A relative

Nationality and Ethnic References

In Ekegusii, nationalities and ethnicities are expressed by affixing "Oyo" to the name of a country or ethnic group. The plural is formed by replacing "Aba" with "Oyo", applicable to groups of people.

The flexible use of "Omo-" with both countries and ethnicities illustrates Ekegusii's productive nominal morphology and its strong emphasis on locational and group identity.

Gender and Socio-cultural Dimensions

The morphological distinction between "Oyo" and "Omo" not only reflects grammatical gender but also encodes cultural expectations and social organization.

Plural Forms and Social Reference

Pluralization in provenance terms is straightforward and systematic:

  • "Omo" (singular) becomes "Aba" (plural).
  • This applies to people of a country, region, or ethnic group.

Examples:

  • Abakisumu – People from Kisumu
  • Abakenya – Kenyans
  • Abagere – People from Asembo Luo
  • Abaganda – Ugenya Uganda community

This makes Ekegusii ideal for sociolinguistic studies in plural marking, ethnonymic referencing, and identity signaling.

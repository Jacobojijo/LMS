2.5.1 Introduction

Introduction to Ekegusii Pronouns

Definition: Pronouns in Ekegusii are words used to replace nouns in order to avoid repetition and simplify speech.

Types of Pronouns:

  • Personal Pronouns – Refer to specific persons or things (e.g., I, you, he, we, they).
  • Possessive Pronouns – Show ownership or possession (e.g., mine, yours, theirs).
  • Demonstrative Pronouns – Point to specific things (e.g., this, that).
  • Interrogative Pronouns – Used to ask questions (e.g., who, what, which).
  • Relative Pronouns – Link clauses and refer to nouns mentioned previously.

Personal Pronoun Examples (Subject Form):

  • Inche – I
  • Aye – You (singular)
  • Ere – He/She/It
  • Intwe – We
  • Inwe – You (plural)
  • Barabwo – They

Object and Possessive Forms: Often implied or context-driven, but can be formed using suffixes or word placement.

Pronoun Use:

  • Subject pronouns are often incorporated into verbs (e.g., aye – You Ngocha tore – We will come).
  • Emphasis can be added by stating the pronoun explicitly before the verb (e.g., Inche ngochande – I will come).

Interrogative Pronouns (examples):

  • Ning'o – Who
  • Kereki – What
  • Aseki – Why
  • Rireri – When
  • Arari – Where

2.5.2 Personal pronouns

1. What Are Personal Pronouns?

  • Personal pronouns refer to specific persons or groups in conversation.
  • In Ekegusii, they are often used, but can be dropped when the verb form or context makes the subject clear.
  • Used to show who is performing the action — e.g., "I eat", "He speaks", etc.

2. The Six Core Personal Pronouns in Ekegusii

Dholuo English Equivalent Usage Example Sentence
an I / me 1st person singular Inche ninka inde → I am at home
in you (singular) 2nd person singular Aye ninka ore → You are at home
en he / she 3rd person singular Ere ninka are → He/She is in the house
wan we / us 1st person plural Intwe ninka tore → We are in the land
un you (plural) 2nd person plural Inwe monsi imore ase omoyega → You (all) are at the ceremony
gin they / them 3rd person plural Barabwo ninka bare → They are at home

Note: Gender-neutral — "en" applies to both he and she.

3. Demonstratives and Personal Pronouns Together

Demonstratives (Inche, Barabwo, Ere) pair with personal pronouns to refer to people or things precisely.

Phrase Meaning
Inche nince ere I, this (as in "I am the one")
Nere ere oria He/She is that one (far)
Nabwo baria They are that/those ones (near you)

4. Structure of a Simple Sentence

Common structure in Ekegusii when using personal pronouns: Subject Pronoun + Verb (+ Location/Object/Demonstrative)

Examples:

  • Inche genda inka → I am go home.
  • Bono gwachire→ You are coming now.
  • Ere nabo akuruga→ He/She can cook.
  • Nigo toisaransete insaa → We are sitting here.
  • Inwe mwensi karua igaa → You (all) leave that place
  • Ngosoa bare nyomba→ They are entering the house

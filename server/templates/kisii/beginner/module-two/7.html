2.3.1 Abstract & Concrete Nouns

In Ekegusii grammar, nouns are categorized into concrete nouns and abstract nouns, much like in other languages, to distinguish between things we can physically perceive (concrete) and those that exist as ideas or concepts (abstract).

1. Concrete Nouns

Definition: Concrete nouns are tangible objects, people, places, or things that we can perceive using our five senses (sight, hearing, touch, smell, and taste). They are physical and can be directly experienced.

Examples of Concrete Nouns in Ekegusii:

Person:

  • Omworokia (teacher)
  • Omosani (friend)
  • Omwana (child)

Animal:

  • Esese (dog)
  • Ekebusi (cat)
  • Enchogu (elephant)

Place:

  • Egetii (field)
  • Omochie (city)
  • Esukuru (school)

Thing:

  • Ekerogo (chair)
  • Egetabu (book)
  • Egari (car)

Material:

  • Chinko (wood)
  • Echuma (steel)

Example Sentences:

"I saw the dog playing in the city."

Naroche esese egochiesa ase omochie

"She sat on the chair and opened her book."

Aisarasente ase ekerogo naende akaigora egetabu

2. Abstract Nouns

Definition: Abstract nouns refer to ideas, emotions, qualities, concepts, or states of being that cannot be perceived with the senses. They represent intangible things like feelings, thoughts, and philosophical ideas.

Examples of Abstract Nouns in Ekegusii:

Emotions:

  • Omogoko (happiness)
  • Okobea (sadness)
  • Obororo (anger)
  • Obwoba (fear)

Qualities:

  • Obuya (beauty)
  • Obokanyeku (humility)
  • Chinguru (strength)
  • Oboene (honesty)

Ideas:

  • Obwanchani (love)
  • Obosibore (freedom)

Conditions:

  • Obochenu (health)
  • Obotaka (poverty)
  • Omorembe (peace)

Example Sentences:

"His happiness was evident after the good news."

Omogoko oye nigo ororekanete nyuma yamang'ana amayia.

"Justice is important in every society."

Oboene nobuya ase kera ekenyoro

Key Points:

Concrete Nouns:

  • Physical: They refer to tangible things that can be touched, seen, smelled, heard, or tasted.
  • Examples: Omworokia (teacher), esese (dog), Omochie (city), Ekerogo (chair), Oroko (wood)
  • Perception: These nouns are perceptible through the senses, making them easy to visualize or interact with.

Abstract Nouns:

  • Intangible: These represent ideas, qualities, or emotions that are not perceivable by the senses.
  • Examples: Omogoko (happiness), Obokanyeku (humility), Obwanchani (love), Obochenu (health), Omorembe (peace)
  • Mental/Emotional States: Abstract nouns often describe internal experiences, concepts, or conditions.

Further Explanation with Examples:

Concrete Nouns:

A concrete noun is something you can touch, see, or interact with directly.

  • Omworokia (teacher): You can see, speak with, and interact with a teacher.
  • Esese (dog): A dog is a living being that can be seen, touched, and heard.
  • Omochie (city): A city is a physical place you can visit, see buildings, roads, and people.

Abstract Nouns:

An abstract noun describes something intangible, something you cannot directly interact with through the senses.

  • Omogoko (happiness): You can feel happiness, but you cannot see or touch it.
  • Okobea (sadness): This is an emotional state that you experience internally.
  • Obosibore (freedom): A concept or condition, not something you can physically touch, but something you can experience mentally or emotionally.

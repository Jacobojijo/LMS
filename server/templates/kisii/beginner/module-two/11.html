2.3.5 Noun Construct

1. What is a Noun Construct?

A noun construct is a special form of a noun used:

  • To attach suffixes for demonstratives or possessives
  • To modify or describe nouns more precisely

Like regular nouns, noun constructs exist in singular and plural forms.

2. How to Form the Noun Construct

A. Regular Noun Construct Formation (Singular)

  1. Take the plural noun.
  2. Remove the final vowel to form the singular noun construct.

Examples:

English Singular Plural Singular Noun Construct
garden Egeticha Ebiticha Eget-
food Endagera Chindagera Chind-

B. Exception Cases

Some nouns don't follow the regular vowel-removal rule.

Irregular Construct Forms:

English Singular Plural Singular Noun Construct
house Enyomba Chinyomba Ch-
woman Omokungu Abakungu Aba-

C. Plural Noun Construct

  • Usually identical to the plural noun itself.
  • Sometimes, the form changes slightly in exceptions.

Regular Example:

English Plural Noun Plural Construct
gardens Ebiticha Egeticha-
foods Chindagera Chindagera-

Irregular Example:

English Plural Noun Plural Construct
houses Chinyomba Chi-
women Abakungu Aba-

3. Uses of the Noun Construct

A. Demonstrative Descriptions

  • Used to express "this", "that (near)", "that (far)", etc.
  • Formed by adding a demonstrative suffix to the noun construct.

Demonstrative Suffixes:

Meaning Suffix
this -Eke
that (near) -Ekio
that (far) -Keria
these -Ebi
those (near) -Ebio
those (far) -Biria

Examples with "Enchera" (path):

Noun Construct Word Translation
-eye Enchera eye this path
-eyio Enchera eyio that path (near)
-eria Enchera eria that path (far)
-echi Chinchera echi these paths
-echio Chinchera echio those paths (near)
-chiria Chinchera chiria those paths (far)

B. Possessive Constructions (Coming Later)

  • The noun construct is also the base to which possessive suffixes are added.
  • This is treated in more detail in the possessives section (to be covered separately).

4. Summary: Construct Formation Rules

Regular Formation (Singular Construct):

  • Start with the plural noun
  • Drop the final vowel
  • → Eng'ombe → chiombe-

Plural Construct Rule:

  • Most are identical to the plural noun
  • → Endagera yaye → Endagera yaye-
  • Some are modified exceptions
  • → eng'ombe → Chiombe-

5. Memory Tips and Tricks

Tip Type Example
Mnemonic Endagera yaye = food root → used in many compound words
Visual Think of "-e, -o, -chi" as "closer → farther"
Cultural Link Eng'ombe- = "cow" prefix seen in many Gusii names and phrases

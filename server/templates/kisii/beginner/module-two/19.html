2.8.1 Introduction

In Ekegusii grammar, an adjective is a word that describes or modifies a noun or pronoun. It provides additional information about the qualities, characteristics, or attributes of the noun or pronoun it modifies. For example, in the sentence "The big dog barked," Esese enene iyakogete the word "big"- enene' is an adjective describing the noun "dog". Esese

Here's a more detailed explanation:

  • Modifies Nouns and Pronouns:
    Adjectives don't stand alone; they always describe or provide more information about a noun or pronoun.
  • Provides Specific Details:
    Adjectives help paint a clearer picture by adding details about the noun, such as its size, color, shape, origin, or other qualities.
  • Examples:
    • "A red apple" (color)
    • "A tall building" (size)
    • "A beautiful sunset" (beauty)
    • "A broken window" (state)
  • Common Types of Adjectives:
    • Descriptive Adjectives: Describe the qualities of the noun (e.g., "big," "blue," "old," "happy").
    • Comparative Adjectives: Compare two things (e.g., "taller," "smarter," "better").
    • Superlative Adjectives: Compare something to everything else in a group (e.g., "tallest," "smartest," "best").
    • Demonstrative Adjectives: Refer to a specific noun (e.g., "this," "that," "these," "those").
    • Possessive Adjectives: Indicate ownership (e.g., "my," "your," "his," "her," "its," "our," "their").
    • Interrogative Adjectives: Used in questions (e.g., "what," "which," "whose").
    • Proper Adjectives: Formed from proper nouns (e.g., "French," "American")

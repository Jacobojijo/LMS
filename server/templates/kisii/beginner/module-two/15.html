2.5.4 Demonstrative pronouns

1. What are Demonstrative Pronouns?

  • Used to point to or refer to specific nouns, like English this, that, these, those.
  • Indicate proximity (near or far) in relation to the speaker or listener.

2. Singular Demonstratives

Ekegusii English Equivalent Usage Context Example
Eke this Near the speaker Eke negetanda → This is a bed
Ekio that Near the listener Ekio nekerogo → That is my chair
Keria that over there Far from both speaker and listener Oria n'omorisia gochia aria → That is a herdsman over there

3. Plural Demonstratives

Ekegusii English Equivalent Usage Context Example
Aba these Near the speaker Aba nabamura→ These are boys
Ebio those (near) Near the listener Ebio mbirebi → What are those?
Baria those (far away) Far from both Baria nabarisia → Those are herdsmen
Note: "Barabwo" may also mean "snatch from them" depending on pronunciation/stress.

4. Demonstratives in Questions

Used to inquire about identity or description:

  • Eke ninki? → What is this?
  • Ebio mbiribi? → What are those?
  • Keeria ninki? → What is that (far over there)?

5. Demonstratives with Nouns

Used before or after nouns to specify them:

  • Eye n'enka → This is a home
  • Eye n'enyancha → This is a lake
  • Oro n'oroche → This is a river
  • Eye nechikoni yane → This is my hat

6. Demonstratives + Possessive Pronouns

Demonstratives can combine with possessives for added specificity.

Ekegusii Phrase Translation
Eke nekerogo kiane This is my chair
Eye n'engoko yane This is my hen
Echio n'echiombe chiago Those are your cows
Echi n'echisese chiago These are your (2nd person singular) dogs

"-eke" is the demonstrative suffix meaning "this".

  • Omonto oyo→ This person
  • Esese eye → This dog
  • Eng'ombe eye → This cow
  • Ekerogo eke→ This chair

Ekegusii English

  • Enyomba eye → This house

Bonus Tips

-oyo suffix = "this"

Combine with any noun for specific meaning:

  • Ekerogo eke → This chair
  • Endani eyei → This pond
  • Omonto oyo → This person
  • Enka eye→ This home

4.7.1 Introductions

1. General Characteristics of Ekegusii Questions

  • Ekegusii questions frequently employ the subjunctive tense, especially in seeking intentions or preferences.
  • Interrogatives rely on a rich inventory of question words, each targeting specific informational domains such as identity, location, quantity, and cause.

2. Essential Interrogative Vocabulary

Function Ekegusii Term Translation / Use
How Inaki In what manner?
How many Nirenga Numerical inquiry
Why/How Naseki Cause or reasoning

3. Interrogative Sentence Structures: Form and Function

Object-focused Interrogatives:

  • Ekio ninki? — What is that?
  • Eke ninki? — What is this? (For objects)

Person-focused Interrogatives:

  • Ning'o ore ase ekerasi? — Who is in class?
  • Oyo ning'o? — Who is this?

Name Inquiry:

Erieta riago ndireri? — What is your name?

Preference Inquiry with Subjunctive:

  • Endagera ereri oranche? — Which food would you like?
  • Endagera oranche nereri? — (Lit: Food which you would like?)

Plural Object Inquiry:

Mbirogo ki oanchete? — Which chairs do you like?

Location-based Interrogatives with "gochia":

  • Ngai ebirogo bire? — Where are the books?
  • Ngai ekaramu ere? — Where is the pen?

Use of copula "gochia" indicates specific placement or attribution of objects.

  • Ebitabu ebire gochia aria — The books are over there.
  • Gochia aria — It's over there (Note: "gochia" dropped with pronouns like en)

4. Question Tags in Ekegusii: Confirmatory & Contradictory Extensions

  • Tags provide a means to confirm, challenge, or elicit validation from a conversational partner.
  • Structure: Main clause + tag (often with "Nabo" or "gose")
Example Translation
Nomomanyete ere, nabo? You know him, don't you?
Nomonyete omworokia, nao? You know the teacher, isn't that so?
Ngokenda ore gose tori? Are you going or not?
Onywete gose tonyweti? Are you drunk or not?
Kwamwaka ere, nabo? You beat him, didn't you?

Responses incorporate affirmation/negation particles:

  • Ee — Yes
  • Yaya — No
  • Gose — Not (appears before verb to negate)

5. Syntax of Negation within Interrogative Structures

  • "Ti-" as the negation particle, directly precedes the verb.
  • Changes verb to negative form in all relevant tenses:
  • Tindi kogendai — I am not going.
  • Inche timomanyeti ere — I do not know him.

Subjunctive negation examples:

  • Yaya, inche tindigocha — No, I will not come.
  • Yaya, ayetori gochakonyara — No, you will not manage.

6. The Role of "Naende" in Questions

Igo is a flexible interrogative particle used to:

  • Signal yes/no questions.
  • Emphasize inquiry tone.
  • Occasionally optional but helpful in framing direct questions.

Example:

Aye igo ngokenda ore gose torikogenda? — Will you go or not?

Conclusion: Functional Diversity in Ekegusii Questions

Ekegusii interrogatives reflect a highly nuanced grammatical system, combining:

  • Subjunctive mood for respectful inquiries and hypotheticals,
  • Morphosyntactic negation via "ti-",
  • Copulative structures for location queries,
  • Affirmation/Negation particles for response clarity,
  • and Tag questions for conversational cooperation.

This system ensures linguistic precision, contextual awareness, and a high degree of interpersonal sensitivity in communication.

4.3.4 The Compliment of Agent & Origin

1. The Complement of Agent

Definition and Function

  • The Complement of Agent specifies the doer of an action, particularly in passive constructions.
  • It answers the fundamental grammatical question: "Who does what?"
  • It typically follows the verb and is introduced by the preposition "na" (by).

2. Structural Formula

Subject (Passive) + Passive Verb + na + Agent

3. Key Features

  • Agent phrases appear in passive voice sentences to retain clarity on who performed the action.
  • The subject of the sentence is the receiver of the action, while the agent performs the action in the background.
  • The preposition "na" is the syntactic marker that signals agency in passive constructions.

4. Illustrative Examples and Linguistic Analysis

Omogondo oyo goika oremwe na eeri.

This garden has been ploughed by an ox

  • Omogondo oyo: Passive Subject
  • Gioka oremwe: Passive Verb (has been ploughed)
  • Na eeri': Agent Phrase (by an ox)

✧ The doer (eeri) is introduced using "na," clarifying who executed the action.

Endani eye eyaremetwe na ekeragita.

This pond was dug by a tractor

  • Endani eye: Subject (receiver of action)
  • eyaremetwe: Verb (was dug)
  • na ekeragita: Agent (by a tractor)

✧ Emphasizes machinery as an agent of change on the environment.

Rigena eri riabiarirwe na engoko.

This egg has been laid by a hen

  • Rigena eri: Subject
  • Riabiarirwe: Verb (has been laid)
  • Na engoko: Agent (by a hen)

4.6.1 Introductions

A. Purpose of the Chapter

  • Explores how to affirm and negate statements in Ekegusii.
  • Focuses on grammatical constructions, polite forms, and verb transformations used to express "Yes" (affirmation) and "No" (negation).
  • Introduces response strategies for yes/no questions, negative commands, and various tenses.

B. Core Vocabulary and Meaning

Expression Meaning Usage Context
Ee Yes (Affirmative) Polite/formal affirmation
Yaya No (Negative) Polite/formal negation
Ti- Not Negative particle before verb
Konya Not yet Past perfect negative particle
Ti- Don't Imperative/command negation

C. Affirmation Structures

1. Affirmative Responses:

  • Used to confirm or agree with a statement or question.
  • Forms: Ee

Example:

Nomomanyete? — Do you know him?

Ee — Yes

D. Negation Structures

1. Standard Negation with "ti-"

  • "Ti-" is placed before the verb to negate an action or state.

Example constructions:

  • Inchi tindi kogenda — I am not going
  • Inche timomanyeti — I do not know him
  • Teri bobe — It is not bad
  • Ere tari bobe — He/She is not bad

2. Response-Based Negation:

Combining Yaya + ti- + Verb for polite negative responses.

  • Yaya, inche tindigocha — No, I will not come
  • Yaya, aye torigochi konywoma — No, you will not manage
  • Yaya, inwe timokonyara — No, you (pl.) will not manage

3. Negative Contractions in the Past

Ti- (I did not...) can contract to Noka...

  • Ne ok achiemo Inche tindagereti — I did not eat
  • Nokachiemo Inche tindieti — I didn't eat

4. Present & Future Tense Negation

  • The verb takes the subjunctive form (final -o is dropped)
  • Future tense sometimes uses "tindigochi" instead of "tindigocha"
  • Inche tindikoragera — I am not eating
  • inche tindigochi koragera — I will not eat
  • Inche tindigocha koragera — I will not eat

5. Past Perfect Negation with "Konya"

Konya = contraction of konya + ti- (still not)

Inche Konya tindaragera — I have not yet eaten

6. Negative Imperative with "Tocha"

Tocha replaces "ti-" in command form.

Tocha — Don't come!

E. Pronoun-Negation Fusion

In responses, negation fuses into a single word unit:

  • Yaya, inche tingonyara — No, I won't manage
  • Yaya, aye tokonyara — No, you won't manage
  • Yaya, intwe titokonyara — No, we won't manage
  • Yaya, barabwo tibakonyara — No, they won't manage

4.3.2 Indirect Compliment

Definition and Grammatical Function

An indirect complement in Ekegusii represents a recipient or beneficiary of an action, rather than the direct object of the verb.

It defines a mediated relationship between the subject (agent) and the object (theme).

This structure reflects transfer, communication, or exchange, where the object is given, sent, or delivered to someone.

2. Structural Composition: Subject + Verb + Indirect Complement + Direct Object

General Pattern:

Subject → Verb → Recipient (Indirect Complement) → Thing Given (Direct Object)

The indirect complement answers the question: To whom? or For whom?

The direct object answers: What?

3. Key Characteristics

Dual-Object Construction: Involves both a direct object (the item) and an indirect object (the recipient).

The verb often implies transfer: verbs like oire (give), onyorire (receive/get), 'tebia (tell), etc.

Word order is critical in distinguishing who is giving/receiving and what is being given/received.

4. Illustrative Examples and Analyses

Nyabuto omoire Makori enyimbo.

Translation: Nyabuto gives Makori the stick

  • Subject: Nyabuto
  • Verb: omoire (give)
  • Indirect Complement: Makori (recipient)
  • Direct Object: enyimbo (stick)

✔ The action (giving) is directed at Makori, who receives the stick.

Inche namoete Makori riuko.

Translation: I gave Makori the letter

  • Subject: Inche- (I)
  • Verb: -Namoete (give)
  • Indirect Complement: Makori
  • Direct Object: riuko (letter)

✔ The action and possession are separated; the letter is transferred to Makori by me.

Omwana oire ng'ina egekabu.

Translation: The child gives the mother a basket

  • Subject: Omwana (child)
  • Verb: oire (gives)
  • Indirect Complement: ng'ina (his/her mother)
  • Direct Object: egekabu (basket)

✔ Emphasizes the social-relational layer — the child contributes to the mother.

Makori obwatire enyimbo korwa ase Nyabuto

Translation: Makori receives the stick from Nyabuto.

  • Subject: Makori
  • Verb: Obwatire (receives/takes)
  • Direct Object: enyimbo (stick)
  • Prepositional Indirect Complement: Korwa ase Nyabuto (from Nyabuto)

✔ The inverse of giving — Makori becomes the recipient, and Nyabuto becomes the source.

5. Semantic Functions of Indirect Complements

Recipient Role: Identifies the end-user or final destination of an action.

Beneficiary Role: Sometimes marks who benefits from an action, especially in dative constructions.

Source Role: In receptive constructions (obwatire), marks origin (korwa ase Nyabuto).

6. Morphosyntactic Considerations

Prepositional Phrase: When using receptive verbs (obwatire), the indirect complement is introduced by "korwa" (from).

Object Positioning: The indirect complement typically precedes the direct object when placed inside the verb clause (e.g., omoire Makori riuko).

Subject-Verb Concord: The subject is morphemically embedded in the verb prefix.

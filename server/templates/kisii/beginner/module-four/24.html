4.15.3 Subjunctive Mood

I. Definition and Core Function of the Subjunctive Mood

Subjunctive mood in Ekegusii expresses non-factual or non-indicative actions such as:

  • Politeness
  • Probability
  • Doubt
  • Desire
  • Supplication (prayer or pleading)
  • Exhortation (urging or encouraging)

II. Common Contexts for Subjunctive Usage

Expressions of Possibility/Probability

  • "May" or "Might" in English = "nabo" in Ekegusii
  • "Nabo" means "can/may/be able to" and is conjugated with the subject:
  • Nabo eratwe — It may rain.
  • Nabo tokogenda ankio — We may go tomorrow.
  • Narengeria ing'a nabo bagocha 'ngaki ende yonsi — I think they may come anytime.

Requests and Inquiries (Polite/Subjunctive Tone)

Ngoboria inde gose nongonye — I ask if you can help me.

Prayers and Pleadings (High Subjunctive Use)

Omonene, koranche, reta embura! — Lord, if You will, bring rain! (Subjunctive of divine pleading)

III. The Imperative and Its Tone-Based Duality

Tone-Dependent Mood Interpretation

Identical verb forms may imply:

  • A command if said in authoritative tone
  • A request if said in gentle or polite tone

Example:

Saba Tata oito ore igoro! — "Pray our Father in heaven!"

Can be both imperative or polite subjunctive, depending on tone

IV. Imperative Verb Forms

Derived by removing final vowel or with modified tones:

  • Endagera → Ragera! (Eat!)
  • Okorara → rara! (Sleep!)
  • Emeremo → kora! (Work!)
  • Okoruga → ruga! (Cook!)
  • Okorika → rika! (Write!)
  • Ogosaba → saba! (Pray!)

Command strength varies by tone, not only morphology

V. The "A-n-A" Imperative Future Pattern

  • Used to issue a strong command with future reference
  • Verb loses its final -o or -yo ending in imperative conjugation
  • Conjugated with both subject prefix and final vowel
  • Ana dhi Ngogenda inde! — Will I go!
  • Ini dhi Ngogenda ore! — Will you go!
  • Eno dhi Ngogenda are! — Will he/she go!
  • Wana dhi Ngogenda tore! — Will we go!
  • Unu dhi Ngogenda more! — Will you (pl) go!
  • Gini dhi Ngogenda bare! — Will they go!

Rule 1: Add -i in Some Verbs

  • Ogosibia → insibi — That I may wash
  • Ogosomi → Gosomi — That they may teach

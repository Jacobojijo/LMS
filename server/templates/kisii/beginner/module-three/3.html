3.1.3 Adjective of Color

In Ekegusii, adjectives denoting color are primarily formed through the prefixation of the particle "eke-" to the color base/root. This morphological process results in adjective forms that semantically function as descriptive modifiers of nouns, encapsulating visual characteristics.

1. Embariri → Chimbariri (Red)

Root Color Term: Rira — red

Adjectival Form: Embariri

Morphology: Eke- + bariri

Usage: Commonly used for items with red pigmentation.

Example:

Ekerogo ekebariri — "A red chair"

Risakara ribariri — "Red paper"

2. Emwamu → Chimwamu (Black)

Root: Mwamu— black

Adjectival Form: Emwamu'

Semantic Note: Also carries metaphorical connotation of darkness or obscurity.

Example:

Embori emwamu— "Black goat"

Egetambaa ekemwamu— "Black cloth"

3. Ekerabu → Chindabu (White)

Root: ndabu— white

Adjective: Endabu

Semantic Range: Symbolically linked with purity and cleanliness.

Example:

Rech maracha Enswe endabu — "White fish"

Ng'ato marachar Omonto omorabu — "A white person"

4. Enchembera → (Blue)

Root: Chabera — blue

Adjective: mabere

Usage: Describes objects or clothing with a blue hue.

Example:

Chianga mabere — "Blue clothes"

Ekerogo mabere — "Blue chair"

5. Nyamotobu → Motobu(Yellow)

Root Phrase: Toba — literally "the color of egg yolk"

Adjective: motobu

Semantic Origin: Culturally specific term derived from the yolk of chicken eggs.

Example:

Omote motobu' — "Yellow tree"

Eyanga nyamotobu — "Yellow garment"

6. Machani/bonyansi→ (Green)

Roots: Obonyansi — green

Adjective: Bonyansi

Usage: Often describes vegetation or items with a natural green shade.

Example:

Omote bonyansi — "Green tree"

Ching'eni machani — "Green vegetables"

7. Indigo → (Indigo)

Root: indigo (deep blue-violet)

Adjective: Indigo

Semantic Note: Used for dark hues, particularly in fabrics or dyes.

Example:

Chianga indigo — "Indigo clothing"

Ekerogo giakire indigo — "Indigo-painted chair"

8. Monyinga→ (Purple)

Root: 'Nyangi — purple

Adjective: monyinga

Usage: Often associated with royalty or rich tones.

Example:

Eyanga monyinga — "Purple dress"

Ebitabu monyinga — "Purple books"

9. Matonto/Makarabe → (Black and White)

Root: ritonto — black-and-white

Adjective: matonto

Semantic Complexity: Denotes a dual tone or patterned coloration.

Example:

Embori Matonto — "Black and white goat"

Eyanga matonto — "Checkered clothing"

10. Maraba→ (Brown)

Root: raba — brown

Adjective: maraba

Semantic Context: Used for natural or earthen tones, commonly for hair, skin, or soil.

Example:

Ekerogo maraba — "Brown chair"

Omonyaroka omorabu — "Brown-skinned girl"

General Observations

The prefix "omo-" in Ekegusii not only creates adjectival agreement but also transforms color roots into full adjectival forms, maintaining descriptive consistency in noun phrases.

Some colors (e.g.,Gesicha, Embariri, Makarabe) arise from cultural and environmental references.

The color lexicon in Ekegusii reflects both linguistic precision and cultural embodiment, with each adjective tightly bound to the semantic field of its referent.

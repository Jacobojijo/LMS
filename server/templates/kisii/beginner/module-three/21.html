3.8.7 Other Tenses

1. The "Should" Construction

Function: Expresses moral or expected obligation in the present or near future.

Structure:

Nabo/Kero kende (should) + Subject Prefix + Subjunctive Verb (often without final -o)

Subjunctive Marking: Often involves removal of the final -o in the verb stem.

Examples:

Inche nabo 'ngorika/ Kero kende indike → I should write

Nabo'ngorika (Contracted)

Nabo tokorika → We should write

Nabo'tokorika(Contracted)

2. The "May" Construction (Possibility/Permission)

Function: Expresses possibility, permission, or potential action.

Structure:

Nenyarekane (may) + Subject Prefix + Full Verb

Note: No subjunctive is applied here; the verb retains its full form including final -o.

Examples:

Nenyarekane indike → I may write

Nenyarekane'indike(Contracted)

Nenyarekane torike → We may write

Nenyarekane'torike (Contracted)

3. The "Would" Construction (Hypothetical or Conditional Past)

Function: Used to express hypothetical or conditional intentions/actions.

Structure:

Nango (would) + Subject Prefix + Verb (with final -o)

Contracted: The "Nango" merges with subject prefix.

Examples:

Nango inarika → I would write

Nango'narika (Contracted)

Nango intwe intwarika → We would write

Nango'intwarika (Contracted)

4. The "Let Me/Us" Imperative (Subjunctive Mood)

Function: Expresses a polite imperative or request, especially for oneself or a group including the speaker.

Structure:

Subject Prefix + Verb (Subjunctive)

No auxiliary used.

Examples:

Tiga indike → Let me write!

Tiga torike→ Let us write!

5. The "Should Have" Construction (Past Obligation)

Function: Expresses an action that was expected or required in the past but did not occur.

Structure:

Nabo (past marker) + nanga (should) + Subject Prefix + Subjunctive Verb

Contracted: "Nabo" + "nanga" + subject + verb merges into one unit.

Examples:

Nabo nanga narigete → I should have written

Nabo'narigete (Contracted)

Nabo nanga twarigete → We should have written

Nabo'twarigete (Contracted)

6. The "Had Done" Construction (Past Perfect)

Function: Expresses a completed action in the past before another past event.

Structure:

Nabo (past auxiliary) + Subject Perfective Marker + Verb

Subject Perfective Markers:

Nabo n-- (I had), Nabo t- (we had), etc.

Contracted: Full clause is phonetically merged.

Examples:

Nabo narigete → I had written

Nabo twarigete → We had written

7. The Causative Construction ("To make someone do something")

Function: Expresses coercion, instruction, or influence over another person's actions.

Structure:

Subject Prefix + namo(make) + Object Prefix + Verb

Negation: "Ok" negates the entire causative clause.

Examples:

Namokorire arike → I make him write

Twamokorire ere arike → We make him write

Twagokorire orike → We make you write

Tinamokora ere arike → I don't make him write

Ntoramokora ere arike → We don't make him write

8. Morphological Features Summary

  • Subjunctive mood often signaled by removal of final -o in the verb
  • Contracted forms are phonological compressions, not semantic shifts.
  • Modal auxiliaries like Nabo, Nenyarekane, Nanga are the primary mood markers.
  • "Nabo" marks past-tense or past perfect actions.

9. Syntactic Properties

  • Subject-verb agreement is marked by prefixes (i-, in-, etc.), while the verb typically remains uninflected in modal constructions.
  • Causative verbs follow a Subject + namo + Object + Verb model.
  • Contracted forms are common in both colloquial and fluent literary usage but require internal parsing to identify each component.

10. Pedagogical Recommendations

  • Subjunctive Practice: Learners should internalize the dropping of final -o for commands and obligations.
  • Contrastive Drills: Use side-by-side examples of full vs. contracted forms.
  • Mood Emphasis: Teach the subtle mood distinctions between "may," "should," "would," and their contracted/expanded versions.
  • Causative Mastery: Emphasize object marking in causative clauses.

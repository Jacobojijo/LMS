3.8.1 Introduction

In Ekegusii grammar, tenses indicate the time of an action or event, classifying verbs into past, present, and future forms. Each of these three main tenses is further divided into four aspects: simple, continuous, perfect, and perfect continuous. This results in a total of twelve distinct tenses.

Present Tense: Describes actions happening now, habitual actions, or facts.

Past Tense: Describes actions that have already occurred.

Future Tense: Describes actions that will happen in the future.

Within each main tense, there are four forms:

Simple: Expresses a general or habitual action.

Continuous (Progressive): Shows an ongoing action at a particular time.

Perfect: Indicates an action completed before a specific time or a past action with present relevance.

Perfect Continuous (Perfect Progressive): Describes an action that began in the past, continues until the present, or was completed just before a specific point in time.

Understanding tenses is crucial for accurate and effective communication in Ekegusii, ensuring that actions and events are described in the correct temporal context.

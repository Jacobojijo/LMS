4.6 Negation & Affirmation

4.6.1 Introductions

A. Purpose of the Chapter

Explores how to affirm and negate statements in Dholuo.

Focuses on grammatical constructions, polite forms, and verb transformations used to express "Yes" (affirmation) and "No" (negation).

Introduces response strategies for yes/no questions, negative commands, and various tenses.

B. Core Vocabulary and Meaning

Expression Meaning Usage Context
Kamano / Ee Yes (Affirmative) Polite/formal affirmation
Ooyo / Daa No (Negative) Polite/formal negation
Ok Not Negative particle before verb
Pok Not yet Past perfect negative particle
Kik Don't Imperative/command negation

C. Affirmation Structures

1. Affirmative Responses:

Used to confirm or agree with a statement or question.

Forms: Kamano, Ee

Example:

Bende ing'eye? — Do you know him?

Kamano / Ee — Yes

D. Negation Structures

1. Standard Negation with "Ok"

"Ok" is placed before the verb to negate an action or state.

Example constructions:

  • Ok adhi — I am not going
  • Ok ang'eye — I do not know him
  • Ok rach — It is not bad
  • Ok orach — He/She/It is not bad

2. Response-Based Negation:

Combining Ooyo + Ok + Verb for polite negative responses.

  • Ooyo, ok abi biro — No, I will not come
  • Ooyo, ok ubi nyalo — No, you will not manage
  • Ooyo, ok unu-nyal — No, you (pl.) will not manage

3. Negative Contractions in the Past

Ne ok (I did not...) can contract to Noka...

  • Ne ok achiemo — I did not eat
  • Nokachiemo — I didn't eat

4. Present & Future Tense Negation

The verb takes the subjunctive form (final -o is dropped)

Future tense sometimes uses "be" instead of "bi"

  • Ok achiem — I am not eating
  • Ok abi chiemo — I will not eat
  • Okabechiemo — I will not eat

5. Past Perfect Negation with "Pok"

Pok = contraction of pod + ok (still not)

  • Pok asechiemo — I have not yet eaten

6. Negative Imperative with "Kik"

Kik replaces "ok" in command form.

  • Kik bi! — Don't come!

E. Pronoun-Negation Fusion

In responses, negation fuses into a single word unit:

  • Ooyo, ok ana-nyal — No, I won't manage
  • Ooyo, ok ini-nyal — No, you won't manage
  • Ooyo, wana-nyal — No, we won't manage
  • Ooyo, ok gini-nyal — No, they won't manage

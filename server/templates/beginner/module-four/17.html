4.13 Conjunctions

4.13.1 Introductions

I. Definition and Function of Conjunctions

  • Conjunctions are syntactic tools used to join or separate:
    • Words
    • Phrases
    • Clauses
    • Ideas
    • Objects
  • They provide grammatical and logical cohesion in Dholuo sentences, similar to their function in English.

II. Categories of Dholuo Conjunctions

A. Additive and Coordinating Conjunctions

English Dholuo Usage
And Gi, to gi To join similar elements: nouns, verbs, phrases.
With Kod, to kod Denotes accompaniment, often with animate beings or instruments.
Together Kanyakla, Ka'achiel Emphasizes unity, collectivity, or joint action.
Also / Even Kata Functions as an additive or emphasizing conjunction.
Or Kata, Koso Offers alternatives or choices, often interchangeable.

B. Illustrative Usage of Coordinating Conjunctions

  1. Mia ma gi macha — Give me this and that.
    • "Gi" joins two objects.
  2. Mia ma to gi macha — Give me this then that.
    • "To gi" adds a sequential tone.
  3. Abiro dhi kode — I will go with him.
    • "Kod-e": "kod" means with and "-e" denotes the pronoun him.
  4. Ating'o okombe kod tate — I carry a cup with the saucer.
    • "Kod" links instrumentality or accompaniment.

Note:

  • In many cases, "kod" and "gi" can be used interchangeably to convey togetherness or accompaniment.
  • Choice depends on context, emphasis, and dialectal variation.

III. Disjunctive and Alternative Conjunctions

A. Expressing Alternatives (Either/Or)

  • Dholuo equivalents:
    • Koso
    • Kata

Example Phrases:

  1. Idwa dhi koso ihero mondo idong'? — Do you want to go or would you prefer to remain?
  2. Idwa dhi kata ihero mar dong'? — Do you want to go or do you love staying?

Observation:

  • Both "kata" and "koso" are used to denote choice or exclusion, and may be stylistically interchangeable.

IV. Practical Coordination in Dholuo

  1. Ring'o gi rech — Maize and fish
  2. Kuon gi gweno — Ugali and chicken
  3. Nyako gi wuoyi — Girl and boy
  • Such phrases show paralleled pairing, often denoting natural or thematic pairings.

V. Conjunctions of Reason and Purpose

A. Why? — Ang'o momiyo...?

  • Used to seek justification or causality for an action or decision.

Example:

  • Ang'o momiyo isomo Dholuo? — Why do you study Dholuo?

VI. Expressing Cause or Reason

A. Principal Causal Conjunctions:

Dholuo English Equivalent Usage Note
Nimar Because Most common, neutral
Nikech Because Widely used, especially in Alego/Ugenya
Nikuop Because Regional variation

Example Sentences:

  • Asomo Dholuo nimar adwaro ng'eye — I study Dholuo because I want to know it.
  • Asomo Dholuo nikech adwa ng'eye — I study Dholuo because I want to know it.
  • Asomo Dholuo nikuop adwa ng'eye — I study Dholuo because I want to know it.

All three expressions are mutually intelligible across Luo-speaking regions.

VII. Purpose Clauses and Subjunctive Intent

A. Ni Mondo — "So that" / "In order to"

Construction Explanation
Ni mondo Compound form used for expressing purpose, often followed by a verb in the subjunctive.
Mondo (shortened) Acceptable, but loses the subjunctive tone if used without "ni".

Example:

  • Chiemo ni mondo ayud teko — I eat so that I may get energy.

B. Syntax Highlight:

  • "Ni" = that / for
  • "Mondo" = purpose marker (may...)

VIII. Important Usage Notes and Clarifications

  • "Ni mondo" ≠ "Nimar" or "Nikech":
    • "Ni mondo" → Purpose (why something is done to achieve a future result)
    • "Nimar/Nikech" → Reason (why something is done due to a present or past cause)
  • Incorrect Usage:
    • ❌ I play because I win — Incorrect causal logic
    • ✅ I play so that I win — Purpose is expressed

Correct Dholuo Forms:

  • Atugo ni alo — I play to win
  • Atugo (ni) mondo alo — I play so that I may win

X. Summary and Pedagogical Implications

  • Dholuo uses a rich array of conjunctions for joining, contrasting, and justifying ideas.
  • Functional distinctions between "gi/kod", "kata/koso", "nimar/nikech/nikuop", and "ni mondo" must be carefully taught.
  • Tone, syntax, and verb dependency guide appropriate usage.

Mastery of conjunctions enables advanced narrative, persuasive, and argumentative expression in Dholuo.

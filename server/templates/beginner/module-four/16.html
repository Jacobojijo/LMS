4.12.2 Relative Pronouns and Causal Connectives

I. Introduction to Relative Pronouns in Dholuo

Relative pronouns in Dholuo serve to connect a principal clause with a subordinate clause, allowing for complex sentence construction.

The central relative marker is "mondo" — equivalent to "that" or "so that" in English, but it is not a preposition.

II. Key Relative Pronoun: "Móndo"

1. Function and Usage

"Móndo" introduces a dependent clause indicating purpose, condition, or reported speech.

Anyise mondo ong'e — I tell him that he may know.

2. Phonetic Clarification

Móndo (sharp tone): That/so that

Mondo (flat tone): To arrive early

Nyithindo mondo e skul — Pupils arrive early at school

This tonal contrast carries semantic weight and must be carefully observed to avoid ambiguity.

III. Structural Scheme: Principal + Relative Clause Pattern

Principal Phrase Relative Pronoun Predicate/Subordinate Clause
Wabiro (ni) mondo wabed mamor — that we may be happy
Wakwayi (ni) mondo Nyasaye okonywa — that God may help us
Ahombi (ni) mondo ayud cham — that I may get food
Giyombo (ni) mondo kik jowasigu negi — that enemies may not kill them
Otiyo (ni) mondo ji obi — that people may come
Osomo (ni) mondo ong'e wuotho — that he may know the way
Olupo (ni) mondo gibed mamor — that they may be happy

The phrase "ni mondo" is an intentional two-word construct where:

  • Ni = So
  • Mondo = That

Together, they express intent, aim, or necessity.

IV. Contextual Examples and Syntax

A. Expressing Intent or Purpose:

Aidho yath mondo apon olemo — I plant a tree so that I may find shade.

Adhi e skul mondo a som — I go to school so that I may study.

B. Reporting or Quoting Speech:

Apenjo ni ang'e — I ask that I know.

Apenjo ni mondo ayud teko — I ask so that I may gain strength.

C. Imperative Constructs:

Inka ni iti! — You are here to work!

Note: "Ni" here takes on a directive and motivational tone, functioning within imperative constructions.

V. Expressing Cause: Causal Connectors in Dholuo

1. Nikech — Because

Used to express direct cause or reason for an action or state.

Examples:

  • Aringo nikech alewo — I'm running because I'm late.
  • Achiemo nikech adenyo — I eat because I'm hungry.
  • Gidhiyo e od thieth nikech gituo — They went to the hospital because they're sick.
  • Oywak nikech owinjo rem — He cried because he was in pain.
  • Orogo nikech okecho — He chased them because he was angry.
  • Abiro nikech aduaro neni — I'll come because I want to see you.
  • Amose nikech ang'eye — I refused because I know him.
  • Otamore wuoyo koda nikech nene wadhaw — He refused to talk to me because we quarreled.

2. Additional Causal Forms:

A-ringo nikech a-lewo — I run because I am late.

Opodho nikech omer — He fainted because it was hot.

3. Other Related Causal Markers (mentioned but not expanded in usage):

  • Nikuop — because
  • Nimar — because

VI. Comparative Overview of "Mondo" and "Nikech"

Connector Function Example
(ni) mondo Relative clause introducer Wamiel nimondo wabed mamor — We celebrate so that we may be happy
nikech Causal explanation Achiemo nikech adenyo — I eat because I'm hungry

"Mondo" expresses future or hypothetical results, while "nikech" accounts for past or existing reasons.

VII. Synthesis and Semantic Insight

"Mondo" enriches Dholuo syntax by enabling complex subordinate structures, primarily to express:

  • Purpose
  • Intention
  • Indirect Speech
  • Causality (future-oriented)

"Nikech" anchors immediate or observed causes behind an action, akin to "because" in English.

VIII. Summary of Key Takeaways

  • "Mondo" is a powerful syntactic tool enabling intention and consequence-based expression.
  • It must be distinguished phonetically and semantically from the homophone "mondo" meaning "arrive early."
  • "Ni" in "ni mondo" has a directive force, forming a purposeful link.
  • "Nikech" and its variants (nikuop, nimar) are used to convey reasons and justification.
  • The relational logic embedded in these connectors is crucial for mastering academic, persuasive, and functional Dholuo.

4.5.2 The Compliment of Company & Argument

A. The Complement of Company

Definition and Functional Role

  • Denotes companionship or accompaniment in an action or movement.
  • Introduces social co-presence, marking who is with whom during an activity.
  • Often misinterpreted as coordination ("and"), but in this context, it expresses association (with).

Structural Formula

Subject + Verb + Destination/Action + "gi" + Companion

Core Grammatical Insight

  • The preposition "gi" in this usage means "with" rather than "and."
  • It emphasizes relational participation, i.e., not a listing of individuals but the presence of a companion in the action.

Illustrative Examples and Interpretive Notes

  1. Adhi e chiro gi Maria — Maria and I are going to the market
    • gi Maria = with Maria, not a compound subject
    • ✧ Emphasizes shared participation in the act of going to market.
  2. Odipo dhi e tich gi Olum — Odipo and Olum are going to work
    • gi Olum = with Olum, companion of Odipo
    • ✧ Highlights work-related association between individuals.
  3. Olum dhi e skul gi Anyango — Olum is going to school with Anyango
    • gi Anyango = accompanying Anyango
    • ✧ Marks joint action, not equal grammatical subjects.

Summary Insight

  • This complement class captures interpersonal dynamics in activity.
  • "gi" here is prepositional, not coordinative, affirming company, not equality in sentence role.

B. The Complement of Argument

Definition and Semantic Function

  • Expresses the topic or subject matter of speech, discussion, or discourse.
  • Answers the question: "What are we talking about?"
  • Introduced through the preposition "kuom", functioning analogously to on, about, or concerning in English.

Structural Formula

Subject + Speech Verb + kuom + Argument Topic

Key Lexical Feature

The preposition "kuom" is polysemous, covering a wide semantic range:

  • talking of
  • about
  • speaking on
  • discussing concerning

Illustrative Examples and Analytical Breakdown

  1. Ker nene owuoyo kuom dongruok — Jatend piny talked on development
    • kuom dongruok = about development
    • ✧ Positions development as a discourse object, central to political narrative.
  2. Nene wawuoyo kuom tiegruok — We talked of training
    • kuom tiegruok = about training
    • ✧ Emphasizes intellectual engagement or thematic dialogue on education.

Functional Summary

  • "Kuom" anchors topics to verbs of speech or thought.
  • Its deployment situates the argument within mental or verbal action, connecting linguistic structure to conceptual focus.

C. Comparative Table: Company vs. Argument Complements

Complement Type Marker Semantic Role Prepositional Meaning Typical Verb Context
Company gi Companionship/Presence With Movement/Action (e.g., go)
Argument kuom Topic/Subject of Discourse Of, on, about, concerning Speaking, discussing, talking

D. Cultural and Linguistic Implications

  • Company Complements reflect social behavior and companionship, revealing interpersonal ties in everyday activities.
  • Argument Complements demonstrate cognitive and thematic engagement, central to community dialogue, governance, and education.
  • Both employ prepositions that ground abstract or relational ideas within tangible syntactic structures.

4.12 Preposition

4.12.1 Introductions

I. Definition and Functionality

Prepositions in Dholuo serve to define:

  • Spatial orientation (location, direction, and position),
  • Relational context between objects,
  • Possessive and instrumental functions.

Equivalent in function to English prepositions such as: at, in, on, under, behind, with, for.

III. Semantic Distinctions & Morphological Origins

1. Spatial Prepositions with Anatomical Roots

Dholuo spatial terms often derive from body parts, indicating metaphorical orientation:

  • ewi ← e wiy (at the head of)
  • ei ← e iy (at the stomach of)
  • etie ← e tiende (at the foot of)

These etymologies highlight a culturally embodied way of conceptualizing space.

IV. The Triadic "For" Structure: Nuanced Uses of ni, kuom

a. Ni

Function: Denotes purpose, motive, or target.

Atiyo ni pesa – I work for money

b. Kuom

Function: Conveys causal attribution, gratitude, or reason.

Erokamano kuom chiemo – Thank you for the food

These subtle distinctions underscore the semantic richness of Dholuo, enabling precision in role assignment.

V. Expressing Companionship: "With" in Dholuo

1. Gi

Used for general accompaniment with nouns.

Abiro gi agulu– I come with a pot

Limitation: Cannot attach to pronoun suffixes, except when go is used:

Abirogo e ot – I come with it (not "him")

2. Kod

Employed when expressing companionship with people or pronouns.

Abiro kod Achieng' – I come with Achieng'

Abiro kode e ot – I come with him to the house

Grammatically flexible with pronoun suffixation: kode (with him), koda (with me), kodwa (with us), etc.

VI. Possessive Expression: The Role of "mar"

Mar is used to express ownership or association:

chak mar nithindo – the children's milk

Used more extensively in nominal constructions across possession and classification.

VII. Cultural-Linguistic Summary: The Dynamics of Prepositions in Dholuo

Dholuo prepositions are richly rooted in spatial metaphors, social relationships, and functional clarity.

They reflect:

  • A deeply embodied spatial logic (based on body part analogies),
  • A nuanced view of possession, motivation, and association,
  • A high degree of syntactic flexibility for precise expression.

Understanding prepositions is essential to mastering the relational structure of Dholuo communication.

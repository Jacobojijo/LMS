4.16 Writing & Reading in Dholuo

4.16.1 Introductions

Reading Dholuo

1. Phonemic Awareness

Dholuo is largely phonemic, meaning there is a close match between the way words are written and how they are pronounced.

2. Tonal Awareness

Dholuo is tonal, and tone plays a lexical and grammatical role.
High, Low, and Falling tones are significant.
Tones can distinguish meaning:
bẹ́d (stay!) vs bẹd (he stays)
Unfortunately, most orthographies do not mark tone, so learners must develop strong auditory and contextual awareness.

3. Orthography and Reading Skills

Current Orthographic Conventions

  • Roman alphabet is used.
  • Tonal marks are usually omitted in non-academic texts.
  • Vowel length is significant

Strategies for Effective Reading

  • Start with graded texts: Use materials with simple sentence structure.
  • Read aloud: Helps internalize tone, vowel harmony, and rhythm.
  • Annotate texts: Mark tones and vowel lengths to aid comprehension.
  • Use audio-text pairs: Especially useful for second-language learners.
  • Practice morphological parsing: Dholuo is highly agglutinative.

Writing in Dholuo

1. Grammar and Sentence Structure

Dholuo has a Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) structure.

Noun Classes

No noun class system like in Bantu, but number is marked:
Some semantic classes: animate vs inanimate

Verbal Morphology

Verbs are complex and encode:
Subject agreement
Tense/aspect/mood (TAM)
Polarity (negation)

Example:
a-se-timo → I have done
a- (1st person singular)
se- (perfect aspect)
timo (do)

Common TAM Markers:

Marker Meaning Example
bi- future abiro tich (I will work)
se- perfective ase bedo (I have been)
ka- progressive eka tedo (I am cooking)

Verb conjugation is a central feature of writing fluently in Dholuo.

2. Orthographic Accuracy

  • Mark vowel length clearly in academic writing.
  • Use standardized spelling.
  • Be cautious of homophones that differ by tone or vowel length.

3. Writing Techniques

  • Practice morphology drills: especially verbs and compound nouns.
  • Compose short essays or dialogues using known vocabulary.
  • Use peer review or native speakers for feedback.
  • Use dictionaries such as English–Dholuo Dictionary

4. Common Challenges and How to Overcome Them

Challenge Strategy
Vowel harmony inconsistency Practice minimal pairs, use vowel charts
Tone ambiguity Learn through audio exposure and context
Complex verbs Break down into morphemes during composition
Spelling variation Use standard grammar books and dictionaries

4.16.2 Reading Comprehension

Text: Sigand Simbi Nyaima

Chon gilala nene nitie dala moro maduong' mar Waswa ma nene oger e wi thur. Chieng' moro jodalano nene oloso mor maduong' mar kong'o. Nene gimetho gi mor nyaka odhiambo. Koth ne obiro maduong' gi yamo e kar odhiambo. Dhako moro nene obiro koa Wagasi kuma jokoth nitie. Dhani ne en dhako ma oti kendo nene onenore marach. Ne odonjo e dalano mondo obuogi ni koth mabiro maduong'. To ka ne odonjo e dala, nene odhi mondo obuogi e ot ma ne ji madhoe kong'o. Ne giriembe oko. Kamano nene otemo donjo e udi duto, to onge ng'at mane oruake. Eka ne odhi e ot machielo manene nitie tung'. Ne oyudo wuon od gi nyithinde, to dichuo to nene odhi madho kong'o gi jodongo. Wuon ot ma Mikayi nene olosone mach mondo oo nimar koth nene ogoye kendo koyo bende nene omake.

Ka nene oseoyo mach ne openjo dhakono ma wuon ot ni; "In kod chuori koso?" To Dhakono nene oyiene. Ne onyise mondo odhi piyo piyo mondo oluonge mondo obi mapiyo kaka nyalore. Mikayi ma wuon ot nene odhi luongo chwore to chwore nene oriembe matek ka owinjo ni dhako mane giriembo ema luonge. Mikayini nene odok ir chwore nyaka didek ka osayo chuore, to chwore nene otamore chuth. Ka dichuo ne osedagi to nene openjo mikayini,"Bende intie kod nyithindi?" Mikayi mawuon ot nene oyiene, kendo ne onyise kar romb nyithindego. Nene ochike mondo ochokgi mondo giwuogi mapiyo piyo, nimar dalano nene biro kethore. Mikayini nene dwaro dok mondo oom chwore to dhakoni nene otamore. Dhakoni iluonge ni Anyango ma nyar Gwasi.

Nene giwuok bi gidhi. Nene giyoro pi nyaka chonggi. Bang'gi to dala nene osienyore monego ji duto, gi jamni to gi dhok to gwen nene olokore winy mag akado, asoko gi wiwi nyaka chil kawuono. A kanyo gi luongo kanyo ni Simbi Nyaima. Pige otimo bala to kendo buoyo mar pi lokore kido maratiglo.

  2. Nying dhakoni nene iluongo ni ng'a?
  3. Dhako motini nene dwaro ang'o e dalano?

Answers

1. Tense of the Story

The story is narrated in the past simple tense, with most verbs conjugated in the past (e.g., nene oloso, nene odonjo, nene oyiene). There are no constructions indicating future or present continuous tenses.

2. Paragraph 1 – Verb Analysis

a) Verb "olosone"

  • Verb root: loso – "to make"
  • Conjugated verb: olosone – "he/she made it for him/her"

Subject and Object of "loso"

  • Subject: Wuon ot ma Mikayi – the old man (father/husband)
  • Object: mach – fire

So, the sentence "Wuon ot ma Mikayi nene olosone mach" means "The old man of the household made fire for her."

3. Paragraph 2 – Questions

b) Who went to call who and where?

Mikayi ma wuon ot (the old woman of the house) went to call her husband (chwore) from where he was drinking with the elders.

Did the person accept the call? Why?

Initially, no. The husband (chwore) was hesitant and reluctant because he was told by his wife that the strange woman who had tried entering several houses was the one calling him.
His suspicion and the unusual behavior made him reluctant.

"Kar romb nyithindo" – What does it mean?

  • Literal meaning: "the place where the children are counted" or "register/list of children"
  • Contextual meaning: a record or a census of the children in the household.

Why did the old woman ask for "kar romb nyithindo"?

To confirm the presence and safety of all her children, fearing that the strange woman might pose a danger. She wanted to ensure the children were accounted for before taking any risks.

4. Paragraph 3 – Language Analysis

c) "Yoro pi" – To walk through water

Tense: Past simple
The verb is in narrative form, referring to actions that already happened.

d) "Mapiyo piyo" – Adjective or Adverb?

Type: Adverb
It describes how the person was to come – "quickly", modifying the verb.

Adverbs in the Passage (sample list):

  • Mapiyo piyo – quickly
  • Chuth – reluctantly
  • Matek – strongly/intensely
  • Nyaka – until
  • Maduong' – greatly (contextually can also function as an adjective)
  • Ka – when (can function adverbially in clauses)

Nouns in the Passage (sample list):

  • Dala – homestead
  • Koth – rain
  • Dhako – woman
  • Chwore – her husband
  • Nyithindo – children
  • Mach – fire
  • Jodongo – elders
  • Simbi Nyaima – name of the village (literally "the village that sank")
  • Jamni – property/belongings mainly animals
  • Winyo – bird
  • Akado, asoko, wiwi – types of birds or insects (nature imagery)

5. Interpretation and Inference

i) Did the rain start before or after the woman reached the homestead?

The rain had already started or was just beginning as the woman approached. The sentence "Koth nene ochako chwe ka nene dhakoni ochopo e dalano" suggests the rain was intensifying as she arrived.

ii) What was the name of the strange woman?

The woman was eventually called Anyango, and she was said to be nyar Gwasi (daughter of Gwasi).

iii) What did the strange woman want in the homestead?

The woman wanted to warn the homestead about the impending heavy rain and flooding. She was trying to save the family by urging them to evacuate before the village was flooded.

Onyango Japur

Nene nitie ng'atmoro modak Ugenya ma Masawa. Nyinge ne en Onyango Japur. Nene ohero pur ahinya. Kama nene odakie nene en kama otuo ahinya, kendo ndalo mag chieng' nene ji chando pi. To kinde mag koth, nene en kama rach mokadho ni mar pi nene pong'e te.

Chieng' moro Onyango nene ochiew gokinyi mar kok gwen. Ne okawo kweye mar pur kaka pile kendo owuok. Ka nene ochopo e apaya madhi e puothe, to ne oromo kod dhako moro mamigumba ka wuotho. Dhakono nene omose. To ka nene oduoke maber gi mos, to dhakono nene onyise mondo ochik ite. Onyango nene otimo kamano.

Dhakono nene onyise niya, "Dhi e puothi kama ipuroga pile, to ka isechopo to kuny piny dichiel kendo gima owuok e manobedi hapi."

Onyango nene oparore ahinya ka oparo ni dhako maotino owuonde. To katakamano nene odhil kendo odhi adhiya nyaka e puodo. Nene oparo mondo otim kaka dhako cha nene okone. Nene okunyo piny, to gima nene owuok ne en gima chalo kidi mar ombo. Nene okawe e lwete kendo ong'iye. Ne en kidi manyilini mana kaka sulwe mar nam.

Onyango nene oringo matek kadok e pacho. Kane ochopo to ji nene obiro neno gima Onyango oseyudo. Nene ok ging'eyo ni en ang'o. Nene giluongo jarieko moro manene odak machiegni mondo obi onyisgi ni en ang'o.

Ka nene jarieko ochopo, to ne okonigi ni mano en kidi mar dhahabu (gold). Kendo nene onyiso Onyango ni mano oseyudo pesa mang'eny ahinya, kendo obiro bedo ng'at ma jamwandu.

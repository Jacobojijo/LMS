2.5 Pronouns (Part 01)

2.5.1 Introduction

Introduction to Dholuo Pronouns

  • Definition: Pronouns in Dholuo are words used to replace nouns in order to avoid repetition and simplify speech.
  • Types of Pronouns:
    1. Personal Pronouns – Refer to specific persons or things (e.g., I, you, he, we, they).
    2. Possessive Pronouns – Show ownership or possession (e.g., mine, yours, theirs).
    3. Demonstrative Pronouns – Point to specific things (e.g., this, that).
    4. Interrogative Pronouns – Used to ask questions (e.g., who, what, which).
    5. Relative Pronouns – Link clauses and refer to nouns mentioned previously.
  • Personal Pronoun Examples (Subject Form):
    • An – I
    • I – You (singular)
    • En – He/She/It
    • Wa – We
    • U – You (plural)
    • Gin – They
  • Object and Possessive Forms: Often implied or context-driven, but can be formed using suffixes or word placement.
  • Pronoun Use:
    • Subject pronouns are often incorporated into verbs (e.g., Idhi – You go, Wabiro – We will come).
    • Emphasis can be added by stating the pronoun explicitly before the verb (e.g., An abiro – I will come).
  • Interrogative Pronouns (examples):
    • Ng'ano – Who
    • Ang'o – What
    • Nang'o – Why
    • Karang'o – When
    • Kanye – Where

2.5.2 Personal pronouns

1. What Are Personal Pronouns?

  • Personal pronouns refer to specific persons or groups in conversation.
  • In Dholuo, they are often used, but can be dropped when the verb form or context makes the subject clear.
  • Used to show who is performing the action — e.g., "I eat", "He speaks", etc.

2. The Six Core Personal Pronouns in Dholuo

Dholuo English Equivalent Usage Example Sentence
an I / me 1st person singular An e pacho → I am at home
in you (singular) 2nd person singular In e dala → You are at home
en he / she 3rd person singular En e ot → He/She is in the house
wan we / us 1st person plural Wan e piny → We are in the land
un you (plural) 2nd person plural Un e chokruok → You (all) are at the ceremony
gin they / them 3rd person plural Gin e dala → They are at home

Note: Gender-neutral — "en" applies to both he and she.

3. Demonstratives and Personal Pronouns Together

Demonstratives (ma, mano, macha) pair with personal pronouns to refer to people or things precisely.

Phrase Meaning
An ma I, this (as in "I am the one")
En macha He/She is that one (far)
Gin mano They are that/those ones (near you)

4. Structure of a Simple Sentence

Common structure in Dholuo when using personal pronouns: Subject Pronoun + Verb (+ Location/Object/Demonstrative)

Examples:

  • An dhi dala → I am go home.
  • In biro sani → You are coming now.
  • En nyalo tedo → He/She can cook.
  • Wan wabedo kae → We are sitting here.
  • Un uwuog kacha → You (all) leave that place
  • Gin gidonjo ot → They are entering the house

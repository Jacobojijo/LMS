2.7 Pronouns & Noun Declension

2.7.1 Interrogative Pronoun

Interrogative pronouns are pronouns used to introduce questions, replacing nouns when the identity of the noun is unknown. In Dholuo, the main interrogative pronouns are "what," "which," "who," "whom," and "whose". These pronouns function as question words, replacing nouns or noun phrases in a sentence to seek specific information.

1. Basic Interrogative Pronouns:

  • How – Nade/i
    • Used to inquire about the manner or process of something.
    • Example:
      • Idhi nade? — How are you going?
  • Why – Nang'o/marang'o?
    • Used to ask for reasons or causes.
    • Example:
      • Nang'o itamori? — Why do you refuse?
  • How many – Adi?
    • Used to inquire about quantity, especially in the case of countable nouns.
    • Example:
      • Gin ji adi? — How many people are they?
  • When – Karang'o?
    • Used to ask about time or when an event is happening.
    • Example:
      • Ibiro karang'o? — When will you come?
  • Who – Ng'ano?
    • Used to ask about a person or people, identifying an individual.
    • Example:
      • Ng'ano ma Openja? — Who asked for me?
      • Ng'a? — Who?
  • What – Ang'o?
    • Used to inquire about things or actions.
    • Example:
      • Ma en ang'o? — What is this?

2. Common Questions: Present Tense

These questions are used to inquire about the current action of a person or group.

  • Are you going? – Idhi?
  • Is she/he going? – Odhi?
  • Are you (pl) going? – Udhi?
  • Are we going? – Wadhi?
  • Are they going? – Gidhi?
  • Are you coming? – Ibiro?
    • Example of more meaning using "Be" (for emphasis):
      • Be idhi? — Are you really going?
      • Be odhi? — Is she/he really going?

3. Tag Questions (Expressing Agreement or Disagreement):

Tag questions are used to confirm or deny a statement. The structure involves asking a question and adding a tag at the end to expect confirmation or disagreement.

  • Bende idhi, koso? — Are you going, aren't you?
  • Bende odhi, koso? — Is she/he going, Isn't she/he?
  • Bende gidhi, koso? — Are they going, aren't they?
  • Bende ing'eyogi, koso? — Do you know them, don't you?
  • Ok idhi, koso? — Aren't you going?
  • Ok owuo, koso? — Doesn't he/she speak, does he/she?

4. Specific Question Examples (Exploring deeper meanings of questions)

  • Ng'ano ma oyie?
    • Who is willing?
    • Asking about a person's willingness or readiness to do something.
  • Mara ang'o ok ne ibiro?
    • Why did you fail to come?
    • This question seeks to understand the reason behind not attending an event.
  • Ng'ato ang'ata nyalo bedo jaloyo?

5. Location-related Questions (Where?):

  • Where? – Kanye?
    • A general question asking the location of something.
  • Pala ni kanye? — Where is the knife?
  • Ere pala? — Where is the knife?
    • Both are variations of the same question, asking about the location of the knife, with slight differences in phrasing.
  • Kom ni kanye? — Where is the chair?
  • Kom nitie kanye? — Where is the chair located?
    • The difference here is in the use of "nitie", which emphasizes the exact location.

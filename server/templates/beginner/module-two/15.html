2.5.4 Demonstrative pronouns

1. What are Demonstrative Pronouns?

  • Used to point to or refer to specific nouns, like English this, that, these, those.
  • Indicate proximity (near or far) in relation to the speaker or listener.

2. Singular Demonstratives

Dholuo English Equivalent Usage Context Example
ma or mani this Near the speaker Ma en otanda → This is a bed
mano that Near the listener Mano en kom-a → That is my chair
macha that over there Far from both speaker and listener Macha e jakwath → That is a herdsman over there

3. Plural Demonstratives

Dholuo English Equivalent Usage Context Example
magi these Near the speaker Magi gin yawuoi → These are boys
mago those (near) Near the listener Mago ang'o? → What are those?
maka those (far away) Far from both Maka gin jokwath → Those are herdsmen
Note: "Magi" may also mean "snatch from them" depending on pronunciation/stress.

4. Demonstratives in Questions

Used to inquire about identity or description:

  • Ma ang'o? → What is this?
  • Mago ang'o? → What are those?
  • Macha ang'o? → What is that (far over there)?

5. Demonstratives with Nouns

Used before or after nouns to specify them:

  • Ma en pacho (dala) → This is a home
  • Ma en ataro → This is a lake
  • Ma en aora → This is a river
  • Ma en ogut-a → This is my hat

6. Demonstratives + Possessive Pronouns

Demonstratives can combine with possessives for added specificity.

Dholuo Phrase Translation
Man en kom-a This is my chair
Ma en guend-a This is my hen
Maka gin dhog-u Those are your cows
Magi gin guogi-ni These are your (2nd person singular) dogs

"-ni" is the demonstrative suffix meaning "this".

  • Ng'atni → This person
  • Guoni → This dog
  • Dheni → This cow
  • Komni → This chair

8. Demonstratives Indicating Place

Dholuo English
Ka Here
Kae This place
Ka-cha / Kachande / Kachacha That place (far)

Examples:

  • Odni → This house
  • Odcha → That house
  • Yorni → This path
  • Yorcha → That path
  • Ng'atni → This person
  • Ng'atcha → That person
  • Dalani → This home
  • Dalacha → That home
  • Dheni → This cow
  • Dhecha → That cow

9. Demonstratives Indicating Time

Dholuo English
Sani Now
Kawuono (or Gonyo) Today
Nyoro Yesterday
Nyocha Day before yesterday
Kiny Tomorrow
Orucha Day after tomorrow
Bang'e Later

10. Demonstratives for Speed/Action

Dholuo English
Piyo Quickly
Mos Slowly

11. Exercise – Possessive Fill-ins

Sentence Meaning
Ma en yadh-e This is her tree
Maka gin ndap-gi Those are their cigarettes
Ma en dala-wa This is our home
Magi gin chiemb-u- These are your (plural) foods

Bonus Tips

  • -ni suffix = "this"
  • Combine with any noun for specific meaning:
    • Komni → This chair
    • Yawoni → This pond
    • Ng'atni → This person
    • Dalani → This home

2.5.3 Possessive Pronouns and Cases

1. Overview of Possessive Pronouns in Dholuo

Possession in Dholuo is shown by adding a suffix to the noun.

These suffixes change depending on:

  • ✅ The person (1st, 2nd, 3rd)
  • ✅ The number (singular/plural)

You may also use "mar" to express "of/belonging to," like mar Adhiambo = "Adhiambo's"

2. Singular Possessive Forms

✅ First Person Singular (I)

Suffixes: -na or -wa

Examples:

  • Nyathi-na = my child
  • Puodho en mara = the garden is mine

✅ Second Person Singular (You)

Suffixes: -ni or -i

Examples:

  • Nyathi-ni = your child
  • Puodho en mari = the garden is yours

✅ Third Person Singular (He/She/It)

Suffix: -ne or -e

Gender-neutral

Examples:

  • Nyathi-ne = his/her child
  • Puodho en mare = the garden is his/hers/its

3. Plural Possessive Forms

✅ First Person Plural (We)

Suffix: -wa (used for both inclusive & exclusive)

Examples:

  • Nyathi-wa = our child
  • Puodho en marwa = the garden is ours

✅ Second Person Plural (You all)

Suffix: -u

Examples:

  • Nyathi-u = your (plural) child
  • Puodho en maru = the garden is yours (plural)

✅ Third Person Plural (They)

Suffix: -gi

Examples:

  • Nyathi-gi = their child
  • Puodho en margi = the garden is theirs

4. Use of "Mar" – Expressing Belonging/Ownership

The prefix "mar" is used with names and pronouns to emphasize possession.

Works like "of" or "belonging to" in English.

Phrase Meaning
Nyathi mar Adhiambo Adhiambo's child
Mara mine
Mari yours (singular)
Mare his/hers
Marwa ours
Maru yours (plural)
Margi theirs

5. Real-life Sentences Using "Puodho" (Garden)

Sentence Translation
Puodho en mara The garden is mine
Puodho en mari The garden is yours (singular)
Puodho en mare The garden is his/hers/its
Puodho en marwa The garden is ours
Puodho en maru The garden is yours (plural)
Puodho en margi The garden is theirs

6. Important Notes

  • Gender Neutrality: Third-person singular form (-ne or -e) does not change for male or female.
  • Flexibility: Speakers may choose between suffixes (like nyathi-ne) or "mar" + pronoun (like marwa) depending on emphasis or clarity.
  • Context Matters: Some suffixes (like -wa) appear in both first person singular and plural. Context and verb agreement help distinguish them.

1.8.1 Summary of Nouns (Part 01)

Nouns in Dholuo

Dholuo nouns name people, animals, objects, places, and concepts. They function as subjects or objects in sentences and may be modified for plurality and possession.

Categories of Nouns:

Category Examples
Concrete Nouns (tangible) buk (book), bunde (gun), dhok (cows)
Abstract Nouns (intangible) ler (light), kwee (peace), tim (action)
Proper Nouns (specific names) Nam Lolwe (Lake Victoria), Ochieng (born during daytime)
Common Nouns (general items) nyathi (child), chieng' (sun/day), jowi (buffalo)
Collective Nouns (groups) jomoko (some people), joma (group/they)

2. Equivocal Words in Dholuo

Dholuo features tonal words with multiple meanings based on pronunciation and context.

Examples of Equivocal Words:

Word Meanings
Bor distance, fat, clouds (bor polo – sky clouds)
Dhok cows, mouth, language (dhok Luo – Luo language)
Kendo marry, again, fireplace (kendo mar tedo – cooking place)
Tho death, dew, exclamation (Tho mar polo – morning dew)
Liel burning, shaving, anthill, grave (liel mar buch – anthill)
Tong spear, eggs, boundary (tong piny – land boundary)
Ler light, neat, vein (ler mar wang – eye vein)
Chik law, trap, plan (chik mar piny – national law)
Yweyo breathe, rest, sweep (yweyo ot – sweeping the house)

Challenges & Solutions:

Challenge Solution
Phonetic Complexity: Non-native speakers struggle with tones Practice with native speakers
Contextual Dependence: Misinterpretation occurs without clarity Cultural immersion, and use of linguistic tools

Gender of Nouns

Dholuo classifies nouns based on natural gender (biological sex) and grammatical neutrality rather than strict linguistic rules.

Gender Examples
Masculine Nouns (Male beings) dichuo (man), nyuok (he-goat), thuon (cock), wuoyi (boy), ruath (bull)
Feminine Nouns (Female beings) dhako (woman), swini (she-goat), nyabur (hen), nyako (girl), duasi (cow)
Neuter Nouns (Objects, places, concepts) kidi (stone), got (mountain), apaya (path), ndiga (bicycle), pacho (home)

Synonyms in Dholuo

Synonyms enrich expression, reflect cultural depth, and vary regionally.

Category Synonyms
Paths/Roads yoo (way), apaya (bush path), ndara (village road)
Homestead dala (family home), pacho (household)
Footwear pat pat, champat
People/Crowd ji (people), oganda (community), unji (group)
Garden/Farmland puodho (garden), ndalo (common in Alego, Ugenya)
Clothing nanga (cloth), law (blanket)
Blanket onget (general), baranget (thicker, used in colder regions)

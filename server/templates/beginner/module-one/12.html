1.6.1 Summary of General Ways of Communication

General Forms of Communication in Dholuo Culture

1. Introduction

The Luo people have a deeply oral culture where greetings (Mosruok) serve as fundamental expressions of respect, kinship, and social cohesion. Dholuo greetings reflect temporal specificity, plurality, and the community's structured worldview.

2. Temporal Greetings in Dholuo

Dholuo greetings vary by time of day, with distinct verb forms indicating grammatical complexity.

Time Singular Plural Response
Morning Greetings (Mos Mar Okinyi) Oyawore Oyaworeuru Oyawore ahinya
Midday/General Greetings (Mos Mar Odiechieng') Misawa Misawauru Misawa ahinya
Evening Greetings (Mos Mar Odhiambo) Oyimore Oyimoreuru Oyimore ahinya
Night Farewell (Mos Mar Otieno) Oriti inind maber! ("Goodnight, sleep well.")

3. Farewell and Departure Expressions

The term Oriti (derived from Rito, "to protect") signifies goodwill and divine protection.

Expression Meaning
Oriti! (singular)
Oriti uru! (plural)		 "Goodbye"
Oriti idhi maber "Go well, safe journey."
Oriti inind maber "Sleep well."
Oriti idong' maber "Stay well."

4. Common Conversational Phrases

Dholuo English

Family Structure in Dholuo Culture

Luo kinship is hierarchical and inclusive, extending beyond the nuclear family to incorporate a broad network of relatives who hold significant social and cultural roles.

1. Core Family Terms

Dholuo English
Nyathi "Child" (male or female)
Miyo / Minwa / Minu "Mother" (Minwa - "My mother", Minu - "Your mother")
Wuoro "Father"
Wuowi "Son"
Nyara "Daughter"

2. Grandparents and Descendants

Dholuo English
Kwaro "Grandfather"
Dayo "Grandmother"
Nyakwaro ma wuoyi "Grandson" (son's or daughter's son)
Nyakwaro ma nyako "Granddaughter" (son's or daughter's daughter)

1.7 Nouns (Part 01)

1.7.1 Introduction to Nouns

Nouns in Dholuo serve as the names of people, animals, objects, places, and abstract concepts, just as in many other languages. They are essential in constructing meaningful sentences, often acting as subjects or objects.

Categories of Dholuo Nouns

Category Examples
Concrete Nouns buk (book), bunde (gun), dhok (cows)
Abstract Nouns ler (light), kwee (peace), tim (action)
Proper Nouns Nam Lolwe (Lake Victoria), Ochieng (male name), Odiwuor (male name)
Common Nouns nyathi (child), chieng' (sun/day), jowi (buffalo)
Collective Nouns jomoko (some people), joma (the group/they)

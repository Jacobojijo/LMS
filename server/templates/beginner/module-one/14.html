1.7.4 Gender of Nouns

Noun gender is a fundamental linguistic concept that classifies nouns into different categories based on their inherent characteristics. In Dholuo, noun classification is primarily based on natural gender (biological sex) and grammatical neutrality. The language distinguishes between masculine, feminine, and neuter nouns.

The gender system in Dholuo is not strictly grammatical but is largely influenced by nature and culture. While masculine and feminine nouns correspond to living beings with distinct sexes, neuter nouns are used for inanimate objects and abstract entities.

Categories of Nouns Based on Gender

Masculine Nouns (Singular and Plural Forms)

Masculine nouns in Dholuo refer to male beings or objects associated with masculinity. These include:

Masculine Noun Meaning
Dichuo Man
Nyuok He-goat
Thuon Cock
Wuoyi Boy
Ruath Bull

Feminine Nouns (Singular and Plural Forms)

Feminine nouns in Dholuo refer to female beings or objects associated with femininity. Examples include:

Feminine Noun Meaning
Dhako Woman
Swini She-goat
Nyabur Hen
Nyako Girl
Duasi Cow

Neuter Nouns (Singular and Plural Forms)

Neuter nouns refer to inanimate objects, places, and concepts that do not have a biological gender. Examples include:

Neuter Noun Meaning
Kidi Stone
Got Mountain
Apaya Path, way, road
Ndiga Bicycle
Pacho Home

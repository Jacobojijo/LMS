3.3 Adjectives and Comparatives

3.3.1 Introduction to Comparatives

1. Introduction to Comparatives

Comparatives are used to compare qualities between two or more entities.

In Luo, comparison may involve:

  • Relative comparison (e.g., taller, sweeter)
  • Comparison of equality (e.g., as tall as, as sweet as)

These are often formed with:

  • Direct comparative forms
  • The construction "machal kod" (similar to)
  • The use of "kaka" (as...as)

2. Adjectives and Their Comparative Forms

The following are selected adjectives and their corresponding comparatives:

Adjective (Positive) Meaning Comparative Meaning
mabor tall bor taller
mamit sweet mit sweeter
marach bad rach worse
maber good ber better
mariek wise/clever riek wiser/cleverer

Example Sentences:

Mae bor moloyo macha. → "This is taller than that one."

Mae mit moloyo macha. → "This is sweeter than that one."

Mae rach moloyo macha. → "This is worse than that one."

Mae ber moloyo macha. → "This is better than that one."

Mae riek moloyo macha. → "This is wiser than that one."

3. Comparison of Equality

Equality in comparison is expressed using:

  • "machal kod" = "similar to" or "as...as"
  • "kaka" = used to express "as...as"

Standard Form:

"ma [adjective] machal kod macha" — "this is as [adjective] as that one"

Alternatively: "ma [adjective] kaka macha" — same meaning, slightly more flexible form.

Examples:

Construction Translation
Mae bor machal kod macha This is as tall as that one
Mae mit machal kod macha This is as sweet as that one
Mae rach machal kod macha This is as bad as that one
Ma ber kaka macha This is as good as that one
Ma nyap kaka macha This is as beautiful as that one
Ma riek kaka macha This is as wise as that one

4. Use of "Kaka" in Comparative Sentences

The word "kaka" functions similarly to the English word "as" in expressions of equality.

Used to frame parallel qualities between two entities.

Examples of Kaka Constructions:

Ma rach kaka macha → This is as bad as that one

Ma bor kaka macha → This is as tall as that one

Ma nyap kaka macha → This is as weak as that one

Ma mit kaka macha → This is as sweet as that one

Ma ber kaka macha → This is as good as that one

Ma riek kaka macha → This is as wise as that one

5. Morphological Patterns in Comparatives

Many Luo adjectives show a reduction in the syllable when forming the comparative:

  • mabor → bor
  • mamit → mit
  • marach → rach
  • maber → ber
  • mariek → riek

This pattern is consistent and reflects a grammatical regularity in the formation of comparatives.

6. Cultural Linguistic Note

Luo comparisons often focus not only on physical attributes (height, beauty), but also character and moral qualities (goodness, wisdom, badness).

These reflect the community's emphasis on personality and moral uprightness in communication.

3.3.2 Comparison of two things & Superlatives

1. Comparing Two Things (Relative Comparison)

a. Standard Comparative Structure

In Dholuo, comparing two distinct entities (e.g., people, places, qualities) involves the phrase:

ma oloyo/moloyo = "than"

This phrase follows the adjective, forming the equivalent of "X is more [adjective] than Y".

b. Sentence Structure:

[Subject A] [adjective] moloyo [Subject B]

Examples:

Mae bor moloyo macha
→ This is taller than that one.

Mae chiek moloyo macha
→ This is shorter than that one.

Mara ber moloyo mari
→ Mine is better than yours.

Chiega ber moloyo chiegi
→ His wife is better than theirs.

Puotha duong' moloyo puothi
→ My farm is bigger than yours.

Oda tin moloyo odi
→ My house is newer than his/hers.

Yorni bor moloyo yor cha
→ This way is longer than that one.

c. Special Note on "bor" (long)

Unlike most adjectives that change form in the comparative, "bor" (used to describe length or height) does not change morphologically.

Instead, the degree of comparison is shown purely by the structure, particularly with the inclusion of "ma oloyo".

Example: "Yorni bor ma oloyo yor cha" → "This way is longer than that one."

2. Comparisons Using "Kaka" (Equality)

a. Structure for Expressing Equality:

ma [adjective] kaka [second entity]
"as [adjective] as [that one]"

b. Examples of Equality Comparisons:

Ma mit kaka macha
→ This is as sweet as that one.

Ma ber kaka macha
→ This is as good as that one.

Maber kaka macha
→ This is as beautiful as that one.

This structure emphasizes parallel quality between two entities.

3. Superlative Forms in Luo

a. Superlative Structure

The superlative degree is formed by:

[Adjective] + moloyo te

  • "moloyo" = more than
  • "te" = all

Together, "moloyo te" = "more than all" → i.e., the most / the best / the biggest, etc.

b. Sentence Structure:

[Subject] [adjective] moloyo te
→ "X is the most [adjective]"

c. Examples of Superlative Forms:

Adjective Superlative Meaning
maber maber moloyo te the best
mariek mariek moloyo te the most intelligent
mofuwo mofuwo moloyo te the most stupid
mamit mamit moloyo te the sweetest
maduong' maduong' moloyo te the biggest
mabor mabor moloyo te the tallest / longest
manyap manyap moloyo te the weakest / laziest

Illustrative Sentence:

"Chiege riek moloyo te" → "His wife is the most intelligent."

4. Intensifiers: Expressing Extreme Qualities

a. High Degree Expressions

In Dholuo, certain adjectives can be intensified with specific intensifier phrases that emphasize extremes.

b. Examples of Intensified Adjectives:

Adjective Phrase Meaning
Rachar thiriri very white
Kwar ha very red
Rateng' ti very black
Liet ha very hot
Ngich thi very cold
Riek ndii very clever

Example Sentences:

Ng'atno riek ndii.
→ He is very clever.

Chieng' liet ha!
→ The sun is very hot!

Ot rateng' ti.
→ The room is very black/dark.

5. Summary of Key Grammatical Markers

Function Marker / Phrase Translation / Role
Equality Comparison kaka as...as
Similarity Comparison machal kod similar to
Comparative Degree ma oloyo/moloyo than
Superlative Degree moloyo te the most / more than all
Extreme Quality (intensity) Contextual phrase very [quality]

6. Cultural-Linguistic Insight

Luo expressions of comparison are not confined to physical traits but extend into:

  • Moral (e.g., maber, marach)
  • Intellectual (e.g., mariek, riek ndii)
  • Emotional or sensory qualities (e.g., mamit for sweetness)

This reflects a deep cultural value placed on character, intellect, and ethical judgment in personal and social interactions.

The use of "moloyo te" for superlatives especially highlights communal awareness of excellence and comparison across domains.

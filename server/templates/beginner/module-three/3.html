3.1.3 Adjective of Color

In Dholuo, adjectives denoting color are primarily formed through the prefixation of the particle "ma-" to the color base/root. This morphological process results in adjective forms that semantically function as descriptive modifiers of nouns, encapsulating visual characteristics.

1. Rakwar → Makwar (Red)

Root Color Term: rakwar — red

Adjectival Form: makwar (also analyzable as marakwar)

Morphology: ma- + rakwar

Usage: Commonly used for items with red pigmentation.

Example:

Kom marakwar — "A red chair"

Kalatas marakwar — "Red paper"

2. Rateng' → Marateng' (Black)

Root: rateng' — black

Adjectival Form: marateng'

Semantic Note: Also carries metaphorical connotation of darkness or obscurity.

Example:

Diel marateng' — "Black goat"

Law marateng' — "Black cloth"

3. Rachar → Marachar (White)

Root: rachar — white

Adjective: marachar

Semantic Range: Symbolically linked with purity and cleanliness.

Example:

Rech marachar — "White fish"

Ng'ato marachar — "A white person"

4. Rambulu → Marambulu (Blue)

Root: rambulu — blue

Adjective: marambulu

Usage: Describes objects or clothing with a blue hue.

Example:

Law marambulu — "Blue clothes"

Kom marambulu — "Blue chair"

5. Ratong' → Maratong' (Yellow)

Root Phrase: ratong' gweno — literally "the color of egg yolk"

Adjective: maratong'

Semantic Origin: Culturally specific term derived from the yolk of chicken eggs.

Example:

Yien maratong' — "Yellow tree"

Law maratong' — "Yellow garment"

6. Ralum / Majan → Maralum (Green)

Roots: ralum or majan — green

Adjective: maralum

Usage: Often describes vegetation or items with a natural green shade.

Example:

Yien maralum — "Green tree"

Alot maralum — "Green vegetables"

7. Rapir → Marapir (Indigo)

Root: rapir — indigo (deep blue-violet)

Adjective: marapir

Semantic Note: Used for dark hues, particularly in fabrics or dyes.

Example:

Law marapir — "Indigo clothing"

Kom marapir — "Indigo-painted chair"

8. Ralik → Maralik (Purple)

Root: ralik — purple

Adjective: maralik

Usage: Often associated with royalty or rich tones.

Example:

Law maralik — "Purple dress"

buk maralik — "Purple books"

9. Radier → Maradier (Black and White)

Root: radier — black-and-white

Adjective: maradier

Semantic Complexity: Denotes a dual tone or patterned coloration.

Example:

Diel maradier — "Black and white goat"

Law maradier — "Checkered clothing"

10. Rabuor → Marabuor (Brown)

Root: rabuor — brown

Adjective: marabuor

Semantic Context: Used for natural or earthen tones, commonly for hair, skin, or soil.

Example:

Kom marabuor — "Brown chair"

Nyako marabuor — "Brown-skinned girl"

General Observations

The prefix "ma-" in Dholuo not only creates adjectival agreement but also transforms color roots into full adjectival forms, maintaining descriptive consistency in noun phrases.

Some colors (e.g., ratong' gweno, radier) arise from cultural and environmental references.

The color lexicon in Dholuo reflects both linguistic precision and cultural embodiment, with each adjective tightly bound to the semantic field of its referent.

3.6.1.2 The Objective Pronoun

1. Introduction to Objective Pronouns in Dholuo

Definition:

Objective pronouns in Dholuo represent the receiver of an action performed by the subject. They correspond to the object in a sentence.

Function:

They are morphologically embedded into the end of the verb to indicate who or what is receiving the action.

Position in the Verb Structure:

Always appears as a suffix attached to the verb root.

Format: Subject Pronoun + Verb Stem + Object Suffix

Example:
Puonj-e → Teach him/her/it
Puonj = to teach (verb stem)
-e = him/her/it (object pronoun)

2. Personal Object Pronouns in Dholuo

Object Pronoun Meaning
-a me
-i you (singular)
-e him/her/it
-wa us
-un you (plural)
-gi them

3. Verb Structure: Conjugation of Transitive Verbs

General Rule:

A transitive verb in Dholuo is conjugated on both ends:

  • Prefix: indicates the subject
  • Suffix: indicates the object

Structure:

Subject Pronoun + Verb Stem + Object Pronoun

Example:
Gi-nego-gi → They have killed them
Gi = they (subject)
Nego = kill (verb stem)
-gi = them (object)

4. Translations: Verb "Nego" (to kill)

5. The Verb "Hero" — to love

This verb demonstrates object pronoun endings within emotional or relational verbs.

Conjugated Verb Structure Translation
Aheri A-hero-i I love you
Oheri O-hero-i He loves you
Waherogi Wa-hero-gi We love them
Giherou Gi-hero-u They love you (pl.)
Ohere O-hero-e He loves him/her
Aherou A-hero-u I love you (pl.)

6. The Verb "Nyuomo" — to marry

This verb shows both subject and object in intimate relationships.

Conjugated Verb Structure Translation
Anyuomi A-nyuomo-i I am marrying you
Onyuoma O-nyuomo-a He is marrying me
Okende O-kendo-e He married her
Gikendore Gi-kendo-re They are married

Note: Kendo can also mean fireplace or again, depending on accent and context. Here, it refers to marriage.

7. Syntax Summary: Subject–Verb–Object Alignment

The canonical word order in Dholuo transitive verb conjugation is:

Subject Pronoun + Verb + Object Pronoun

Each verb form is fully fused and agglutinative, meaning:

  • A single verb unit encodes the subject, verb, and object simultaneously.
  • Morphological richness ensures high information density per word.

9. Language Learning Implications

Understanding objective pronouns is crucial for:

  • Mastering complex verb conjugations.
  • Discerning between subject and object in sentence interpretation.
  • Enhancing listening comprehension and precise speaking.

Knowledge of verb structure (prefix and suffix) reveals:

  • Dholuo's status as a highly agglutinative language.
  • The efficiency of encoding syntactic roles in compact, fused forms.

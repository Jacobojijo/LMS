3.1 Adjectives

3.1.1 Adjectives & Adjective Clauses

1. Definition and Morphosyntactic Structure of Adjectives in Dholuo

Adjectives in Dholuo serve as modifiers that describe the qualities, states, or attributes of nouns. Morphologically, Dholuo adjectives are formed by prefixing the particle "ma-" to a descriptive root or base. The prefix "ma-" can be glossed as "which is" or "that is" in English, creating a relative adjective clause.

Syntax Structure:

Noun + ma- + Descriptor (Adjective Root)

e.g., Kom ma-kwar = "Chair that is red"

2. Morphological Marker: "ma-" as Adjectival Prefix

The particle "ma-" is the defining grammatical marker of adjectives. It links the noun and the descriptive root, thus forming an adjective phrase or a reduced relative clause.

4. Formation of Adjectives from Verbs

Some adjectives are derived from verbs using "ma-" (present participle/ongoing action) or "ma + past tense verb" (completed action). This creates adjective clauses, functioning similarly to English relative clauses ("that is X-ing", "that has X-ed").

Rules:

  • ma + verb (root) = ongoing or habitual action
  • ma + o-verb (past) = completed state/action

E.g., ma otho → "that has died" → motho

Verb-to-Adjective Conversions:

Verb Gloss Derived Adjective Meaning
wer to sing mawer / ma wer who is singing
tho to die matho which is dying
tho to die motho which has died (dead)
loso to make/fix miloso which is being made
loso to make/fix molosi which is already made

Observations:

The structure offers temporal nuance:

  • matho = present progressive
  • motho = past perfective

6. Linguistic and Cultural Insights

The prefix ma- enables adjectives to function as mini relative clauses, preserving Dholuo's preference for descriptive clarity.

Adjectives carry:

  • Descriptive weight (size, shape, color, morality)
  • Cultural importance (e.g., maber implies moral uprightness)
  • Semantic nuance, especially in verb-derived forms (motho ≠ matho)

In discourse, Dholuo avoids bare descriptors; adjectives must be anchored to nouns or pronouns via ma-.

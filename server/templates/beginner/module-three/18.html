3.8.5 Past Perfect Tense

Past Perfect Tense in Dholuo – "Bedo" Construction

1. Definition and Grammatical Function

The Past Perfect Tense in Dholuo expresses an action or a state that had occurred before another past event.

It denotes a state of having been or having done something, often used for backgrounding in narrative or contrastive temporal sequences.

This tense integrates both pastness and completion prior to another past event.

2. Morphological Structure

2.1 Composite Construction

The tense is formed through a compositional structure:

Nene + subject prefix + se (auxiliary) + bedo (participle)

Here:

  • "Nene": A past tense auxiliary indicating that the event took place in the past.
  • "-se-": A perfective auxiliary indicating completion (equivalent to "have").
  • "Bedo": The unchanging participial root meaning to be or to have been.
    • Not conjugated itself; remains constant across all persons.

3. Subject-Concord Conjugation Patterns

English Dholuo Form
I had been Nene asebedo
You had been Nene isebedo
He/She/It had been Nene osebedo
We had been Nene wasebedo
You (pl.) had been Nene usebedo
They had been Nene gisebedo

Note: The subject prefix (a-, i-, o-, wa-, u-, gi-) integrates with -se- to express the subject-specific perfective.

4. Semantic Characteristics

Indicates a state or activity that:

  • Started earlier and
  • Was still relevant or ongoing at a reference point in the past.

Often used to set the scene or describe contextual background before a main past event.

5. Syntactic Features

  • "Bedo" functions as a non-finite participle, following the auxiliary construction.
  • Negation, temporal adverbials, or dependent clauses (like ka = "when") may follow the construction.
  • Sentence structure: Subject + nene + subject-perfective + bedo + (optional clause)

6. Exemplary Sentences and Analysis

6.1 Individual Conjugated Examples

Nene asebedo ka ariti
→ I had been waiting for you
"ase" = I have, "bedo" = been; ka ariti = when you delayed.
Nene osebedo ka oringo
→ He had been running
Used to express an ongoing action prior to a referenced point in time.
Nene osebedo ka koth chue
→ It had been raining
Emphasizes environmental condition before another event.
Nene gisebedo ka gilimore
→ They had been visiting one another
Suggests a reciprocal, prolonged action concluded before another.
Achieng' nene osebedo ka lime
→ Achieng had been visiting her
Describes past background behavior of Achieng preceding another narrative moment.

7. Linguistic Observations

The participle "bedo":

  • Does not inflect or take agreement markers.
  • Is grammatically stable and attaches only to auxiliary constructions.

The auxiliary -se-:

  • Functions semantically similar to the English "have" in the present perfect.
  • Indicates a completed or endured state that remains relevant at a past moment.

The pre-verbal "nene":

  • Places the whole construction unequivocally in the past, removing any present relevance.

8. Pragmatic and Pedagogical Implications

8.1 Learner Caution Areas

Learners must not:

  • Conjugate bedo independently.
  • Confuse nene osebedo (he had been) with osebedo (he has been) — the presence of "nene" shifts tense.
  • Replace -se- with habitual -ga, which alters aspectual meaning.

8.2 Instructional Emphasis

Focus on the fixed pattern:

Nene + subject + se + bedo
  • Encourage oral practice with subordinate time clauses (e.g., ka + verb) to deepen temporal awareness.
  • Train learners to recognize this form in longer narrative structures for backgrounding.

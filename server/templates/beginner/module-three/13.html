3.6.2 Intransitive & Reflexive Verbs

1. Intransitive Verbs in Dholuo

Definition:

Intransitive verbs are verbs that do not take a direct object.

In Dholuo, these verbs stand independently and describe actions that are complete without affecting another noun.

Examples of Common Intransitive Verbs:

Dholuo Verb English Meaning
Tiyo to work
Wer to sing
Nindo to sleep
Miel to dance
Ringo to run
Bet to sit

Example Sentences:

Abet e kom – I sit on the chair.

Gitiyo matek – They work hard.

Note: The emphasis is on the subject performing the action without a direct object.

2. The Verb "To Have" in Dholuo

A. Possession using "gi" (with):

In Dholuo, the idea of "having" is expressed by combining a personal pronoun with the conjunctive form of "with" — gi.

The construction is compact: Personal Pronoun + gi = "To have"

Conjugation Table (Possessive with "gi"):

Pronoun Dholuo Form English
I an gi I have
You (sg.) in gi You have
He/She en gi He/She has
We wan gi We have
You (pl.) un gi You (pl.) have
They gin gi They have

Examples:

An gi kom – I have a chair.

Gin gi lowo – They have land.

Un gi ot – You (pl.) have a house.

En gi abila – He has a hut.

En gi dichuo – She has a husband.

Wan gi guok – We have a dog.

Important Note:
While "an kod guok" can mean "I have a dog," it more literally translates as "I and the dog," depending on context. Thus, gi is the correct possessive construction.

3. The Verb "To Have" as an Auxiliary Verb – Se

Usage in the Imperfect Past Tense:

The verb "se" expresses the idea of having done something or an action that has been completed in the past.

It is always used in compound verb constructions to indicate perfect aspect (have + past participle).

Conjugated Examples:

Dholuo Form English Translation
Asekadho penj I have passed the exam.
Asedhi I have gone.
Asechiemo I have eaten.
Osenindo He/she has slept.
Gisechiemo They have eaten.
Ase nindo I have slept.
Wase budho We have stayed.
Usedhi Kisumu You have gone to Kisumu.
Osedhi tedo She has gone to cook (She is married).
Osedhi katedo She is married (gone to marriage).
Osenyuome She is married.
Osetho He/She/It has died.

Note:
The auxiliary "se" is sensitive to the tense, especially the past perfect, denoting an action completed recently or relevant to the present.

4. Reflexive Verbs in Dholuo

A. Forming Reflexive Verbs:

Reflexiveness is expressed by adding the suffix -re to the infinitive verb.

This typically means the subject is doing the action to oneself or among themselves.

Infinitive vs Reflexive Comparison:

Verb Meaning Reflexive Meaning
Tedo to cook Tedore to cook itself
Neno to see Nenore to see oneself/each other
Puonjo to teach Puonjore to learn (teach oneself)
Kano to keep Kanore to be kept

B. Reflexive Verb Conjugation Table (Puonjore – to learn):

Subject Dholuo English
I Apuonjora I am learning (teach myself)
You (sg.) Ipuonjori You are learning
He/She Opuonjore He/She is learning
We Wapuonjore We are learning
You (pl.) Upuonjoru You are (pl.) learning
They Gipuonjore They are learning

C. Reflexive Verbs Used as Nouns – -ruok Form:

The suffix "-ruok" converts a reflexive verb into a noun form, representing the state or action itself.

Examples:

Reflexive Action Dholuo English Translation
Washing oneself Luokruok ber Washing oneself is good.
Not washing Ok odwar luokore He does not want to wash himself.
Fighting Goruok rach Fighting one another is bad.
Refusing to fight Ok gidwar gore They don't want to fight each other.

5. Special Notes and Clarifications:

  • Nyuomo (to marry): In Dholuo culture, only a man marries, thus in linguistic usage the subject of the verb nyuomo is traditionally male.
  • No Object Marker: Dholuo verbs do not use separate object pronouns—subject prefixes alone determine who is performing the action.
  • Conjunctions: Dholuo uses subject prefixes (an, in, en, etc.) rather than separate conjunctions; these carry most of the grammatical weight.
  • Important Morphological Point: Possessive constructions like "an gi kom" are written as single units in usage and should be read as such.

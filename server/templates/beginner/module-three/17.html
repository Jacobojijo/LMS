3.8.4 Past Tense

1. Simple Past Tense (Kinde Machon) – 'ne' / 'nene' Marker

1.1. Definition and Function

Expresses actions that were completed in the past.

Indicates an event that occurred and ended before the time of speaking.

The auxiliary particle "nene" (abbreviated as "ne") is prefixed to the subject-verb structure.

1.2. Structure

Subject + ne/nene + Verb + Object/Predicate

1.3. Examples

Olum nene odhi kwath – Olum went to graze cattle

Nene achamo rech – I ate fish

Nene ayudo tich – I found work

Mama nene onudo nyuka ni jotich – Mother bought soda for the workers

Japuonj nene mor ka nyithindo okalo penj – The teacher was happy when the children answered questions

Atieno nene otedo kuon – Atieno cooked ugali

Adoyo nene odhi neno waymare – Adoyo went to see her aunt

1.4. Word Order Sensitivity

Subject and object placement significantly affect meaning.

Example:

Nene oromo kode – He/she met him/her

Nene uromo kodwa – You met us

Nene giromo kodgi – They met them

2. Contracted Past Tense Forms

2.1. Verb Conjugation with "ne"

Common verbs combine "ne" with the subject prefix for compact expression.

English Dholuo Contracted Form
I wrote ne andiko nandiko
You wrote ne indiko nindiko
He/She wrote ne ondiko nondiko
We wrote ne wandiko newandiko
You (pl.) wrote ne undiko nundiko
They wrote ne gindiko negindiko

3. Immediate Past: 'a-' Prefixation

3.1. Definition

The prefix "a-" marks a just-completed action, often indicating recentness or immediacy.

3.2. Example

A apuro – I have just dug (but now stopped)

4. Yesterday Past Tense – 'nyo-' or 'nyoro-' Prefix

4.1. Function

Indicates an action that took place specifically yesterday.

4.2. Example

Nyoro natuo – I was sick yesterday

5. Extended Past Tense Markers

Marker Meaning Example Translation
nyocha Two days ago Nyocha atuo I was sick two days ago
yande Recently Yande atuo I was sick recently
a- Just now A tuo I was just sick (but recovered)

6. Recent Past Perfective – '-se-' Infix

6.1. Structure

Subject prefix + -se- + verb root

6.2. Function

Marks an action that has just been completed (equivalent to English present perfect).

6.3. Examples

English Dholuo
I have eaten asechiemo
You have eaten isechiemo
He/she has eaten osechiemo
We have eaten wasechiemo
You (pl.) have eaten usechiemo
They have eaten gisechiemo

7. Past Continuous / Habitual Tense (Kinde Manenedhi Nyime)

7.1. Definition

Describes ongoing or repeated actions in the past.

Combines "nene" with habitual suffix "-ga" to express repetition or continuity in the past.

7.2. Structure

Nene + Subject Prefix + Verb + -ga

7.3. Examples

Nene achiemoga – I was eating (habitually or during that time)

Nene asomoga Dholuo e ot – I used to study Dholuo at home

Ochieng' nene oheroga yuoro tum – Ochieng used to like attending dances

Nene awachoga Dholuo maber – I used to speak Dholuo well

Chon gilala nyiri nene nindoga e siwindhe – Long ago, girls used to sleep in the siwindhe (girls' hut)

Kwara nene oheroga nyuka – My grandfather used to like soda

8. Morphological Observations

  • Nene is an auxiliary that does not inflect but co-occurs with the verb for past meaning.
  • Subject-verb agreement still applies even when "ne/nene" is used.
  • Dholuo expresses past events via a combination of pre-verbal particles, inflectional prefixes, and suffixes (agglutinative morphology).
  • Tense interpretation is heavily reliant on prefixes, suffixes, and adverbial particles.

9. Pedagogical Implications for Learners

Learners must distinguish between:

  • Plain past (ne/nene + verb)
  • Recent or immediate past (a- prefix)
  • Yesterday/timed pasts (nyo-, nyocha, yande)
  • Habitual past (nene + verb + -ga)
  • Perfective past (-se- infix)

Emphasis on mastering word order and understanding tense based on particle usage and morphological integration.

Learners should practice interpreting subtle shifts in aspect using minimal morphological cues.
